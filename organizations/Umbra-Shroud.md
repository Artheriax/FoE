# The Umbra Shroud

*Motto: "Understand the Dark to Control It." (Official) / "No Shadow Too Deep." (Unofficial)*

The Shroud specializes in engagement categories that other guilds decline: Umbral and Unholy threats at elevated tiers, Keystone monsters with temporal or spatial affinities, and phenomena at the boundary between monster behavior and arcane incident. Its headquarters location is not registered with MOD; the exemption from disclosure requirements was negotiated at a level that neither MOD's public records nor the Shroud's accessible documents clearly account for.

---

## Overview

- **Specialization:** Umbral, Unholy, and Keystone threat categories; temporal and spatial anomaly containment.
- **MOD Relationship:** Operationally cooperative; structurally opaque. MOD deploys the Shroud when the threat category falls outside what Sentinels and major guilds are equipped to handle. The institutional arrangement's exact terms are not in MOD's public regulatory filings.
- **Recruitment:** Invite-only. The Shroud approaches individuals; the reverse does not appear to work. It seeks Gaze bearers with darkness, binding, or revelation affinities; Voidlings and Umbraxans; and individuals who have survived encounters with Unholy or Umbral entities without documented psychological compromise.
- **Public Perception:** Feared, distrusted, and regarded as indispensable. When a Dungeon begins exhibiting Unholy contamination spread or Keystone temporal instability, the Shroud is what MOD deploys when standard protocols have already failed. The population is consistently grateful for their outcomes and consistently uncomfortable with their presence.

## Notable History

**The Ashenveil Deep Breach (Third Era):** The Shroud's origin operation in documented record — the engagement result established both the guild's reputation and current protocols for Unholy contamination zone management. The Ashenveil Breach Aftermath — the contamination zone requiring ongoing isolation — is a Shroud-adjacent holding, though the precise terms of their involvement in its ongoing management are not in MOD's public operational filings.

**Cross-Reference:** `systems/Monsters.md` — Unholy Monsters; Dungeon System, The Ashenveil Deep Breach; `world/history/Third-Era.md` — Shadow Magic Case I

**The Ashenveil Shadow:** The Umbral Shadow investigation file held by MOD's Dungeon Division was partially sourced from materials the Shroud provided before MOD's formal engagement. The Shroud has not commented on what it knows about the Shadow's origin that it has not provided to MOD.

**Cross-Reference:** `systems/Monsters.md` — Umbral Monsters, The Ashenveil Shadow

**The Stormstep:** The Umbra Shroud claimed affiliation with the Stormstep within a week of the operative's death announcement, after seven years of unsuccessful MOD pursuit. The timing of that claim — and what it implies about when the Shroud knew — has not been addressed publicly.

## Institutional Notes

The Umbra Shroud's unregistered headquarters status is the most visible sign of an arrangement whose full terms are unknown. MOD granted the exemption; the Shroud operates within it. Whatever was exchanged for that accommodation has not appeared in any accessible record.

Dark Elves maintain a natural affiliation with the Shroud. Whether this is institutional policy or emergent preference, the outcome is the same: a significant portion of the Shroud's membership draws from a community that MOD registers at below-average rates and the Shroud recruits from directly.
