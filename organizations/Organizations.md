## The Magic Oversight Division (MOD)

*Motto: "Vigilance, Order, Balance."*

The Magic Oversight Division is the governing authority on all matters related to magic, Dungeon activity, power growth, and aberrant phenomena across Hyperion. While individual nations and city-states retain authority over civil governance, MOD's jurisdiction over hunters, essences, and magical regulation is absolute and recognized across all major territories. Its influence extends from the fortified complex of The Bastion to the smallest regional field outpost near a contested Dungeon zone. An estimated 340,000 individuals hold active MOD registration in some capacity, from E-rank hunters to contracted specialists operating under lifetime institutional agreements.

### History & Purpose

The MOD was established in the aftermath of the event recorded as the Great Breach — a catastrophic simultaneous rupture of multiple high-level Dungeons that overwhelmed competing national response forces, producing casualties across several regions before coordinated containment was achieved. The near-collapse of that response made clear that fragmented national authority over Dungeon activity was structurally insufficient. A treaty among the major powers created MOD as a neutral institutional authority with binding cross-national jurisdiction.

Its founding mandates remain unchanged:

- **Regulation:** Register, test, and rank all hunters; track powerful individuals and aberrant ability categories.
- **Containment:** Monitor Dungeon activity, predict and respond to breach events, maintain population safety perimeters.
- **Research:** Study magic, Essence properties, power growth mechanics, and threat phenomena to improve institutional response.
- **Balance:** Prevent any single entity, guild, or nation from accumulating power sufficient to destabilize the regional order.

### Organizational Structure

MOD's operational structure is divided into functional divisions, each with independent authority within its domain and shared reporting obligations to the central command structure at The Bastion.

#### The Bastion

MOD's headquarters is a fortified city-scale complex whose precise location and internal layout are classified at the institutional level. Regional offices and field outposts extend to thousands of active Dungeon zones across Hyperion. Communications between The Bastion and regional operations use a classified relay architecture developed in the Seventh Era by contracted Spatial Magic specialists; this system remains operational and its architecture remains sealed.

#### The Hunter Registry

The Registry maintains the active database of all registered hunters: rank, race, arcane affiliation, Amplitude assessment, Gaze and Sigil status, combat record, and breach response history. As of the current registration cycle, the Registry holds approximately 180 million active hunter files. An additional estimated 30–35 million individuals with hunter capabilities operate outside MOD oversight in contested or remote territories; pursuit of registration compliance in those regions is an ongoing Registry enforcement priority.

Registration requires a full assessment of physical and mental Source grades, arcane affinity, and any identified passive abilities. Failure to report a power breakthrough for re-assessment is a sanctionable offense under Registration Code, Section 3.

#### The Ranking System

The alphanumeric Rank (E–X) is both a competency certification and an operational license. Higher Ranks grant access to classified Dungeon zones, restricted contract types, and significant institutional privileges.

| Rank | Status | Notes |
|------|--------|-------|
| E | Entry-level | No prior engagement record required |
| D–C | Established | Regional contract access |
| B–A | Advanced | Multi-dungeon and breach authorization |
| S | Strategic asset | Continental recognition; standing breach response designation |
| X | Exceptional | Direct Eternal Guard coordination on sealed-classification engagements |

An A-rank hunter holds regional strategic significance. S-rank hunters are tracked individually by The Bastion independent of guild affiliation. X-rank hunters are a standing population of fewer than fifty confirmed individuals at any given time; their assignments are coordinated at a level above standard MOD chain of command.

#### Field Operations Division

The operational arm of MOD enforcement. The Sentinels are career hunters who have accepted institutional employment within MOD's command structure, foregoing independent contracting in exchange for institutional protection, steady compensation, and access to classified equipment caches. Sentinel teams handle major Breach responses, apprehension of rogue hunters, and suppression of unregistered operation in contested territories. They are not the primary engagement force for Keystone-class threats; those engagements require X-rank authorization and coordination through The Bastion rather than regional Field Operations.

Field Operations maintains standing records of all active breach perimeters and ongoing containment operations. The division is the primary interface between independent hunters and MOD during major events.

#### Internal Review

The auditing and investigative division. Internal Review's mandate covers compliance verification, conduct investigations on registered hunters and MOD employees, legal proceedings arising from regulatory violations, and procedural review of historical casework when new information is introduced.

Internal Review operates in numbered cycles; each cycle corresponds to a rotating senior caseload period. Maelen Voss held a senior analyst position in Internal Review's 22nd Cycle, producing an unmatched case-resolution record across thirty years before her Senses Sigil status was disclosed following her retirement. The procedural review of her prior casework — not disputing factual outcomes, but auditing identification methodology — has not yet concluded. Internal Review's legal division cites the Voss case as standing precedent on the question of retroactive procedural validity, without a consensus resolution. *→ `systems/Sigils.md` — Sigil of Senses; `world/History.md` — The Voss Suppression Case*

#### Dungeon Division

The monitoring and classification authority for all active Dungeon sites. The Dungeon Division maintains the official Class I–V Dungeon Registry, conducts structural surveys, and issues breach risk assessments. It holds sealed incident archives on Dungeon events whose causes or outcomes have not been publicly resolved.

Notable sealed holdings include the investigation file for the Ashenveil Shadow — an Umbral Elemental monster assessed as potentially originating outside any registered Dungeon source. The Division's notation on that file: *[Origin status — Unresolved. Archive sealed pending further evidence. No additional evidence received.]*

The Ironfield Surge (Third Era) is the foundational case in Dungeon Division training for survey blindspot failure. It contributed to the protocol shift from quarterly manual Dungeon assessment to continuous passive monitoring for all Class II and above sites. *→ `systems/Monsters.md` — Dungeon System; `world/History.md` — The Ironfield Surge*

#### Arcane Registry

The licensing and oversight body for all arcane practitioners. The Arcane Registry holds files on approximately 45 million registered practitioners as of the current registration period, covering amplitude rating (Novice through Archmage), spell school affiliation, restricted school status, and research history.

Practitioners are required to register prior to public practice. Arcane activity that falls within or adjacent to any forbidden school triggers mandatory Registry notification and review. Registry authority extends to the issuance of research licenses for restricted study areas and the revocation of practice credentials for violations of Arcane Code.

The Registry does not hold independent authority over forbidden magic prosecutions; those actions pass to Field Operations for containment and to the Eternal Guard for final disposition in extinction-level cases. *→ `systems/Arcane.md` — MOD Arcane Registry Overview*

#### Gaze and Sigil Assessment Unit

The specialist branch responsible for identifying, evaluating, and tracking Gaze and Sigil bearers. The Assessment Unit conducts formal evaluations of suspected bearers, maintains bearer files, monitors for unauthorized use of high-tier abilities, and enforces disclosure requirements.

Gaze bearers are required to register their ability category and tier upon identification. Sigil bearers occupy a more complex regulatory position — because Sigils are fully passive and cannot be deactivated, enforcement of disclosure is weighted against the bearer's privacy interests. This balance is revisited periodically and is currently the subject of active Internal Review policy debate.

The Assessment Unit's sealed population files for Absolute and Ethereal tier holders are maintained at a classification level requiring senior Eternal Guard authorization to access. Active file count: [Sealed]. *→ `systems/Gazes.md` — MOD Registry Overview; `systems/Sigils.md` — MOD Classification*

#### Theoretical Research Division

MOD's internal research arm, focusing on phenomena that fall outside current explanatory frameworks. The Theoretical Division produces internal briefings not made available to the general Registry; its findings are classified by default and declassified selectively.

The Division's position on the Wall of Nihility is documented: no meaningful research interaction has been achieved, and requests for authorized proximity study have been denied. Staff who have approached research in this area have been removed from active assignment on an indefinite basis. The Division's current policy recommendation is zero engagement, zero research authorization, and zero documentation of interaction attempts.

The Theoretical Division has a partial overlap with the Eternal Guard's research mandate in the domain of pre-record phenomena and sealed entity studies. The operational boundary between them is not publicly defined. *→ `systems/Arcane.md` — Forbidden Magic; `systems/Power-Sources.md` — Entity Zero*

### Regulatory Framework

MOD regulatory authority operates on a tiered system of disclosed obligations and sealed prohibitions.

**Disclosed Obligations** (publicly registered):

- Hunter rank assessment and renewal on power breakthrough
- Breach notification and reporting for all encounters above D-rank solo thresholds
- Essence taxation on Core and Keystone Essence transactions through registered channels
- Arcane practitioner registration prior to public practice
- Gaze and Sigil disclosure upon MOD identification

**Sanctionable Offenses** (Field Operations Code):

- Misclassification of a monster threat tier (Section 14)
- Failure to report a power breakthrough for re-assessment (Registration Code, Section 3)
- Unauthorized arcane practice above Novice amplitude without registration
- Operation in sealed or quarantined zones without Eternal Guard authorization
- Black market Essence transactions (23 active investigation networks as of current cycle)

**Sealed Prohibitions** (Eternal Guard authority):

- Any research into or attempted use of forbidden magic schools
- Unauthorized access to sealed classification records
- Expeditions into Eternal Guard-designated permanent quarantine zones

### Institutional Precedents

Several MOD legal and procedural rulings are actively cited in ongoing policy debates without consensus resolution:

- **The Voss Suppression Case** — Whether a Sigil bearer's undisclosed passive ability retroactively compromises the procedural validity of their professional work. Internal Review cites it in both directions. No ruling. *→ `world/History.md` — The Voss Suppression Case*

- **The Compact Defiant Adjudication** — Whether an Absolute Soul Anchoring Sigil constitutes grounds for infernal contract exemption under MOD contract law. Panel ruling: affirmative. Precedent has not been challenged since. *→ `systems/Sigils.md` — Soul Anchoring Sigil; `world/History.md` — The Compact Defiant Adjudication*

- **The Callax Protocols** — Origin case for MOD classification of Soul Forging as an extinction-level forbidden practice, with practitioners subject to execution on identification rather than trial. Eternal Guard authority invoked at case conclusion. *→ `systems/Arcane.md` — Forbidden Magic: Soul Forging; `world/History.md` — The Callax Abomination*

- **The Valcrest Engagement Standards** — Origin case for mandatory subjective-duration disclosure in Keystone engagement after-action reports. Three hunters classified [Psychic Displacement — Sealed]; cited in training materials on acceptable engagement trade-offs without detail. *→ `systems/Monsters.md` — Keystone Monsters: Dream/Nightmare; `world/History.md` — The Dreaming of Valcrest*

### Cultural Impact

To the public, MOD occupies the position of a global institutional necessity — present enough to be constant, distant enough to be abstract. Its enforcement record is respected. Its sealed archives are assumed to contain more than it discloses. The bureaucratic tone of MOD communications is consistent enough that deviations from it are treated as significant.

To hunters, MOD is the structure they operate within whether they acknowledge it or not. Guild-affiliated hunters interact with MOD primarily through contract channels and compliance requirements. Independent hunters carry a more adversarial relationship: they value the contract intelligence and breach-response infrastructure while resenting the regulatory weight, the Essence taxation, and the consistent impression that the Registry monitors them more carefully than it acknowledges.

## The Eternal Guard

The Eternal Guard is not a guild, a military division, or a political body in any form that MOD or national governments would formally categorize. Its relationship to MOD is one of superseding authority on a narrow but absolute set of matters: the classification and disposition of extinction-level threats, the governance of sealed phenomena, and the management of information whose public release would produce outcomes the Guard has determined to be worse than its continued suppression. MOD enforces. The Eternal Guard decides what MOD is not permitted to investigate.

### Structure and Authority

The Eternal Guard's internal structure is not publicly documented. Its membership count is not known to MOD's general Registry. What is known is that it operates above MOD chain of command in its designated domains, that its classification authority cannot be overridden by any national power or guild regardless of rank, and that the individuals who carry its warrants operate under no recorded institutional constraints short of the Higher Ones.

X-rank hunters are the threshold population for Eternal Guard direct engagement. Assignments coordinated through Eternal Guard channels do not appear in standard MOD after-action records.

### Jurisdictional Record

The Eternal Guard's documented interventions accessible through adjacent records:

- **The Callax Case (Fourth Era):** Invoked Eternal Guard authority at case conclusion. Practitioners executed; all research materials destroyed across institutional records. Issued the standing classification of Soul Forging as extinction-level forbidden, with practitioners subject to execution on identification without trial. This classification has not been revisited. *→ `systems/Arcane.md` — Forbidden Magic: Soul Forging; `world/History.md` — The Callax Abomination*

- **The Sixth Era Collapse:** Executed both practitioners upon identification. Maintained permanent quarantine of the 4,000 km² temporal fracture zone. Standing order: no expeditions authorized under any circumstances. No documented challenge to the order has been filed or accepted. *→ `systems/Arcane.md` — Forbidden Magic: Reality Fabrication; `world/History.md` — The Sixth Era Collapse*

- **Sealed Research Authority:** All research into Entity Zero, the Wall of Nihility, and Nil Space Magic is sealed at Eternal Guard authority. Theoretical Division staff who have approached proximity research on these areas have been removed from active assignment. *→ `systems/Power-Sources.md` — Entity Zero; `systems/Arcane.md` — Forbidden Magic*

- **Absolute and Ethereal Tier Files:** Gaze and Sigil bearer files at Absolute tier and above require senior Eternal Guard authorization to access within the Assessment Unit. Active file count: [Sealed].

- **The Sleeper Incident (Seventh Era, ongoing):** Engagement in progress. X-rank only. All documentation sealed at Eternal Guard authority. The operation has not concluded. *→ `systems/Gazes.md` — Ethereal Gaze of Mindscape; `systems/Monsters.md` — Keystone Monsters: Mind*

### Institutional Character

The Eternal Guard does not publish statements, hold press positions, or respond to institutional inquiries. When it communicates, it communicates through rulings, seals, and the termination of access. The operational logic of its documented interventions — insofar as that logic is legible — is consistently consequentialist: the action that produces the least catastrophic available outcome, regardless of procedural preference or individual cost. This is not a philosophy the Eternal Guard has articulated. It is a pattern that can be observed across the record of its interventions.

## House Dravenmore

House Dravenmore is a noble research house with institutional standing extending across several eras of documented history. It occupies an unusual position: neither a guild nor a MOD division, but its research output, genealogical records, and practitioner lineage have been absorbed into institutional frameworks often enough that the boundary between the House's private scholarship and MOD's official record is genuinely unclear in places.

### Research Mandate

House Dravenmore's primary research focus is mortal and near-mortal potential: what the body, mind, and soul can sustain; how Sigil and Gaze bearers manifest historically; and what lineage patterns in extraordinary individuals suggest about inheritance and awakening. Their genealogical registry of confirmed Sigil bearers is the most complete private record in existence. MOD's own Assessment Unit considers it more accurate than its own files for pre-Fourth Era identification cases.

The House does not publish its registry. It makes selective contributions to MOD records when those contributions serve purposes the House considers aligned with its interests. The terms of the ongoing exchange between House Dravenmore and MOD's Assessment Unit are not in MOD's public regulatory filings.

### Notable Institutional Contributions

- **Healer Cassian:** Following the Ashenmoor Campaigns, Cassian spent twelve years as a formal advisor to House Dravenmore, systematizing field healing protocols from personal methodology. The protocols he codified during this period remain the foundation of MOD emergency response medical procedures. The House's archives hold the original versions. *→ `world/History.md` — Healer Cassian of the Ashenmoor*

- **Orvielle Caste:** A House Dravenmore scholar reviewing Ashenmoor Campaigns records connected Caste's field journal entries — thirty-seven observations across fifteen years of noting that her wounds closed faster than expected — to a posthumous Vitality Sigil identification. The House added her lineage to its registry within a week of the identification. Her case has since been used in MOD training materials as the canonical example of delayed Sigil identification when the bearer's conventional skills are sufficient to explain observed outcomes. *→ `systems/Sigils.md` — Sigil of Vitality; `world/History.md` — The Ashenmoor Campaigns*

- **The Dual Sigil Lineage:** Following the Third Era dual-Sigil incident involving a Morvayne vassal, House Dravenmore has maintained active genealogical tracking of that bearer's lineage across subsequent generations. MOD's notation on the sealed case file: *review suspended, subject deceased, lineage tracked.* "Lineage tracked" is attributable to House Dravenmore. The House has not published its findings. *→ `systems/Sigils.md` — Dual Sigils; `world/History.md` — The Dual Sigil Incident*

- **Archon Dravenmore:** The House's most cited historical figure outside Sigil research domains. Archon Dravenmore was the preeminent Spatial Magic practitioner of the Seventh Era, responsible for the Hexline System — a framework for architectural spatial magic that became the basis for MOD's Bastion communications relay and remains in active institutional use. Dravenmore held a lifetime contract with MOD, the only such contract on record. *→ `systems/Arcane.md` — Keystones: Spatial Magic; `world/History.md` — Archon Dravenmore: Hexline System Architecture*

### Current Status

House Dravenmore's present research direction is not publicly disclosed. Its genealogical work continues; the House receives notification when MOD completes Sigil bearer identifications, by arrangement that is not reflected in MOD's public regulatory code.

## Guilds

Guilds are the primary operational vehicle for hunters pursuing active careers outside independent contracting. They are licensed and regulated by MOD; the terms of that licensing include financial reporting requirements, breach notification obligations, and minimum competency standards for internally deployed teams. MOD's relationship with guilds is functionally cooperative and structurally supervisory — the major guilds provide operational capacity that MOD could not replicate through Sentinels alone, while MOD provides contract infrastructure, Dungeon access, and institutional legitimacy that independent operation cannot match.

Strike teams that operate outside MOD regulation occupy a persistent gray zone: not proscribed by law, but treated by Field Operations as unpredictable variables. Major guilds maintain formal MOD compliance as a condition of their operating licenses. Several minor guilds do not.

### Major Guilds

#### Aethelgard Consortium

*Motto: "Order. Efficiency. Evolution."*

The largest and most operationally influential guild in current record. Aethelgard functions as a multi-national institutional structure — salaried employment, clear internal rank progression, standardized operational protocols, and benefits packages that make it the dominant recruitment destination for graduates of MOD-accredited hunter academies.

- **Specialization:** General contract operations across all monster tiers. The Consortium does not specialize by elemental affinity but by operational scale; it is the primary guild for large-footprint, multi-team operations on Class IV and Class V Dungeon clearances.
- **MOD Relationship:** Full compliance. Aethelgard maintains the most complete MOD compliance record of any major guild and is the first recipient of MOD's large-contract delegations. Field Operations coordinates directly with Aethelgard on S-rank and above Breach responses when Sentinel capacity is insufficient.
- **Recruitment:** Top graduates from MOD-accredited academies; selective headhunting of successful independents. High Amplitude ratings and disciplined, team-oriented assessment profiles are favored over high-outlier individuals.
- **Public Perception:** Professional, reliable, and faintly institutional. The safe choice for a hunter career. Critics describe them as too structured to take necessary risks; the operational data does not obviously support this characterization, but the reputation persists.

#### Solaris Vanguard

*Motto: "From the Dawn, We Stand."*

The oldest surviving guild in continuous operation, founded in the aftermath of the Cataclysm of Yggdra. The founding generation described its purpose as ensuring that the catastrophe that shattered the old world would not recur through insufficient preparation. That founding intent has shaped the guild's culture and operational emphasis across all subsequent eras.

- **Specialization:** Radiant, Holy, and Frost-affinity monsters; defensive and purification magic; containment operations on Unholy breach events. The Irenmere Sentinels — two Holy-tier monsters observed defending the Pass alongside Valdren Ashcrast during the Seventh Era siege — were approached by a Vanguard research team following the battle. Their behavior during the siege was assessed as benign; the engagement was concluded without neutralization. This remains an anomaly in the Vanguard's operational record and an ongoing subject of internal study. *→ `systems/Monsters.md` — Holy Monsters, The Irenmere Sentinels; `world/History.md` — Siege of Irenmere Pass*
- **MOD Relationship:** Full compliance with a traditionalist posture — the Vanguard fulfills regulatory requirements but maintains internal standards that exceed MOD minimums. It does not need to request MOD contract delegation; it receives it because its operational record on Radiant and Holy threats is unmatched across guild history.
- **Recruitment:** Heavily favors Elves, Astrae, and practitioners with Radiant or Holy affinities. Membership is treated internally as a long-term commitment rather than a career stage.
- **Public Perception:** Revered and occasionally regarded as archaic. The Vanguard is the institutional memory of what Hyperion looked like before the current order. Some find that comforting. Others find it inflexible.

#### The Umbra Shroud

*Motto: "Understand the Dark to Control It." (Official) / "No Shadow Too Deep." (Unofficial)*

The Shroud specializes in engagement categories that other guilds decline: Umbral and Unholy threats at elevated tiers, Keystone monsters with temporal or spatial affinities, and phenomena at the boundary between monster behavior and arcane incident. Its headquarters location is not registered with MOD; the exemption from disclosure requirements was negotiated at a level that neither MOD's public records nor the Shroud's accessible documents clearly account for.

- **Specialization:** Umbral, Unholy, and Keystone threat categories; temporal and spatial anomaly containment. The Ashenveil Deep Breach (Third Era, Class IV) is the Shroud's origin operation in documented record — the engagement result established both the guild's reputation and current protocols for Unholy contamination zone management. The Ashenveil Breach Aftermath — the contamination zone requiring ongoing isolation — is a Shroud-adjacent holding, though the precise terms of their involvement in its ongoing management are not in MOD's public operational filings. *→ `systems/Monsters.md` — Unholy Monsters; Dungeon System, The Ashenveil Deep Breach; `world/History.md` — Shadow Magic Case I*
- **MOD Relationship:** Operationally cooperative; structurally opaque. MOD deploys the Shroud when the threat category falls outside what Sentinels and major guilds are equipped to handle. The institutional arrangement's exact terms are not in MOD's public regulatory filings. The Umbral Shadow investigation file held by the Dungeon Division was partially sourced from materials the Shroud provided before MOD's formal engagement. *→ `systems/Monsters.md` — Umbral Monsters, The Ashenveil Shadow*
- **Recruitment:** Invite-only. The Shroud approaches individuals; the reverse does not appear to work. It seeks Gaze bearers with darkness, binding, or revelation affinities; Voidlings and Umbraxans; and individuals who have survived encounters with Unholy or Umbral entities without documented psychological compromise.
- **Public Perception:** Feared, distrusted, and regarded as indispensable. When a Dungeon begins exhibiting Unholy contamination spread or Keystone temporal instability, the Shroud is what MOD deploys when standard protocols have already failed. The population is consistently grateful for their outcomes and consistently uncomfortable with their presence.

### Minor Guilds

#### The Geode Crawlers

- **Specialization:** Terra and Keystone Monsters in subterranean Dungeon environments. Primary supplier of rare minerals and earth-attuned Core Essences through registered channels.
- **Culture:** Practical, methodical, and uninterested in prestige. Their MOD mineral taxation compliance record is complete. They are the guild that shows up, does the work, and submits the paperwork correctly.

#### Stormseekers

- **Specialization:** Shock and Blaze Monsters in high-energy volatile environments — volcanic rift Dungeons, storm-cell formations. *→ `systems/Monsters.md` — Shock Elemental; Blaze Elemental*
- **Culture:** Reckless, competitive, and thrill-seeking. The Stormcalm Trials (Seventh Era), which produced the first documented Kinesis Gaze evolution to Omnikinesis, included a Stormseeker engagement team. The connection between that engagement and the evolution event is noted in the MOD incident report without comment. *→ `world/History.md` — The Stormcalm Trials*

#### The Crimson Gauntlet

- **Specialization:** Dire and Lesser Monster mass clearance. Handles MOD contracts for settlement-perimeter sweeps and infrastructure corridor maintenance.
- **Culture:** Operational and unglamorous. High volume, low profile. Their casualty rates exceed any major guild's in absolute terms; their contract completion rates are comparable.

## Non-Registered Organizations

### Keepers of Nil

One of Hyperion's oldest active organizations. Not registered with MOD. Not a guild, not a research body in any institutional sense. Founded in the Second Era around a question that every institution eventually stopped asking: *why does the Wall of Nihility exist?*

Membership is restricted to immortals. Current active membership is fewer than thirty. The organization maintains a research archive on the Wall and its theoretical contents that exceeds any institutional equivalent by a significant margin, in part because the zero-engagement policy means the institutional equivalent is effectively empty.

They are not a threat organization. They are not a political body. They are not well-known. They are, as of the Modern Era, the organization closest to knowing what the Wall was built to hold — and the first to be present when it no longer holds it.
