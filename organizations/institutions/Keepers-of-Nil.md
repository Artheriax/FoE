# Keepers of Nil

## Overview

The Keepers of Nil are one of Hyperion's oldest active organizations, predating MOD, the current guild system, and most of the institutional structures that govern the modern world. They are not hunters. They are not researchers in any registered sense. They are not a political body. They are a persistent question given organizational form:

*Why is there a Wall?*

The Wall of Nihility was first formally documented by mortal-adjacent observers in the Pre-Record era. Every institution that subsequently encountered it reached the same conclusion: the Wall is beyond engagement, study carries unacceptable risk, and the correct response is to note its existence and maintain official distance. MOD's Theoretical Research Division arrived at the same position centuries later and formalized it as policy.

The Keepers of Nil are the organization that refused to stop asking.

Their founding premise was never aggressive. It was this: a structure without a visible architect implies intent. Intent implies purpose. Purpose implies something worth understanding. Across millennia, that premise has produced a body of research that MOD's zero-engagement policy has never generated and likely never will.

Their goal is not the Wall specifically. It is Entity Zero — the theoretical substrate-being from which the Higher Ones' Cosmic Sources are believed to have separated, a consciousness so fundamental to the architecture of reality that no one has ever confirmed its existence. The Keepers believe Entity Zero is real, is conscious, and is imprisoned behind the Wall of Nihility. Their centuries of research have all been aimed at a single act: breaching the Wall, contacting Entity Zero, and freeing her. They have had a very long time to become precise about what that means.

---

## Name

"Nil" references the Wall of Nihility directly — the Keepers' founding subject and the center of their research. The name is not widely known because the organization does not advertise itself. Among those who have encountered them, it is generally shortened to "the Keepers." Among themselves, it is never shortened.

---

## Founding Principles

The Keepers operate under three principles that have held since the Second Era founding:

- **The question precedes the answer.** No theory is too disruptive to consider. No institutional prohibition changes what is true.
- **The Wall holds something.** Walls are built for reasons. Absence of explanation is not the same as absence of cause.
- **Hyperion deserves the truth of its own history.** Whatever the Wall's purpose, the world built on a foundation of incomplete information cannot be fully understood by the people living in it.

These principles have never been formally written down. They are understood. Every member could recite them in different words and mean the same thing.

---

## Membership

Membership is by invitation only, extended after long observation. The criteria are not formally stated, but the pattern across documented admissions is consistent: the candidate is immortal, is already asking the right questions independently, and has demonstrated that their interest in a better Hyperion is not abstract principle but active investment.

The Keepers admit members from any race without restriction — including those that other institutions exclude, prohibit, or monitor with suspicion. A devil and an angel have held continuous membership since the Third Era. This is not idealism. It is the practical recognition that the truth of the Wall belongs to no single community, and that the individuals most useful to the question are often the ones most excluded from asking it through official channels.

Current active membership is small: fewer than thirty individuals across Hyperion. The organization does not expand for its own sake. Dead weight is a liability for a group operating at the margin of institutional tolerance.

## Notable Members

### Vael

*Race: Ascendant | Role: Founder, Current Lead*

Vael predates every institution currently governing Hyperion. They were a scholar in an era when magic was new enough that its rules were not yet bureaucratized, and they were among the first to formally encounter the Wall and ask what it was for. Every institutional answer they received over the following millennia amounted to a variation of "stop asking." The Keepers were the alternative to stopping.

Vael leads through patience rather than authority. They do not issue orders. They ask questions and allow the implications to resolve. Their value to the organization is not power but continuity — they hold the full record of what the Keepers have learned across its entire existence, and they are old enough that even the Eternal Guard's files on them are demonstrably incomplete.

Small detail that those who work with Vael eventually notice: they still speak in the register of someone who genuinely expects to be surprised by new information. After millennia, the curiosity has not become performance.

### Seraphiel Oswin

*Race: Angel | Role: Operational Oversight*

Oswin diverged from the Solaris Vanguard several centuries ago after a long period of watching the official institutional response to the Wall — originally catalogued as "active investigation priority" — quietly reclassify into "zero-engagement policy" without public explanation. Angels work through divine jurisdiction and institutional compliance; Oswin's departure from that structure was not dramatic, because nothing Oswin does is dramatic. They decided something. They have not revisited it.

Their presence in the Keepers is not technically prohibited under any regulation, because no regulation anticipated an angel doing it. The Solaris Vanguard is aware of their current affiliation. The Vanguard has made no public statement. In internal communications, the subject is described as "Oswin's ongoing leave assignment."

Operationally, Oswin manages the Keepers' relationship with entities and institutions that respond to authority framing. When the organization needs something from a body that operates through formal channels, Oswin is the representative. Their history with the Vanguard grants them access that the Keepers' informal status would not independently produce.

### Carix

*Race: Devil | Role: Intelligence and Contract Law*

Old, careful, and possessed of the kind of accumulated correct decisions that make other devils nervous. Carix joined the Keepers in the Fourth Era and has never given a complete explanation to any other member. The closest he has come is a single recorded statement, offered once without elaboration:

*"The Wall has held something since before any of these institutions existed. What was put inside it was put there by beings with no accountability to anyone. I have opinions about unaccountable power."*

No one has pressed him further, partly out of professional regard and partly because the answer would not change the fact of his continued membership.

His operational value is the intersection of infernal contract law and intelligence methodology. He was the one who identified that the scroll *The Soul of the First* had most likely passed into specific vampire house holdings, tracked its probable transfer chain, and planned the retrieval operation that ultimately failed to recover it before Gaelira walked out with it. His annotated post-operation assessment — which identified Gaelira by name and documented the second layer's activation — is the intelligence document that eventually led the Keepers to Sersey's group.

**Cross-Reference:** `world/history/Modern-Era.md` — The Scroll Retrieval Attempt; `stories/Story-Arcs.md` — FoE: Politics of Blood

### Thresh

*Race: Demon | Role: Unspecified*

The second-oldest member after Vael. Their presence predates the Keepers' formal organizational structure — they were involved before the question had a name, and when the Second Era founding formalized the organization, Thresh was simply already there. What Thresh wants from the Keepers' eventual answers is not clearly stated and has never been asked directly by any member. They attend, they contribute in ways that prove consistently useful, and the record of their involvement across millennia is extensive without being explanatory.

MOD holds no file on Thresh. Demons are prohibited from registration, and Thresh has never done anything that generated a formal incident report under MOD review protocols. Whether this represents restraint or precision is, like most things about Thresh, not publicly answerable.

### House Velmira *(Representative Seat)*

*Race: Vampire | Role: Intelligence and Institutional Access*

House Velmira quietly diverted a portion of its intelligence network toward Keeper operations several centuries ago, negotiating a permanent representative seat that passes to each successive house head's designated successor. The seat has never been vacant. The terms of the arrangement — what the house receives in exchange for this long-term commitment — are not documented in any accessible record. House Velmira does not discuss it.

The operative deployed to recover *The Soul of the First* from the vampire house in the Politics of Blood incident was acting under House Velmira's authorization. Their failure — specifically the identification of Gaelira as an Astrae bearer who had unlocked the scroll's second layer and exited under her own terms — produced the most significant intelligence the Keepers had received in over a century. Carix's post-operation assessment was based on the House Velmira operative's filed report.

**Cross-Reference:** `world/history/Modern-Era.md` — The Scroll Retrieval Attempt; `stories/Story-Arcs.md` — FoE: Politics of Blood

### Sorin Dask

*Race: Elf (pre-formalization era, effectively immortal) | Role: Historical Lineage Research*

The Keepers' foremost researcher on the historical lineage of innate magical traits. Dask joined the organization after exhausting every institutional research channel for tracking how magical affinity propagates across generations: every thread ended at the Wall, and MOD's zero-engagement policy made that a terminal obstruction rather than a starting point. Within the Keepers, the Wall was the starting point.

Their joining predates several current members and postdates several who are no longer active. They are precise, patient, and occasionally difficult to interrupt when they are working through something.

Dask has spent decades building the theoretical framework for the original biological-arcane architecture — the mutation-magic fusion that produced the first inheritable magical traits. Their research traces lineages back to the original formula. The formula's creator was imprisoned in the Wall. Dask's life's work is understanding what was sealed with him.

## Research Holdings

The Keepers maintain the most comprehensive private archive on the Wall of Nihility in existence. Its contents include:

- Pre-Record and early Era documentation of the Wall's first observed and formally recorded appearances
- Theoretical analyses from each subsequent era, including material assembled before MOD's zero-engagement classification made institutional research impossible
- The complete research branch on *The Soul of the First*, its scroll lineage, and the theoretical implications of its text — both The First Account (the surface narrative of "the First") and The Breach Configuration (the Astrae-accessible divine seals)
- Sorin Dask's compiled theoretical history of innate magical lineages — documented cases, lineage chains, and the original bearer's theoretical profile
- Dimensional approach mapping for the Wall's location and the surrounding void-adjacent pathways, developed in coordination with sources familiar with Dreamveil and Void geography
- Intelligence files on MOD's Project Crimson: incomplete but functional, assembled primarily from Carix's operational tracking and House Velmira's institutional access

The archive is not held in a single location.

## Relationship with Other Institutions

**MOD:** The Keepers operate in the gap between MOD's jurisdictional reach and its zero-engagement policies. They are not registered, not monitored as an active threat, and not in violation of regulations that were written without anticipating their existence. MOD's Theoretical Research Division is aware of what the Keepers represent but has no formal position on record. The zero-engagement policy on Wall research is internally cited as sufficient.

**Project Crimson:** Competing institutional interest in *The Soul of the First* created the Keepers' most significant recent operational complication. Project Crimson is not aware of the Keepers as a distinct organization. Their appearance in Project Crimson operational reports is as "unknown third party" in the Politics of Blood incident — a designation that has not been upgraded despite Carix's post-operation report being more informative about Project Crimson than the reverse.

**Eternal Guard:** One confirmed interaction on record: the Wall breach operation. Using The Breach Configuration — the divine activation key unlocked by Gaelira from *The Soul of the First* — the Keepers performed a dimensional resonance ritual at the Wall's surface — the culmination of centuries of research. The breach succeeded. A figure emerged. It was not Entity Zero. It was Artem — the original creator of innate magical traits, imprisoned there by the Higher Ones approximately 3,500 years ago. The Keepers, having believed for centuries that the Wall held Entity Zero, had freed the wrong being. Post-emergence analysis confirmed that the error was shared: the scroll's author had also believed Entity Zero was behind the Wall and had written The First Account through that interpretive lens. The Keepers adopted the same mistaken reading of "the First" — interpreting it as "the First Being" (Entity Zero) rather than "the First [creator]" (Artem). The scroll's author, the Keepers, and every institution that had studied the document were wrong for the same reason, which no one discovered until Artem walked out and said so directly. The Guard intercepted the return party during the subsequent Nihility transit, conducted an assessment that returned non-functional results, and remanded Artem to their jurisdiction. The aftermath is ongoing. Vael made no comment on the encounter afterward beyond what was necessary to resume transit. Whether the Keepers have adjusted their Entity Zero thesis or are simply processing new data is not documented.

**Solaris Vanguard:** Oswin's presence in the Keepers and their origin from the Vanguard is known to Vanguard leadership. The Vanguard has taken no official position.
