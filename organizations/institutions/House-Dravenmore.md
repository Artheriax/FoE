# House Dravenmore

House Dravenmore is a noble research house with institutional standing extending across several eras of documented history. It occupies an unusual position: neither a guild nor a MOD division, but its research output, genealogical records, and practitioner lineage have been absorbed into institutional frameworks often enough that the boundary between the House's private scholarship and MOD's official record is genuinely unclear in places.

---

## Research Mandate

House Dravenmore's primary research focus is mortal and near-mortal potential: what the body, mind, and soul can sustain; how innate magical affinity manifests historically across lineages; and what lineage patterns in extraordinary individuals suggest about inheritance and awakening. Their genealogical registry of magically-gifted lineages is the most complete private record in existence. MOD's own Arcane Registry considers it more accurate than its own files for pre-Fourth Era identification cases.

The House does not publish its registry. It makes selective contributions to MOD records when those contributions serve purposes the House considers aligned with its interests. The terms of the ongoing exchange between House Dravenmore and MOD's Arcane Registry are not in MOD's public regulatory filings.

## Notable Institutional Contributions

- **Healer Cassian:** Following the Ashenmoor Campaigns, Cassian spent twelve years as a formal advisor to House Dravenmore, systematizing field healing protocols from personal methodology. The protocols he codified during this period remain the foundation of MOD emergency response medical procedures. The House's archives hold the original versions. *→ `world/history/Mid-Era.md` — Healer Cassian of the Ashenmoor*

- **Orvielle Caste:** A House Dravenmore scholar reviewing Ashenmoor Campaigns records connected Caste's field journal entries — thirty-seven observations across fifteen years of noting that her wounds closed faster than expected — to a posthumous identification of innate accelerated healing. The House added her lineage to its registry within a week of the identification. Her case has since been used in MOD training materials as the canonical example of delayed identification of innate abilities when the bearer's conventional skills are sufficient to explain observed outcomes. *→ `world/history/Mid-Era.md` — The Ashenmoor Campaigns*

- **The Dual Lineage Study:** Following the Third Era incident involving a Morvayne vassal who exhibited simultaneous expression of two normally distinct innate abilities, House Dravenmore has maintained active genealogical tracking of that bearer's lineage across subsequent generations. MOD's notation on the sealed case file: *review suspended, subject deceased, lineage tracked.* "Lineage tracked" is attributable to House Dravenmore. The House has not published its findings. *→ `world/history/Third-Era.md` — The Dual Trait Incident*

- **Archon Dravenmore:** The House's most cited historical figure. Archon Dravenmore was the preeminent Spatial Magic practitioner of the Seventh Era, responsible for the Hexline System — a framework for architectural spatial magic that became the basis for MOD's Bastion communications relay and remains in active institutional use. Dravenmore held a lifetime contract with MOD, the only such contract on record. The contract was structured as an institutional agreement binding the House, not the individual: it includes succession provisions that transfer maintenance obligations and contract standing to the House's designated heir upon the signer's death. MOD's legal division has reviewed the transfer clause twice and declined to challenge it both times. The contract has persisted across eras because the House has never failed to designate a successor. *→ `systems/arcane/Keystone.md` — Spatial Magic; `world/history/Seventh-Era.md` — Archon Dravenmore: Hexline System Architecture*

## Current Status

House Dravenmore's present research direction is not publicly disclosed. Its genealogical work continues; the House receives notification when MOD completes identification of significant innate magical lineages, by arrangement that is not reflected in MOD's public regulatory code.

The House's research on vampire lineages — specifically the interaction between immortality and the expression of innate magical traits across centuries — has not been published. The subjects of that research have not been formally notified that it is ongoing.
