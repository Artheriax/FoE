# Project Crimson

*MOD Internal Division — Classification: [Sealed — Director-Level Access Only]*

Project Crimson is a classified MOD operation overseeing vampire population control, blood supply regulation, and the containment of vampire territorial expansion across Hyperion. Its existence is not publicly acknowledged. Among MOD divisions, it is referenced in internal documentation only by operational designation — no formal division name, no public-facing office, no Registry-listed personnel.

---

## Mandate

Project Crimson's operational mandate covers four domains:

- **Blood Supply Regulation:** Vampires are sustained by blood consumption and derive no nutritional value from conventional food. Unregulated feeding creates conflict with mortal populations. Crimson maintains synthetic blood distribution networks, monitors illegal feeding incidents, and enforces compliance with the Feeding Accord — the treaty between MOD and the three vampire houses (Noctaris, Morvayne, Dravenmore) that establishes legal feeding parameters, victim compensation requirements, and population-density-based territorial limits.

- **Population Control:** Every vampire turning — the process of converting a mortal into a vampire — is required by the Feeding Accord to be registered with Crimson. Unregistered turnings are classified as unauthorized reproduction and carry sanction up to and including hunter-kill orders. The Accord specifically prohibits the turning of Astrae; the projected combat capability of an Astrae-vampire hybrid exceeds what any single house could reliably control, and Crimson's internal threat models classify such a hybrid as a Category II institutional stability risk.

- **Territorial Containment:** Vampire houses are legally restricted to designated territorial zones. Expansion of house-controlled territory requires Crimson authorization and is reviewed under criteria including local mortal population density, proximity to MOD infrastructure, and the house's compliance history. Unauthorized territorial expansion triggers intervention protocols.

- **Intelligence & Monitoring:** Crimson maintains classified surveillance of all three vampire houses, including embedded operatives in household staff, financial monitoring of house-controlled enterprises, and intelligence-sharing agreements with House Velmira (a fourth, smaller house that operates primarily as an information broker rather than a territorial power). The Dravenmore genealogical registry — the most complete private record of magically-gifted lineages in existence — is partially funded through Crimson intelligence channels, though Dravenmore does not publicly acknowledge this arrangement.

## Relationship to The Soul of the First

Project Crimson's interest in the scroll *The Soul of the First* is distinct from — and predates — the Keepers of Nil's interest in its Wall-breaching function. Crimson's archival research, conducted in parallel with Dravenmore genealogical studies, identified textual analysis suggesting that the scroll's surface narrative — **The First Account** — may contain the earliest documented reference to vampirism's biological origin.

The theory, held within Crimson's sealed research division but not publicly disclosed, is that vampirism is a stable mutation of the same biological-arcane architecture that underlies innate magical affinity — a self-sustaining variant of the bridge between biology and magic that became population-scale through transmission rather than inheritance. If confirmed, this would mean vampires are not a separate species but an expression of the same fundamental architecture that produces magical ability in other races. Crimson's interest is in the control implications: if vampirism operates on the same biological-arcane bridge, then it is theoretically susceptible to the same class of intervention. A method that disrupts innate magical function could, with modification, disrupt vampire physiology at its foundation.

This research is classified above MOD Director level. The Keepers of Nil are not aware of it. The vampire houses suspect it but cannot confirm it.

The scroll's additional value — the divine seals containing **The Breach Configuration** (a Wall-breaching key accessible only through Astrae perception) — was not Crimson's primary operational objective. It became relevant only after Gaelira unlocked the second layer and the Keepers of Nil mobilized to acquire the scroll. At that point, Crimson's operational calculus shifted from archival interest to active retrieval: the scroll was now the most significant multi-institutional intelligence asset in Hyperion, and Crimson could not permit it to fall into unregistered hands.

## The Political Context

To the mortal population of Hyperion, vampires occupy an ambiguous position. Elves are accepted as dignified long-lived neighbors; their immortality, tied to the Trees of Life, is viewed as a natural feature of a respected race. Vampires are not afforded this perception. They are immortal through predation — their continued existence requires the consumption of mortal blood. Humans, who constitute the largest population and the primary feeding base, regard vampires with a mixture of fear, resentment, and institutional resignation. The Feeding Accord keeps the peace, but the peace is conditional: every new vampire turned is one more immortal being who will need to feed for centuries, and every feeding incident that exceeds the Accord's limits erodes public tolerance further.

Elven blood is considered a delicacy by vampire standards — richer, sustaining for longer periods between feedings. The vampire houses maintain strict internal prohibitions against feeding on Elves from established Tree communities, not out of principle but out of operational pragmatism: the Elven institutional response to a documented feeding violation would jeopardize the Accord. Astrae blood — rarer, more magically concentrated — is described in vampire cultural records as a once-in-a-lifetime experience. Turning an Astrae, however, is the single most prohibited act in vampire law: the resulting hybrid would be too powerful for any house to control, would trigger immediate MOD intervention, and would almost certainly result in the dissolution of the Feeding Accord and the reclassification of all vampires as hostile entities.

This is the environment Project Crimson manages. Its purpose is not vampire eradication — the Accord, however tense, is cheaper than war. Its purpose is ensuring that the Accord's terms are enforced, that the vampire population does not expand beyond what the Accord can contain, and that no house develops the means to operate outside Crimson's surveillance. The scroll, in this context, represents both threat and opportunity: proof of vampire origins could destabilize the Accord; a method of controlling vampire physiology could render Crimson's enforcement capacity permanent.

## Operational History

- **The Politics of Blood Incident:** Crimson deployed an embedded operative to the vampire household where Gaelira was placed through arranged marriage. The operative's mission was retrieval of *The Soul of the First*; the mission failed when Gaelira independently accessed the scroll, unlocked the divine seals, and exited the household under her own terms. The operative filed a report identifying Gaelira, confirming the second layer's activation, and noting Crimson's exposure. This report was never escalated above Crimson's internal chain — surfacing the failure would have required disclosing the extent of Crimson's operational presence in a noble vampire household, which Crimson's directorate was not prepared to do. The Keepers of Nil, through House Velmira's parallel intelligence network, obtained a more complete picture of the incident than MOD's own classified division possessed internally.

- **Current Status:** Gaelira's possession of the scroll remains an active Crimson tracking priority. The scroll is now known to have been used in the Modern Era Wall breach operation (see Keepers of Nil), and the being who emerged — Artem — is under Eternal Guard jurisdiction. Crimson has not yet determined whether Artem's emergence changes the scroll's value as a vampire origin document, or whether the Eternal Guard's custody of Artem makes Crimson's research objective obsolete. The question is under active internal review.

- **Dravenmore Connection:** House Dravenmore's genealogical registry — the foundation of Crimson's vampire population tracking capability — is maintained through a funding arrangement that neither party publicly acknowledges. Dravenmore receives institutional support for its innate lineage research; Crimson receives the most comprehensive vampire population dataset in existence. The arrangement has persisted across multiple eras. Neither party has ever formalized it in a document that could be subpoenaed by Internal Review.
