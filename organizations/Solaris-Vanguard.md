# Solaris Vanguard

*Motto: "From the Dawn, We Stand."*

The oldest surviving guild in continuous operation, founded in the aftermath of the Cataclysm of Yggdra. The founding generation described its purpose as ensuring that the catastrophe that shattered the old world would not recur through insufficient preparation. That founding intent has shaped the guild's culture and operational emphasis across all subsequent eras.

---

## Overview

- **Specialization:** Radiant, Holy, and Frost-affinity monsters; defensive and purification magic; containment operations on Unholy breach events.
- **MOD Relationship:** Full compliance with a traditionalist posture — the Vanguard fulfills regulatory requirements but maintains internal standards that exceed MOD minimums. It does not need to request MOD contract delegation; it receives it because its operational record on Radiant and Holy threats is unmatched across guild history.
- **Recruitment:** Heavily favors Elves, Astrae, and practitioners with Radiant or Holy affinities. Membership is treated internally as a long-term commitment rather than a career stage.
- **Public Perception:** Revered and occasionally regarded as archaic. The Vanguard is the institutional memory of what Hyperion looked like before the current order. Some find that comforting. Others find it inflexible.

---

## Notable History

**The Irenmere Sentinels:** Two Holy-tier monsters observed defending the Pass alongside Valdren Ashcrast during the Seventh Era siege were approached by a Vanguard research team following the battle. Their behavior during the siege was assessed as benign; the engagement was concluded without neutralization. This remains an anomaly in the Vanguard's operational record and an ongoing subject of internal study.

## Institutional Notes

The Vanguard's founding predates MOD by a significant margin. It was not created by institutional design; it was created by survivors with a specific memory of what insufficient preparation looked like. That origin is present in how the guild conducts itself — it does not wait for MOD frameworks to tell it what a threat is, because its institutional memory includes eras when no such frameworks existed.

The Vanguard's relationship with the Keepers of Nil is complicated by Seraphiel Oswin's departure. The Vanguard has taken no official position on Oswin's continued affiliation. In internal communications, the subject is described as "Oswin's ongoing leave assignment."
