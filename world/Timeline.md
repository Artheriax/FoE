# Timeline of Hyperion

Hyperion's history does not begin with its written record. The Era system — the dominant chronological framework used by scholars, the MOD, and institutional bodies — covers the period from the formal establishment of historical documentation forward, with Pre-Record events reconstructed from physical evidence, surviving legend, and the contested archive of the Observing One. Events further back than the record reaches are classified as Cosmological Origins: the province of theoretical research, sealed Eternal Guard files, and stories most scholars treat as mythology.

What follows is a condensed timeline organized from oldest to most recent. Each era is understood to represent a distinct phase of Hyperion's existential and institutional development, though the exact duration of each era is not uniformly agreed upon. The record is incomplete by design and by accident in roughly equal measure.

**Note on Era Numbering:** Historical texts reference a "First Era" as the anchor point from which earlier periods were retroactively labeled. No surviving documentation covers First Era events in full. What is catalogued begins with the Second Era, making it the practical starting point of the formal historical record. Pre-Record and Cosmological Origins are reconstructed, not directly documented.

## Cosmological Origins

**Status:** *[Sealed / Theoretical]*

The events in this tier predate all records and all dimensions. The Eternal Guard holds the only documented research on this period, and that research is sealed above MOD access level. What is reconstructed from theoretical sources, Power-Sources scholarship, and the case of Artem suggests the following sequence:

- **Entity Zero** exists prior to dimensional structure — the substrate from which Cosmic power Sources were later separated. Whether Entity Zero is a being, a state, or a principle is unresolved.
- A researcher named **Artem**, originating from approximately 3,000 years before the present, is pulled through a failing dungeon rift and enters what he describes as the pre-universe state — before time, before dimension, before form. He is imprisoned in the Wall of Nihility for 3 million years (external time) / 30 days (subjective experience).
- Artem and Entity Zero interact. She builds the cosmos from his descriptions of what the world was like. The Higher Ones emerge as transcendent reflections of aspects of him: the Observing One from his curiosity, the Judging One from his accountability, the Creating One from his delight in function.
- The Higher Ones discover Artem's relationship to their own origin. They imprison him in the Wall of Nihility, a boundary structure designed to process him indefinitely. This imprisonment lasts millions of years by external measurement; thirty days by his.
- Artem walks out of the Wall in Sersey's present — 3,000 years after his original era.

The Wall of Nihility itself is described in Power-Sources scholarship as an inverted substrate boundary: not a location inside the dimensional hierarchy, but the point at which the hierarchy ends.

## Pre-Record Events

**Status:** *[Pre-Record] — events reconstructed from physical evidence, legend, and cross-cultural records*

The Pre-Record period covers the formation of documented Hyperion — the settling of races, the early emergence of Gazes and Sigils, and the first major cosmological disruptions. Dates are estimated and contested.

**The Second Weave** *[Pre-Record] [Contested]*
An event of cosmological significance referenced in multiple surviving texts from incompatible cultural traditions. Physical evidence consistent with dimensional restructuring. Whether this represents the creation of Hyperion's current dimensional arrangement or a catastrophic alteration of a prior one is unknown. The Keepers of Nil cross-reference the Second Weave with their founding documentation; details sealed.

**Cataclysm of Yggdra** *[Pre-Record]*
The collapse or destruction of the world-tree structure that is believed to have predated current Elven civilization. The Elves' current relationship to their Trees of Life is understood by most scholars as a reconstitution or continuation of what was lost here. The full scope of the event is unknown. Three confirmed casts of Nihility Magic are believed by some theoretical scholars to have occurred in this period; the causal relationship is contested.

**Collapse of the Crimson Tower** *[Pre-Record]*
The Crimson Tower at Valdas is the most documented Pre-Record structure whose destruction can be assigned to a specific ability. Bearer of the Gaze of Destruction, *Karthyn the Undoing*, reportedly unmade the tower rather than allowed it to fall into enemy hands. Physical evidence confirms the tower ceased to exist rather than collapsed. Karthyn's fate is unrecorded.

**The Fracturing of the Eastern Rift** *[Pre-Record]*
A major dimensional boundary event. Physical evidence suggests a significant tear in the dimensional fabric of the region now called the Eastern territories. Whether this is a cause or consequence of other Pre-Record events is unknown; most theoretical models treat it as a consequence.

## Second Era

**Status:** *[Formal Record begins]*

The Second Era represents the start of the documented institutional record. Eras preceding it were assigned retroactively. MOD predecessor bodies are operating by this period and beginning to establish regulatory precedents.

**The Compact Defiant Adjudication**
An individual holding a Devil-signed infernal contract contested the binding before MOD authorities on grounds that their Absolute Sigil of Soul Anchoring rendered contract fulfillment physically impossible. The MOD panel ruled affirmatively — the Sigil overrode the contractual obligation. This ruling remains the only affirmative decision of its kind and has not been successfully challenged. It established the foundational precedent that Sigil-based physical impossibility constitutes a valid defense against infernal contract enforcement.

## Third Era

**Status:** *[Documented]*

The Third Era shows a significant expansion of hunter culture, Gaze registration, and the first formally recorded incidents requiring institutional response. The MOD's predecessor structure is clearly operational; modern regulatory frameworks begin to take shape.

**The Dual Sigil Incident**
A vassal of House Morvayne was identified as bearing two simultaneous Sigils — an event without documented precedent at the time. The case became the foundation for Dravenmore genealogical research into Dual Sigil lineage inheritance patterns.

**The Veiled Operations**
A bearer of the Gaze of Seduction (evolving to Gaze of Enchantment) operated as an intelligence asset across a thirty-year career, extracting information from high-ranking officials without force. Methods remain classified. The operative disappeared during the Fractioning.

**Shadow Magic Case I: Ashenveil Disturbance**
First formally documented case of Shadow Magic possession on Hyperion. A practitioner of Deep Umbraturgy lost coherent self during a sustained shadow-space working; the resulting entity drove approximately four hundred casualties across a three-month active period before containment. Protocol origin case for Shadow Magic response.

**Terthas Clearseeing**
First Revelation Gaze bearer registered by what would become the MOD. Served forty-three years as Chief Investigator for the Council of Oversight; retired at age seventy-two.

**The Ironfield Surge**
Approximately twelve thousand Lesser monsters emerged simultaneously from what was classified as a low-activity dungeon — the single largest Lesser emergence on record. Six weeks of prior survey failed to detect precursor activity. Dungeon reclassified following the event. Protocol origin case for dungeon pulse cycle monitoring.

## Fourth Era

**Status:** *[Documented] [Partial Seal — Callax records at Eternal Guard authority]*

The Fourth Era is defined institutionally by the Callax Abomination — the event from which the MOD's harshest standing protocol originates.

**The Callax Abomination: Soul Forging Case Origin**
Mage Callax, operating under sealed institutional affiliation, practiced Soul Forging across a three-year active period, producing forty-seven documented victims. The Eternal Guard intervened directly — one of the earliest documented interventions where the Eternal Guard acted on a mortal-plane case without MOD coordination. Callax was executed without trial. Soul Forging was classified as extinction-level forbidden magic; the standing protocol executes confirmed practitioners on discovery without appeal.

## Mid-Era (Ashenmoor)

**Status:** *[Documented]*

The Mid-Era takes its name from the Ashenmoor Campaigns — a significant military conflict that produced several of the period's most documented practitioners and anchored the era's institutional development. Dungeon activity was elevated throughout this period; Keystone-class protocols were first drafted in response to Ashenmoor-era emergence events.

**The Ashenmoor Campaigns**
A three-year military deployment that defined the period. Produced multiple figures of lasting institutional significance, including Orvielle Caste and Healer Cassian.

**Healer Cassian of the Ashenmoor**
Pioneer of practical Luminancy field medicine. His restoration-rate degradation after sustained field deployment became the protocol origin case for Psyche Source depletion monitoring. Died at age sixty-seven from the same degradation mechanism he first documented. Maintained a twelve-year formalized relationship with House Dravenmore.

**Glaciarch Frostweaver**
Theoretical and applied Cryomancy practitioner whose foundational work predated formal Keystone protocols by three decades. First systematic engagement with Frost-class Keystone specimens.

**Serene of the Ashenveil**
Bearer of the Gaze of Harmony. Medic and spiritual counselor documented to have halted a riot between rival hunter factions through Gaze intervention. Died at eighty-nine, peacefully.

**The Valley of Ashemark Incident** *(~264 Eastern Era, Gazes.md sourced)*
A bearer of the Gaze of Destruction underwent racial advancement during an active field engagement. The Gaze evolved to Cataclysm mid-event, producing the canyon formation now known as the Valley of Ashemark. The bearer did not survive the evolution. Case origin for the Supreme Gaze of Cataclysm.

**Corven Markward**
Bearer of the Gaze of Kinesis. Responsible for the assassination of Warlord Meckris during Treaty Negotiations — a single arrow at impossible range. Registered posthumously by MOD following investigation.

**Shadow Magic Case II: Maltheryx Anchor Mutation**
A practitioner of Shadow Magic was exposed to Maltheryx-adjacent dimensional space under uncontrolled conditions. Subject Meridian was retrieved and contained; the mutation to their Psyche structure was not reversible. Active research ongoing under seal.

## Fifth Era

**Status:** *[Documented]*

The Fifth Era is defined by the first encounters with Keystone-class monsters at the scale that required revised engagement standards — specifically Dream-class specimens, whose effects on Source stability and subjective time created problems the existing protocols could not address.

**The Dreaming of Valcrest**
A Dream-class Keystone specimen (designated NKS-1) was encountered in the Valcrest region. Seventeen civilians entered comatose states during the engagement period. Three hunters involved in the neutralization attempt were permanently displaced into what post-incident analysis characterized as a Dream-adjacent substrate. The displaced hunters are confirmed living by remote Source readings; recovery has not been achieved. Engagement standards for Dream-class Keystones were revised. The subjective-duration disclosure requirement originates here.

**The Northern Face Temporal Weaver**
A Time-class Keystone engagement resulting in fourteen MOD personnel displaced across temporal substrates; six placed in permanent care. First documented case requiring temporal displacement response protocols.

## Sixth Era

**Status:** *[Documented] [Partial Seal — Collapse aftermath at Eternal Guard authority]*

The Sixth Era produced the most severe documented dimensional event in Hyperion's recorded history. Its consequences remain active.

**Ghastward of Ashenveil**
Bearer of the Gaze of Spirits. Operated as a paranormal investigator and spiritual medium for over forty years. Maintained Dreamveil projection access throughout her career — a duration MOD's Assessment Unit considers anomalous for a mortal-tier bearer. Died at eighty-one. Considered a saint by the Shepherd sects.

**The Sixth Era Collapse: Reality Fabrication Catastrophe**
Practitioners Thessalyn and Korven, bearing the Absolute or evolved Illusion Gaze line, initiated a Reality Fabrication event that produced forty-seven confirmed parallel temporal overlays and fractured approximately four thousand square kilometers of territorial fabric. The Eternal Guard quarantined the affected region permanently. Current Hexline routes were rerouted to avoid the quarantine zone. Thessalyn and Korven's fate is classified.

**The Caldenmere Void Reacher**
A Space-class Keystone specimen that was never successfully neutralized. Presumed displaced by the dimensional disruption of the Sixth Era Collapse. Its current location is unknown; the engagement file is open.

## Seventh Era

**Status:** *[Documented] [Active sealed cases]*

The Seventh Era is the most recent era before the Modern. Its defining contribution to current Hyperion is the Hexline infrastructure, which restructured continental transit and became the backbone of MOD operational reach. The era also produced several incidents that remain sealed and ongoing.

**Siege of Irenmere Pass**
Valdren Ashcrast, bearer of the Sigil of Fortitude, held Irenmere Pass against fourteen-to-one odds for thirty-one hours. The engagement is documented as one of the longest sustained solo-Sigil combats on record. Cross-referenced with the Irenmere Sentinels anomaly in Solaris Vanguard records: two Holy-class specimens appeared during the siege and engaged against the opposing force — an event that has no satisfactory institutional explanation.

**The Stormcalm Trials** *(~289 Eastern Era, Gazes.md sourced)*
A Kinesis Gaze bearer undergoing advanced hunter testing evolved their Gaze to Omnikinesis mid-trial. The event concluded peacefully. Case origin for the Supreme Gaze of Omnikinesis.

**Archon Dravenmore: Hexline System Architecture**
Beginning in the late Seventh Era and extending into the Modern, the preeminent Spatial Magic practitioner of the period formalized the Hexline network — stabilized Aetheric pathways allowing rapid continental transit. Dravenmore holds the only lifetime contract in MOD's Registry history. Songweaver Lyric contributed the stabilization harmonics that allow Hexlines to persist without continuous maintenance; MOD records attribute infrastructure authorship to Dravenmore exclusively.

**The Sleeper Incident**
An Absolute-tier Mindscape Gaze event. Details are sealed. MOD records acknowledge the incident; the Eternal Guard has assumed ongoing engagement authority. The incident is listed as active.

## Modern Era

**Status:** *[Documented / Ongoing]*

The Modern Era is the present period. MOD operations are fully formalized; the Hexline network is operational; hunter culture is at its institutional peak. Several incidents from prior eras remain open or sealed and ongoing.

**The Stormstep Operations**
An individual bearing the Gaze of Predation and the Sigil of Agility conducted seventeen documented operations in the Ashenveil quarter over an eight-year active period. Identified posthumously. MOD Internal Review 22nd Cycle has not issued a ruling on whether the MOD identification failure during the active period constitutes a systemic gap.

**The Voss Suppression Case**
MOD analyst Maelen Voss suppressed a documented Sigil of Senses throughout a thirty-year MOD Internal Review career. The suppression was maintained without detection. When identified, Voss was found to also exhibit indicators consistent with the Gaze of Inner Thought and Gaze of Revelation. Internal Review 22nd Cycle acknowledged the case and is conducting an ongoing procedural review with no ruling issued.

**Sersey's Present**
The current moment. Artem walks out of the Wall of Nihility. The Echo Breakers are active. Several sealed incidents from prior eras — the Sleeper Incident, the Caldenmere Void Reacher file, the Sixth Era Collapse quarantine — remain unresolved. New questions are accumulating faster than answers.
