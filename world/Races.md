## Entity Zero

Entity Zero is referenced in the most ancient and sealed texts as something prior to the framework of existence — not a race, not a being that exists within reality's structure, but the substrate from which that structure was apparently separated. Most scholars who have engaged with Entity Zero as a subject of research have been redirected, had their materials sealed, or have simply ceased writing about it.

What is documented is the gap: a category of cosmological reasoning that does not function without it, and a category of phenomena — the Wall of Nihility, the behavior of Cosmic Sources under exhaustion, the properties of Nil Space — that are consistent with the existence of something prior to and outside the normal framework. The Eternal Guard archives contain one entry on the subject accessible to any mortal reader. It describes Entity Zero as *"pre-intentional."* Nothing further is accessible.

## The Higher Ones

The Higher Ones occupy the topmost layer of the cosmological hierarchy. They are not beings in any sense mortals typically use the term — they do not maintain consistent physical forms, do not hold ongoing relationships with mortal institutions, and do not communicate through channels that translate meaningfully to mortal cognition. They are forces with apparent direction and intent, observed through their effects and the patterns of their documented interventions.

### The One

The One is perceived as the confluence of all other Higher Ones, a singular entity possessing absolute control over every form of cosmic power. It is seen by many as the supreme being. Those who believe in Entity Zero see The One as a lesser — though still immense — power. A common criticism among cosmic scholars is that The One is inherently selfish: it possesses ultimate power yet is never known to act, remaining a passive, observing totality.

### The Outer One

The Outer One is akin to The One but defined by its domain: the spaces outside the known universe. It is alien and incomprehensible, most at home in the boundless voids between realities. It is widely credited with the creation of the Aether, and by extension the Aetherians and Aetherions. Its existence is debated, as its nature places it fundamentally beyond mortal perception and understanding.

### The Destroying One

The Destroying One is the embodiment of annihilation — a force of unparalleled devastation tied to the end of matter, energy, and reality. It is not inherently malevolent; its function is a necessary part of the cosmic cycle. It destroys to pave the way for new creation, acting as a cosmic reset to prevent stagnation. Some believe it destroys for its own sake; others argue it is an impartial force of nature, acting only when the flow of the universe demands it. Its power can erase worlds, histories, and memories with a mere thought.

### The Creating One

The Creating One is the counterpart to the Destroying One, embodying genesis, growth, and the manifestation of new realities. It is the source of all creation, breathing life into the void and forming galaxies, species, and laws of magic. It is often viewed through two lenses: as a benevolent, nurturing force, or as an indifferent cosmic function that creates without personal investment. It fosters the birth of civilizations and the awakening of new possibilities, ensuring the universe is in a constant state of becoming.

### The Judging One

The Judging One is the cosmic arbiter of law, often depicted as a grim, unwavering executioner. It does not concern itself with petty mortal conflicts but enforces fundamental cosmic law. It judges those who disrupt the universal order — through forbidden magic, reality-warping, or defying the balance — and delivers absolute verdicts. Its punishments are tailored to the crime and may be carried out by itself or entrusted to enforcers like the Eternal Guards. It is feared and revered, for its judgment is inescapable and its sentence final.

### The Balancing One

The Balancing One is the enigmatic right hand of the Judging One. Where the Judging One determines guilt, the Balancing One ensures equilibrium is restored. Its methods are fluid and mysterious, as balance is not always synonymous with equality. It might ensure no single force dominates by fostering an opposing force, or it might subtly influence fate to correct imbalances. It does not seek fairness, but rather the maintenance of a tense, dynamic equilibrium. It is the silent architect who ensures the universe never tips too far into chaos or order.

### The Intervening One

The Intervening One is a being of unpredictable will. It does not weave fate or guide events, but instead watches until the most pivotal moment — then chooses whether to step in and alter the course of history or remain a silent observer. Its interventions are rare, monumental, and governed by a logic beyond mortal comprehension. It is one of the most feared Higher Ones, for its actions are entirely its own and cannot be predicted, reasoned with, or defied.

### The Observing One (Phariah)

The Observing One, also known as Phariah, is the ultimate historian. It exists to watch, record, and collect all knowledge without ever intervening or allowing itself to be seen. Legends say it was once a powerful being that defied cosmic law and was punished by the Judging One to forever observe without acting. It is the silent eyes of the higher forces, a passive recorder of all that has ever happened. Some believe it decides what is remembered and what is erased from reality itself.

Petitioners have been known to attempt access to Phariah's archive — the most comprehensive record of Hyperion's history in existence. The extent to which any have succeeded is, appropriately, not in any accessible record. *→ `world/History.md` — Pre-Record Events*

### The Nonexistent One

The Nonexistent One is a paradox — a being that both exists and does not. No one has ever seen it, spoken to it, or proven its existence, yet its name appears in ancient texts across dimensions. It is theorized to reside in Nihility, the dimension of nothingness, or to be the remnant of a being that was utterly erased, leaving only an absence behind. To study it is considered dangerous, as one risks being similarly erased from history and memory.

### The Forgotten One

Nothing is known of The Forgotten One, for it has been utterly erased from all memory and record. Its name is the only evidence it ever existed. Perhaps the other Higher Ones know its fate, but they do not speak of it. Its existence serves as a silent testament to the ultimate power of oblivion.

### The Nameless One

The Nameless One is a being without identity. It has no fixed name, form, or presence. It is said to wander the cosmos, shifting its shape and stealing the appearances of others. Those who encounter it struggle to recall what they saw, and details fade immediately. The greatest mystery is its lack of a name; to name something is to define it, and the Nameless One remains unbound. Some believe it was stripped of its name as punishment; others think it never had one, existing as a flaw in reality itself. It is a shadow of forgotten truths, forever searching for an identity it cannot possess.

## MOD Racial Registration Framework

MOD's registration mandates do not apply uniformly across race categories. A significant portion of documented races operate entirely outside MOD jurisdiction; several more occupy contested or exceptional positions. The framework below reflects institutional policy as documented in public regulatory filings.

**Outside MOD Jurisdiction (Cosmic and Supreme Races):**
Higher Ones, Entity Zero, Eternarcs, Nebular Tyrants, and the Eternal Guard race operate outside MOD regulatory authority entirely. Ascendants are technically within MOD's registration mandate until their advancement exceeds measurable parameters, at which point their file is reclassified and closed to standard access. Primordials and Celestials interact with mortal institutions on terms they set, not terms MOD sets.

**Monitored Non-Registrant Races (Spiritual Races):**
Nephryx, Maltheryx, Seraphon, and Xenysian cannot hold standard hunter licenses. Xenysian are the exception within the group — their spiritual mediation capacity makes them operationally valuable to MOD, and contracted Xenysian individuals may hold limited conditional registrations for specific engagement types. Maltheryx are not eligible for any registration; they are a threat classification, not a hunter classification.

**High Races — Variable Registration Status:**

- **Angels and Arch-Angels:** Full voluntary compliance. Angels integrate into hunter structures naturally and are among the most institutional-friendly of the High Races. Arch-Angels rarely register independently; their operations on Hyperion are typically considered divine-mandate deployments rather than MOD-governed activity.
- **Draconians:** Standard registration with mandatory transformation disclosure. Draconian hunters are required to register both forms separately in terms of capability assessment; failing to disclose transformation capability in an engagement is sanctionable under Field Operations Code, Section 14.
- **Aetherions:** Standard registration. Their advanced Aether-manipulation capacity places most in the B–S rank range upon initial assessment.
- **Umbraxans:** Restricted registration. MOD maintains additional oversight requirements for Umbraxan hunters given their Void-adjacent power profile. Umbra Shroud affiliation among Umbraxan hunters is common and is noted in Registry files without implication.
- **Devils:** Rarely register voluntarily. No prohibition exists in public filings, but processing time for Devil applications is, by observation, substantially longer than average. MOD institutional distrust of Devil registration is attributable in part to documented infernal contract manipulation history.
- **Demons:** Prohibited from hunter registration. Demon presence in Hyperion is treated as a Field Operations matter, not a Registry matter.

**Middle Races — Standard and Special Registration:**

- **Astrae, Aetherian, Voidling, Elf, Dark Elf:** Standard registration. Astrae receive priority Gaze and Sigil assessment upon registration due to elevated documented incidence of high-tier abilities in their population. Dark Elf registration rates are below average; Assessment Unit files on Dark Elf Gaze bearer incidence are acknowledged internally as incomplete.
- **Vampires:** House-level contracts with MOD predate the current individual registration framework. Vampire hunters register through house affiliation; the three houses (Noctaris, Morvayne, Dravenmore) maintain individual institutional agreements with MOD not published in public regulatory filings. Vampires operating outside house affiliation register as independent contractors with standard requirements plus an infernal-contract disclosure provision.

**Mortal Races — Standard Registration:**
Arcanians, Kitsune, and Humans register under standard protocols. By volume, these three races constitute the majority of the Registry's 344,000 active files.

## Detailed Race Descriptions

### Eternarc

The Eternarcs are the first confirmed beings outside the enigmatic Higher Ones, acting as cosmic overseers and regulators. They are not abstract concepts like their superiors but tangible, recorded entities who enforce the cosmic law. Their primary role is to maintain order among the immensely powerful races below them — Primordials and Celestials — preventing their power from destabilizing existence. Eternarcs are known to act as judges, wardens, and keepers of forbidden knowledge. They are the architects of cosmic stability, answering only to the Higher Ones, and are the ones who decree the title of Nebular Tyrant upon those who defy the universal order.

The Eternal Guards are their creation and principal enforcement instrument. What the Eternarcs decree, the Eternal Guards execute.

### Nebular Tyrant

A Nebular Tyrant is not a natural race but a title of condemnation bestowed by the Eternarcs. It marks a being — once among the most powerful in existence — that has fallen to utter corruption, greed, or a desire for absolute dominion. Once declared, they are exiled from the cosmic order, stripped of divine rights, and hunted for eternity by the Eternal Guards. Their very presence warps reality, bringing chaos and misfortune. Notable condemned examples include Xal'Zorrath the Cosmic Ruin and Vaelgor the Abyss Monarch — beings of immense power now dedicated solely to their own destructive ambitions, serving as dark warnings of the price of unchecked ambition.

### Eternal Guard

The Eternal Guards are the elite enforcement force of the cosmic order, created by the Eternarcs to hunt Nebular Tyrants and uphold universal law. They are relentless, unyielding warriors who cannot be truly killed; if destroyed, a new one is reborn from cosmic energy. They wield Unmaking Blades capable of erasing a being from reality itself, can traverse dimensions instantly, and are governed by a strict hierarchy led by the Arbiter of Judgment. They are both revered as holy protectors and feared as ultimate executioners, representing the absolute and impartial justice of the cosmos.

Their operational presence in Hyperion's mortal-adjacent affairs is documented in institutional records under the same name — the Eternal Guard — acting as a superseding authority above MOD on sealed matters of forbidden magic, extinction-level threats, and classified research. Whether the mortal-plane institutional Eternal Guard is a formal operational extension of the cosmic Eternal Guards acting through mortal instruments, or a convergent authority that has inherited the name and mandate, is a question with no publicly accessible answer.

*→ `organizations/Organizations.md` — The Eternal Guard*

### Ascendant

Ascendants are beings of limitless potential who exist outside the control of any other entity. They begin their existence as members of a standard race, with no outward signs of their true nature. Unlike others, they do not have a maximum potential form — they continuously grow stronger, forever pushing beyond known limits. Their path is unpredictable and incredibly rare, making them wild cards in the cosmic balance. Some believe an Ascendant may one day challenge or even surpass the Eternarcs themselves.

MOD registration of Ascendants follows standard protocols until their advancement exceeds measurable parameters, at which point the file is reclassified and closed to standard access. No Ascendant has remained within documented MOD registration beyond that point. What they become afterward is outside institutional record.

*→ `systems/Power-Sources.md` — The Source Scale: From Mortal to Cosmic*

### Primordial

Primordials are the fundamental forces of the universe given form. They are not gods of specific things but embodiments of vast, all-encompassing concepts like Time, Creation, Destruction, or Life and Death itself. They are the backbone of reality, shaping the very laws of existence. While immensely powerful, they are subservient to the Eternarcs, who ensure their actions do not disrupt the cosmic balance. Their interaction with mortals is rare, felt in the turning of ages and the birth and death of galaxies rather than in direct intervention.

### Celestial

Celestials are the traditional, accessible gods of the universe. Each governs a specific aspect of existence — War, Love, Death, Nature — and resides in a personal realm reflecting their domain (e.g., Luminara for Light). They are highly involved in mortal affairs, accepting worship, bestowing blessings, and intervening directly in times of great need. While powerful, they are far below the Primordials in scope and answer to the broader cosmic structure maintained by the Eternarcs. They are the most common point of contact for mortals seeking divine favor.

### Nephryx

The Nephryx are benevolent, peaceful spiritual beings. They exist as ethereal presences rather than physical forms, radiating calm and serenity. Their role is to guide and heal those deemed worthy, offering wisdom and comfort during times of emotional or spiritual turmoil. They are the antithesis of the Maltheryx, representing the gentle, healing side of the spirit world and often serving as unseen mentors to those with pure hearts.

### Maltheryx

Maltheryx are rare and dangerous spirits born from immense, unresolved hatred and a thirst for vengeance. They are the dark counterpart to the Nephryx. These vengeful spirits manifest in the physical world to torment and destroy those they believe wronged them, often through haunting, possession, or spreading corruption. They are creatures of pure malice, difficult to control or destroy, and represent the catastrophic consequences of hatred that persists beyond death.

The Shadow Magic Case I Ashenveil Disturbance and Shadow Magic Case II Maltheryx Anchor Mutation document the outcomes of attempted mortal contact with Maltheryx-class entities. Both produced containment-level consequences. The name of the second case type is taken directly from this race. *→ `world/History.md` — Shadow Magic Case I; Shadow Magic Case II | `systems/Arcane.md` — Forbidden Magic: Shadow Magic (Deep Umbraturgy)*

### Seraphon

Seraphons are serene, spiritual beings who embody peace, wisdom, and profound inner calm. They are master healers of the soul, capable of mending even the deepest emotional wounds through their mere presence. Deeply connected to Dreamveil (the Spirit World), they serve as guides for spiritual enlightenment. Their teachings on meditation and balance are highly sought after by elves and monks across Hyperion, who revere them as beacons of tranquility.

### Xenysian

The Xenysian are mediators between the physical and spiritual worlds. They possess the unique ability to communicate directly with spirits like the Nephryx and Maltheryx, and can project their spirit into Dreamveil, leaving their vulnerable physical body behind. This makes them invaluable guides and interpreters but also exposes them to great danger from malevolent spirits. A core ambition of their culture is to one day achieve a full physical presence within Dreamveil, a feat few have accomplished.

Contracted Xenysian are sometimes enlisted by MOD in advisory roles during Unholy breach events or Maltheryx-adjacent hauntings, operating under limited conditional registrations. *→ `organizations/Organizations.md` — MOD Racial Registration Framework*

### Aetherion

Aetherions are the pinnacle of the Aetherian race, supreme masters of the Aether — the energy between realms. They wield profound reality-manipulating magic on a cosmic scale, far surpassing normal Aetherians. They are living legends within their society, revered as sages and advisors. They are distinguished by their glowing, light-colored eyes and intricate markings on their arms, symbols of their immense mastery and connection to the Aether.

### Devil

Devils are cunning, malevolent beings from Gehenna (Hell) who exclusively appear male. Unlike chaotic demons, devils rely on intellect, manipulation, and infernal contracts. They appear as horned, refined humanoids, often dressed elegantly to deceive and persuade mortals into signing magical pacts that always hide disastrous fine print. They are masters of long-term schemes and political intrigue, thriving on the corruption of souls rather than mindless destruction.

The Compact Defiant Adjudication (Second Era) established the legal precedent that Soul Anchoring Sigil bearers are exempt from infernal contractual provisions — a ruling that arose directly from a Devil house's attempt to enforce a soul extraction contract. The ruling has been cited in contract law forty-three times since and has never been successfully challenged. *→ `world/History.md` — The Compact Defiant Adjudication | `systems/Sigils.md` — Absolute Sigil of Soul Anchoring*

### Arch-Angel

Arch-Angels are the elite warriors of Luminara (Heaven), a superior class of Angel. They are significantly more powerful than common Angels, with larger wings adorned with golden metallic elements and an intensely radiant aura. They are the commanders of angelic hosts, guardians of Luminara, and enforcers of divine justice. Their holy magic is devastating to unholy creatures, and their presence on the mortal plane often signifies a divine intervention of great importance.

### Draconian

Draconians are powerful beings with the innate ability to transform into dragons. This is a fixed path of growth; their strength and abilities in both forms are determined by the type of dragon they are linked to (frost, blaze, terra, and others). They cannot naturally progress beyond this form unless they undergo a special transformation to change their type of dragon.

MOD registration requires both forms to be independently assessed for capability. A Draconian whose dragon form substantially exceeds their registered output is subject to re-assessment and rank recalibration; failure to disclose transformation capability is sanctionable under Field Operations Code, Section 14. Physical Sigils manifest in Draconian hunters at higher rates than most other High Races — attributed theoretically to the intensive Vitalis demands of dual-form physiology. *→ `systems/Sigils.md` — Sigil Awakening | `systems/Power-Sources.md` — Physical Source*

### Demon

Demons are chaotic beings from Gehenna (Hell) who delight in the forbidden and revel in chaos. They can mimic the appearances and personalities of other species but cannot replicate their innate energy, making their deceptions hollow. They come in various types: Succubi (female seducers who drain energy through pleasure), Fiends (agents of pure mayhem), and Shades (elusive tormentors). Some demons have used their seductive and fearsome natures to integrate into and manipulate societies on worlds like Hyperion.

### Angel

Angels are celestial beings who reside among mortals on worlds rich with holy magic, like regions of Hyperion. They are humanoid with large feathered wings and often a faint ethereal glow. They are natural wielders of holy magic, making them formidable opponents to dark creatures like demons and vampires. While powerful, they are not invincible and rely on their magic, making them vulnerable to anti-magic measures. They often ally with races like the Arcanians to uphold justice.

The Solaris Vanguard maintains documented historical relationships with Angel populations in Hyperion; several Angels hold long-standing guild memberships. Their Luminancy affinity makes them natural allies for Radiant and Holy monster engagement operations. *→ `organizations/Organizations.md` — Solaris Vanguard | `systems/Arcane.md` — Luminancy*

### Umbraxan

Umbraxans are a variant of the Voidlings, born from the enigmatic Void dimension. They possess a deeper connection to the Void's energies and are more aggressive and powerful than their peaceful predecessors. They are skilled warriors who use the Void's power for combat and manipulation, often venturing into physical worlds to expand their influence or seek resources that strengthen their bond to their dark home.

Umbraxan hunters register under restricted MOD protocols. Their Void-adjacent power profile creates classification overlap with certain Gaze categories that the Assessment Unit monitors actively. Umbra Shroud affiliation among Umbraxan hunters is common and is noted in Registry files without implication. *→ `organizations/Organizations.md` — The Umbra Shroud; MOD Racial Registration Framework*

### Astrae

Astrae are a rare and revered offshoot of Elves, considered holy offspring. They are distinguished by intricate, glowing markings on their faces that signify their divine connection. They possess access to higher-tier magic and celestial crafting techniques beyond the reach of normal Elves, allowing them to create powerful artifacts and commune with divine beings. They are seen as spiritual leaders and prophets within Elven society.

Astrae have the highest documented incidence of Gaze and Sigil manifestation of any mortal or middle race in MOD Assessment Unit records. All Astrae registrations trigger priority review from the Gaze and Sigil Assessment Unit as a matter of standard protocol — not as a sanction, but as a resource allocation the Unit considers too reliably productive to skip. *→ `systems/Gazes.md` — MOD Registry Overview | `systems/Sigils.md` — MOD Classification | `organizations/Organizations.md` — Gaze and Sigil Assessment Unit*

### Aetherian

Aetherians are a race closely resembling Humans but with a natural connection to the Aether, granting them minor reality-manipulating abilities. Their most distinctive feature is their brightly glowing eyes, which shine in colors like blue, pink, or green. While not as powerful as their Aetherion cousins, they are still advanced magic-wielders, often seen as scholars and innovators within the cosmic community.

### Voidling

Voidlings are a peaceful race originating from the Void. They resemble Elves with pointed ears, black hair often tinged with purple, and dark, mysterious eyes. They lack the glowing markings of Umbraxans. Their connection to the Void is used for utilitarian purposes and daily life rather than combat. They are contemplative beings who prefer the serene nothingness of their home dimension, rarely venturing outward and avoiding conflict.

Gaze manifestations in Voidling hunters tend toward psychic and dimensional affinities — Inner Thought, Binding, and occasionally void-adjacent Gaze variations. Combined with their naturally low profile and cultural preference for non-disclosure, Voidling Gaze bearers are among the most likely to go unidentified for extended periods. *→ `systems/Gazes.md` — Gaze Registration*

### Elf

The Elves of Hyperion are a graceful, all-female race born from sacred Trees of Life, the most revered being the Home Tree, Iloni. They are deeply connected to nature and are master enchanters and artisans, known for their longevity and wisdom. Their society is matriarchal and spiritual, centered around the preservation of their forests and the magic that flows through them.

Elven Gaze manifestations historically cluster around harmony, spiritual, and perception-based categories. Several of the most significant Gaze bearers in the historical record are Elven: Karthyn the Undoing (Destruction Gaze, Pre-Record), Serene of the Ashenveil (probable Harmony Gaze, Mid-Era), Ghastward of Ashenveil (probable Spirits Gaze, Mid-Era). Whether this reflects genuinely elevated incidence or the superior recordkeeping of long-lived Elven Tree archives is a question the Assessment Unit has not formally resolved. The Solaris Vanguard's historical composition and culture skew Elven; the guild was founded in the aftermath of the Cataclysm of Yggdra by individuals who had survived it.

The oldest surviving document referencing the Second Weave is held in a sealed archive of an Elven Tree of Life. It has not been made available to MOD. *→ `world/History.md` — The Second Weave; The Cataclysm of Yggdra | `systems/Gazes.md` — Gaze of Harmony; Gaze of Spirits | `organizations/Organizations.md` — Solaris Vanguard*

### Dark Elf

Dark Elves are a divergent subspecies that emerged from Elven populations permanently severed from the Trees of Life — whether by displacement, deliberate choice, or circumstances the surviving records describe only obliquely. The separation, wherever it occurred, was complete: no Dark Elf community has successfully re-established a Tree of Life bond in the documented historical period. Elven scholars who will discuss the question at all consider re-establishment theoretically possible but practically unknown.

The most significant consequence of this separation is biological. Elves are born exclusively from Trees of Life and are exclusively female by that process. Dark Elves, removed from that birth mechanism, reproduce biologically. Their population includes both males and females — a divergence that compounds over generations into social and cultural differences that now extend well beyond the original magical one.

**Appearance:** Dark Elves carry the physical structure of Elves — pointed ears, similar height and proportions — but their coloring shifted over generations in low-light environments. Skin tones range from deep gray to near-obsidian, occasionally pale silver-white in isolated populations. Hair is typically white, silver, or stark black. Their eyes do not glow at rest; instead, they hold a dim luminescence that intensifies under magical exertion or strong emotion — present when active, absent at rest. The negative image of a standard Elf's steady glow.

**Culture:** Dark Elf cultures vary considerably by region and history, but the most consistent throughline is institutional wariness. Communities that survived long-term separation from the Trees typically developed strong internal cohesion and significant caution toward external authorities — including MOD. Dark Elf settlements tend toward defensible, low-profile locations: underground networks, deep forest margins, dimensional border zones. They do not universally seek conflict but have historically operated in spaces where institutional oversight is thin.

**Magical Affinity:** The loss of Tree of Life connection and the environments Dark Elves subsequently occupied produced a shift in magical affinity over generations. Where Elves naturally tend toward nature enchantment, Elven healing, and perceptive magic, Dark Elves more frequently develop ability in Umbraturgy, shadow-adjacent schools, and dimensional manipulation. Gaze manifestations in Dark Elf hunters tend toward Binding, Void, Spirits, and Darkness-adjacent variations — documented as a statistical trend in Assessment Unit data, not a universal trait.

**In Hyperion:** Dark Elves who engage with MOD structures most commonly do so through the Umbra Shroud. The guild's invite-only recruitment, operational secrecy, and specialization in threat categories where Dark Elf abilities are most applicable make the affiliation practical. Dark Elves who register with MOD directly do so at below-average rates; the Assessment Unit's files on Dark Elf Gaze bearer incidence are acknowledged internally as incomplete.

*→ `world/Races.md` — Elf; Astrae | `organizations/Organizations.md` — The Umbra Shroud | `systems/Arcane.md` — Umbraturgy | `systems/Gazes.md` — Gaze Registration*

### Vampire

Vampires are an immortal race sustained by consuming blood, which grants them enhanced strength, speed, regeneration, and other combat-relevant properties. They can consume normal food but derive no power from it. Their society is structured around three great Houses, each representing a distinct approach to the vampire condition.

**House Noctaris** functions as a manipulative aristocracy — patient, politically entrenched, preferring to rule through economic and informational leverage rather than direct force. They have infiltrated political and corporate structures across Hyperion over centuries and are the most institutionally opaque of the three houses.

**House Morvayne** builds and maintains power through demonstrated force. They take contracts, hold territory, and make their presence known. The Third Era Dual Sigil Incident involved a Morvayne vassal — the only confirmed case of dual-Sigil coexistence in documented history. Valdren Ashcrast declined a Morvayne contract offer following the Siege of Irenmere Pass. Both records suggest Morvayne actively acquires exceptional individuals. *→ `world/History.md` — The Dual Sigil Incident; Siege of Irenmere Pass*

**House Dravenmore** are mystic scholars, focused on understanding and perfecting the vampire condition through research. Their genealogical registry of Sigil bearers is the most complete private record in existence. Archon Dravenmore — the preeminent Spatial Magic practitioner of the Seventh Era, architect of the Hexline System, and the only individual to hold a lifetime MOD contract — was a Dravenmore vampire. Their institutional relationship with MOD's Assessment Unit, though not in any public document, has persisted across multiple eras. *→ `organizations/Organizations.md` — House Dravenmore | `world/History.md` — Archon Dravenmore: Hexline System Architecture*

Vampire immortality interacts with the Sigil and Gaze manifestation timeline in ways that are not fully understood. The strain threshold for Sigil awakening may compound differently across centuries than across decades. House Dravenmore's research on this question is not published. *→ `systems/Sigils.md` — Sigil Strain | `systems/Power-Sources.md` — Physical Source*

### Arcanian

Arcanians are humans who possess the innate ability to use magic. Physically indistinguishable from baseline Humans, their talent sets them apart and allows them to compete in a world filled with magical threats and wonders. They are the primary practitioners of magic within human society and are often found in guilds, military units, or as independent mages-for-hire.

Within MOD's Arcane Registry of approximately 12,400 registered practitioners, Arcanians constitute the largest single racial category. Unlike some long-lived races, Arcanian practitioners operate within a standard human lifespan, producing higher institutional turnover and, historically, more diverse innovation in applied magic. Healer Cassian — whose field healing protocols remain the foundation of MOD emergency response medical procedures — is documented in Arcanian practitioner records. *→ `systems/Arcane.md` — MOD Arcane Registry Overview | `world/History.md` — Healer Cassian of the Ashenmoor*

### Kitsune

Kitsune are a charismatic race native to the wilds of Hyperion, resembling Humans but with distinctive fluffy animal ears. They are free-spirited, affectionate, and possess natural pheromones that make them highly alluring, especially to Humans. They live in harmony with nature, valuing pleasure and freedom over strict tradition. Their interaction with Humans has grown since Hyperion became a travel destination, leading to cultural exchange and occasionally complicated formal arrangements.

Kitsune register with MOD at standard rates but rarely pursue Dungeon-focused hunting as a primary career. They appear more commonly in support, intelligence, and civilian-adjacent contract roles. Their natural affinity for social perception and emotional calibration — occasionally flagged as Gaze-adjacent by the Assessment Unit during registration review — makes them effective in contexts that do not require direct monster engagement.

### Human

Humans are the technologically adept race of Hyperion, lacking innate magic but mastering science and engineering to an unmatched degree. Their crowning achievement is Techno-Magic, a fusion of technology and arcane energy that powers their cities, vehicles, and weapons. Their ambition, adaptability, and innovative spirit have made them a major political and economic force, though they often rely on magical races like Arcanians or Angels to deal with supernatural threats.

By raw numbers, Humans constitute the largest registered hunter population in MOD's Registry. The Techno-Magic advantage equalizes capability gaps against magically superior races in many engagement scenarios: well-equipped Human hunters regularly operate at ranks that pure biological assessment would not predict. Their shorter lifespans compared to most other races produce a distinctive career arc — faster advancement windows and a cultural relationship with risk that diverges significantly from longer-lived races.
