# Layer -4: The Abyssal Magic Layer

Layer -4 — the Abyssal Magic Layer — is the fourth stratum beneath the material universe, and the deepest layer whose existence is documented in any form. The documentation is not first-hand. No being has descended to Layer -4 and returned to describe what they found. The descent requires crossing Layer -1's magical frontier, surviving Layer -2's gothic horrors, traversing Layer -3's primal desert, walking through the Lord of Brutality's Graveyard, and passing through the gate beyond it — and the return requires doing all of it in reverse. The journey is beyond the reach of regular beings. The return is beyond the reach of anyone. What is known about Layer -4 comes from inference, from the logic of the layered cosmos, and from the few travelers who reached the layer's threshold and sent back fragmentary reports before descending and not returning. The reports are inconsistent. The reports contradict each other. The contradictions may be the most reliable information the reports contain.

The magic of Layer -4 is abyssal — magic concentrated past the point of coherence. Where Layer -3's primal magic was wild but still followed rules (it was hot, it was red, it acted like energy), abyssal magic has been compressed past the threshold where rules hold. The magic is so dense that it has begun to break the structures that would otherwise contain it, and the breaking is the layer. Reality on Layer -4 does not function as reality functions on the layers above. Form is not reliable. Structure is not stable. Logic is not consistent. The layer is not hostile in the way Layer -3 was hostile — there are no predators, no warriors, no juggernauts seeking to destroy travelers. The layer is hostile in the way that a broken machine is hostile: it does not intend harm, it does not pursue harm, it simply does not function in the way that the traveler's body and mind require, and the malfunction is the danger.

The layer's color is the color of reality breaking. The base is black — the deep, lightless black of absolute depth, the black of a space that has never been illuminated because illumination would require rules the layer does not maintain. Against the black, iridescent shifts move — colors that do not hold, that slide across the spectrum without settling, that appear and disappear and reappear in configurations that do not correspond to any fixed palette. The iridescence is the magic visible — the abyssal concentration made perceptible, shimmering at the edges of perception like oil on water or the surface of a bubble just before it pops. The combination of black and shifting iridescence produces a visual environment that is disorienting in a fundamental way: the eye cannot fix on a stable reference point, because there are no stable reference points, and the attempt to find one produces a sensation that travelers have described as "the ground moving under a floor that is also moving." The layer does not look the way the layers above look. The layer looks the way the layers above would look if looking itself stopped working.

The layer has no inhabitants. There are no tribes, no cultists, no servants, no populations adapted to the broken reality. There are only the three lords — Distortion, Unstability, and Paradox — and the three lords are the layer's only permanent presence. The emptiness is not an accident of the environment; it is a property of the layer. Layer -4 is not meant to be lived in. It is meant to *be*, and the lords keep it being, and anything that enters is simply not part of the equation. A traveler who descends to Layer -4 enters a place that was not designed for travelers, that does not accommodate travelers, and that does not acknowledge travelers except in the specific ways the lords' natures require. The emptiness is part of the horror. The silence is part of the horror. The knowledge that the only things present are the three custodians and the broken reality they maintain — that is the horror.

## The Texture of the Layer

The Abyssal Magic Layer does not have a consistent geography in the way the layers above have consistent geography. The layer's terrain is a function of which type of breakdown dominates in a given region, and the breakdowns do not hold still. A traveler moving through the layer passes through regions where form is unreliable, regions where structure does not hold, and regions where logic itself has failed — and the regions shift, merge, and separate as the abyssal magic moves through its cycles. The layer is not a landscape; it is a process, and the process is the breakdown, and the breakdown is the layer.

**Distortion regions** are regions where form is wrong. Things are present, but they are not what they appear to be. A wall that looks solid is not. A path that looks straight curves. A structure that looks intact is rubble, and a structure that looks like rubble is intact. The distortion is internally consistent — within a distortion region, the lies the region tells about itself hold together, and a traveler who learns the region's internal logic can navigate it. But the internal logic contradicts the actual forms, and a traveler who relies on perception without testing it will find that perception has led them somewhere perception said was not there. Distortion regions are the most navigable of the three breakdown types, which makes them the most dangerous in one specific way: travelers who enter them believe they are safe, because the distortion is not obviously threatening. The danger is not in what the distortion does; the danger is in what the traveler does because they believed the distortion.

**Unstability regions** are regions where structure does not hold. Things that should stay where they are do not. Ground shifts underfoot. Architecture rearranges itself when not observed, and sometimes when observed. Distances change — a corridor that was ten meters long is suddenly fifty, or two, or a length that cannot be measured because the concept of measurement requires a stability the region does not provide. The instability is not random; it follows patterns, but the patterns are in constant motion, and a traveler who learns a pattern in one moment finds it has changed in the next. Unstability regions are more obviously dangerous than distortion regions — the breakdown is visible, the threat is apparent — but they are also, in a paradoxical way, easier to survive, because the visible danger forces the traveler to remain alert, and alert travelers make fewer assumptions. The danger in unstability regions is not the shifting itself; the danger is the moment when the shifting stops, because a traveler who has adapted to constant change will assume the stop is permanent, and it is not.

**Paradox regions** are regions where logic has failed. Cause and effect disconnect. A thing can be present and absent simultaneously. Forward can also be backward. The same action can produce opposite results. Time, space, and identity — the foundational categories that even unstability regions assume — cease to function in any reliable way. Paradox regions are the deepest, hardest, and most dangerous regions of the layer, and they are also the rarest. The paradox breakdown is not continuous; it occurs in patches, and the patches are small, and a traveler who enters a paradox patch may not realize they have entered it until they attempt to leave and discover that "leaving" has stopped meaning what it meant. Paradox regions cannot be navigated, because navigation requires a navigator and a destination, and both require a logic that the region does not provide. The only way to survive a paradox region is to not enter it, and the only way to not enter it is to recognize it before arrival, and recognition requires perceiving the impossible, which most beings cannot do.

The three regions do not have clean boundaries. They shade into one another, and the shading is itself a fourth type of breakdown — a region where two or three types of failure overlap, producing effects that are genuinely difficult to describe because the language to describe them assumes the rules that have failed. A traveler in an overlap region may experience distortion and unstability simultaneously — forms that are wrong and that also do not hold — or unstability and paradox simultaneously — structures that shift and that also disconnect from cause. The overlap regions are the layer's most hostile territory, and they are also where the lords are most likely to be found, because the lords' work is concentrated where the breakdowns are most severe.

## The Three Lords of Layer -4

Layer -4 is governed by three lords, each embodying a fundamental aspect of the layer's broken reality. The three are: the Lord of Distortion, the weakest; the Lord of Unstability; and the Lord of Paradox, the strongest of the three. The power gradient runs from distortion — the breakdown of form — through unstability — the breakdown of structure — to paradox — the breakdown of logic. Each is a deeper level of reality failing, and the strongest lord is the one whose embodiment targets the most fundamental category.

The three lords are not like the lords of the layers above. They are not predators, warriors, or juggernauts. They are custodians. Their task is to uphold the layer together — to keep the breakdown contained, to prevent the abyssal concentration from expanding past the layer's boundaries, to maintain the layer in a state of functional brokenness that does not consume the layers above. They are not hostile to travelers. They do not pursue travelers. They do not engage travelers unless the traveler's presence threatens the layer's stability, which it rarely does, because travelers are insignificant next to the forces the lords manage. The lords are simply present, doing their work, and the work is the maintenance of a reality that destroys travelers as a side effect of its normal operation.

The lords are irreplaceable. This is not a property of deterrents or difficulty, as on the layers above; it is a categorical property of their nature. The lords of Layer -4 are not solid beings. They are shimmering, translucent, composed of liquid rather than solid matter — their forms are not fixed in the way that the forms of the layers above are fixed. The lord system's succession mechanic — kill the lord, inherit the embodiment — assumes that the lord can be killed, and killing requires a body that can be ended. The lords of Layer -4 do not have bodies that can be ended. They have forms, but the forms are expressions of the embodiment rather than containers for it, and ending the form does not end the embodiment. The "always must be a lord" rule is satisfied by their permanence rather than by their succession. They have always been, and they will always be, and the layer has never known a different lord and never will.

The three lords do not maintain diplomatic relationships in any conventional sense. They do not communicate with each other through speech or messengers. They communicate through the layer itself — through the balance of breakdowns they maintain, through the adjustments each makes to compensate for the others' work, through the silent coordination of three custodians who have been doing the same job for longer than the layers above have existed. The coordination is not negotiated; it is *embodied*. The Lord of Distortion distorts what the Lord of Unstability cannot hold; the Lord of Unstability shifts what the Lord of Paradox cannot resolve; the Lord of Paradox contradicts what the other two cannot address. The three are a system, and the system is the layer, and the layer is the system, and the lords are the system's organs.

### The Lord of Distortion

**Embodiment:** Distortion
**Lair:** None fixed — the Lord of Distortion moves through the distortion regions of the layer, and the regions move with him
**Current Bearer:** The original bearer — has never been replaced (irreplaceable by nature)
**Traditional Weapons:** None — the Lord of Distortion does not fight; he distorts
**Kingdom:** The distortion regions, and the overlap regions where distortion dominates
**Colloquial Name:** None (the layer has no inhabitants to name him)

The Lord of Distortion is the embodiment of distortion — the breakdown of form, the failure of what-is to be what-it-appears-to-be. He is the weakest of the three Layer -4 lords, but "weakest" on Layer -4 is a relative term in a category where all three lords are categorically irreplaceable. His weakness is the weakness of the shallowest breakdown: form is the most superficial of the three categories (form, structure, logic), and the lord who embodies its failure is the lord whose work is closest to the surface. The Lord of Unstability breaks what holds form together; the Lord of Paradox breaks what holds logic together. The Lord of Distortion breaks form itself, and form is the first thing to go.

The Lord of Distortion's true form is shimmering, translucent, composed of liquid rather than solid matter — a form that does not hold a single shape, that shifts at the edges of perception, that is present and absent in the same moment. The Lord of Distortion can, however, distort his own form — and this is his power, and the source of his ability to interact with travelers. He can distort his liquid, shimmering true form into any shape he chooses: a human figure, a beast, a structure, a landscape. The shape he takes is not his form; it is a distortion of his form, and the distortion is itself an expression of his embodiment. When the Lord of Distortion appears as a human, he is not becoming human; he is distorting himself into the appearance of human, and the appearance is a lie, and the lie is his nature.

The Lord of Distortion's work is the maintenance of distortion — the keeping of form-breaking within the bounds the layer requires. He moves through the distortion regions, and the regions move with him, and his presence ensures that the distortions do not expand past their assigned territories or intensify past the layer's tolerances. He is the custodian of the breakdown, and the breakdown is what he is, and what he is is what he custodies. The work is constant, because distortion is constant, and the lord does not rest because the breakdown does not rest.

The Lord of Distortion is not safe to approach. His embodiment is not hostile — he does not pursue travelers, he does not intend harm — but his presence distorts reality around him passively, and the distortion affects anything in his vicinity regardless of his intent. A traveler who approaches the Lord of Distortion finds that their own form begins to distort — their perception of their body shifts, their sense of their own shape becomes unreliable, the boundary between self and environment blurs. The effect is gradual, proportional to proximity, and reversible if the traveler withdraws in time. A traveler who does not withdraw — who approaches too closely, or who lingers too long — finds that the distortion has progressed past the point of reversal, and the traveler's form has become a distortion, and the traveler is no longer what they were. The Lord of Distortion does not do this to them. The Lord of Distortion's presence does this to them. The distinction is irrelevant to the outcome.

The Lord of Distortion is the lord travelers are most likely to seek out, because he is the weakest, and because he can take human form, and because human form suggests the possibility of communication. Travelers who reach Layer -4 and survive long enough to think strategically will reason that the weakest lord is the safest to approach, and that a lord who can appear human may be willing to speak. The reasoning is understandable. The reasoning is also wrong. The Lord of Distortion's human form is a distortion, not an invitation; the form does not make him safe to approach, it makes him dangerous in a different way, because the form is a lie, and the lie is the embodiment, and the embodiment breaks form, and the traveler's form is what breaks. The trap is not malicious. The trap is the nature of the lord, and the nature of the lord is the nature of the layer, and the layer does not accommodate travelers.

### The Lord of Unstability

**Embodiment:** Unstability
**Lair:** None fixed — the Lord of Unstability moves through the unstability regions of the layer, and the regions shift as he shifts
**Current Bearer:** The original bearer — has never been replaced (irreplaceable by nature)
**Traditional Weapons:** None — the Lord of Unstability does not fight; he shifts
**Kingdom:** The unstability regions, and the overlap regions where unstability dominates
**Colloquial Name:** None (the layer has no inhabitants to name him)

The Lord of Unstability is the embodiment of unstability — the breakdown of structure, the failure of what-should-hold to hold. He is the middle lord of Layer -4, and his embodiment is what distortion becomes when distortion deepens: form not merely wrong but actively failing, structure not merely misleading but genuinely not maintaining. Where the Lord of Distortion breaks the appearance of form, the Lord of Unstability breaks the substance of structure, and the substance is what holds things where they are, and without it, nothing holds.

The Lord of Unstability's true form is unstable. This is not a description of his form's appearance; it is a description of his form's nature. The Lord of Unstability does not have a form that shifts — he has a form that *is* shifting, and the distinction is the distinction between a thing that moves and a thing that is motion. His form is shimmering, translucent, liquid, like the other two lords, but unlike the Lord of Distortion (who can stabilize a distorted shape) and the Lord of Paradox (who can contradict his form into stability), the Lord of Unstability cannot stabilize. His form is always in flux. His form has never not been in flux. His form will never not be in flux. The instability is not something he does; it is something he is, and he cannot be what he is not, and what he is is unstable.

The Lord of Unstability's work is the maintenance of unstability — the keeping of structure-breaking within the bounds the layer requires. He moves through the unstability regions, and the regions shift as he shifts, and his presence ensures that the instability does not expand past its assigned territories or intensify past the layer's tolerances. The work is, in a sense, self-perpetuating: the lord's presence produces instability, and the instability requires management, and the management is the lord's work, and the lord's work produces the instability. The loop is not a problem to be solved; the loop is the system, and the system is functional, and functional is what the layer requires.

The Lord of Unstability is not safe to approach. His embodiment, like the Lord of Distortion's, is not hostile — he does not pursue travelers, he does not intend harm — but his presence destabilizes reality around him passively, and the destabilization affects anything in his vicinity regardless of his intent. A traveler who approaches the Lord of Unstability finds that the ground beneath them shifts, that the air around them does not hold a consistent pressure, that their own body's stability becomes unreliable — limbs that should be solid become fluid, balance that should be constant becomes intermittent, the boundary between motion and stillness blurs until the traveler cannot tell whether they are moving or the world is moving around them. The effect is more immediately disorienting than the Lord of Distortion's passive distortion, because structure is more fundamental than form, and the loss of structure is harder to adapt to than the loss of form. A traveler who lingers near the Lord of Unstability does not gradually distort; they gradually destabilize, and destabilization, past a certain point, is not reversible, because there is no stable state to return to.

The Lord of Unstability cannot take human form. He cannot take any fixed form. This makes him, in one sense, the most visibly alien of the three lords — a traveler who encounters him sees something that is clearly not a being in any conventional sense, a shimmering, shifting, liquid presence that cannot hold a shape long enough to be recognized as a shape. Travelers who encounter the Lord of Unstability typically do not approach; the visible instability is sufficient warning, and the few who have approached have not returned with reports that any institution considers coherent. The Lord of Unstability does not communicate. He does not speak. He does not signal. He shifts, and the shifting is his presence, and his presence is his work, and his work is the maintenance of the breakdown that he is.

### The Lord of Paradox

**Embodiment:** Paradox
**Lair:** None fixed — the Lord of Paradox moves through the paradox regions of the layer, and the regions exist where he is
**Current Bearer:** The original bearer — has never been replaced (irreplaceable by nature)
**Traditional Weapons:** None — the Lord of Paradox does not fight; he contradicts
**Kingdom:** The paradox regions, and the overlap regions where paradox dominates
**Colloquial Name:** None (the layer has no inhabitants to name him)

The Lord of Paradox is the embodiment of paradox — the breakdown of logic, the failure of what-should-be-possible to be possible and what-should-be-impossible to be impossible. He is the strongest of the three Layer -4 lords, and his embodiment is the deepest of the three breakdowns: form is the most superficial (distortion), structure is more fundamental (unstability), and logic is the foundation on which both form and structure depend (paradox). The Lord of Paradox breaks the category that the other two categories rely on, and this makes him the strongest in the same way that undermining a foundation makes a structure fall.

The Lord of Paradox's true form is shimmering, translucent, composed of liquid rather than solid matter — the same baseline form as the other two lords. His power, however, is the power to make contradictions real, and this power applies to himself as much as to anything else. The Lord of Paradox can contradict his own nature. His nature is to be a shimmering, translucent, liquid being; he can contradict this by appearing as a solid human form, and the appearance is real, because the contradiction is real, and the contradiction is his power. His nature is to be unable to speak (speaking requires choosing one meaning over another, and choosing is the one thing paradox cannot do); he can contradict this by speaking, and the speech is real, because the contradiction is real. His nature is to be a custodian who does not interfere with travelers; he can contradict this by guiding them, and the guidance is real, because the contradiction is real.

The Lord of Paradox's power is, in principle, unlimited within the scope of contradiction. Anything that would be a contradiction of his nature is something he can do, because the contradiction is the precondition for the act. If it should be possible, it is not a paradox, and his power does not apply. If it should be impossible, it is a paradox, and his power makes it real. The range of "things that would contradict his nature" is potentially infinite, and this is what makes him the strongest lord of Layer -4: his power has no fixed limit, because the limits are defined by what his nature is, and his nature is a paradox, and a paradox has no fixed definition.

The Lord of Paradox's work is the maintenance of paradox — the keeping of logic-breaking within the bounds the layer requires. He moves through the paradox regions, and the regions exist where he is, and his presence ensures that the paradoxes do not expand past their assigned territories or intensify past the layer's tolerances. The work is the most demanding of the three, because paradox is the most fundamental breakdown, and the management of paradox requires the most active custodianship. The Lord of Paradox is, in this sense, the busiest of the three lords, and the most essential to the layer's continued existence.

The Lord of Paradox is the only one of the three lords who is safe to approach. His power allows him to choose what to contradict, and he can choose to contradict the passive breakdown that the other two lords radiate. Near the Lord of Paradox, reality is — paradoxically — stable, because he contradicts the instability that would otherwise be present. A traveler who reaches the Lord of Paradox finds, for the first time since entering Layer -4, a region where form holds, where structure maintains, where logic functions. The stability is not the lord's nature; the stability is a contradiction of the lord's nature, and the contradiction is his choice, and his choice is the reason travelers survive his presence.

The Lord of Paradox can speak, and he can guide, and he can take human form, and he does these things by contradicting the nature that would otherwise prevent them. He is the strongest lord, and he is the safest to approach, and no traveler who reaches Layer -4 expects this, because no traveler expects the strongest lord of a reality-breaking layer to be the one who can make himself safe. The trap of Layer -4 is that travelers will seek out the weakest lord (Distortion) expecting safety, and the weakest lord is not safe, and the strongest lord (Paradox) is safe, and the strongest lord does not advertise. The Lord of Paradox does not seek travelers. He does not call out. He does not post signs. He is simply present, in the paradox regions, doing his work, and a traveler who finds him has found the only point of stability in a layer that does not provide stability, and the finding is the traveler's own.

The Lord of Paradox is sane. This is the property that makes him, in some ways, the most unsettling of the three. The Lord of Distortion distorts; the Lord of Unstability shifts; both are, in their ways, expressions of breakdown, and a traveler can understand them as forces even if the traveler cannot survive them. The Lord of Paradox is a person — he perceives, he chooses, he contradicts deliberately. He could, if he chose, contradict his sanity into insanity, or contradict his stability into instability, or contradict his custodianship into destruction. He does not. He chooses, consistently, to maintain the layer, to manage the paradoxes, to be the stable point in a layer that has no other stable points. Whether this choice is a property of the embodiment or a property of the bearer is not a question that can be answered, and the Lord of Paradox, if asked, might answer and might not, because answering is a contradiction he can choose to make or not make, and the choice is his.

## The Passage to Layer -5

The passage to Layer -5 is somewhere in Layer -4. The specific location is not documented. The passage is not guarded, because the lords of Layer -4 are custodians, not guards, and their work is the maintenance of the layer rather than the prevention of descent. The passage is not marked, because the layer does not accommodate travelers and does not provide directions. The passage is simply present, somewhere in the broken reality, and a traveler who wishes to find it must navigate distortion, unstability, and paradox without guidance, without stable reference points, and without the assurance that the passage they find is the passage they seek rather than a distortion of the passage, an unstable manifestation of the passage, or a paradox that appears to be a passage but is not.

The difficulty of finding the passage is compounded by the layer's arrival point. Travelers who descend from Layer -3 arrive at the center of Layer -4 — equidistant from all three lords' regions, in the overlap zone where all three breakdown types meet. The arrival point is, by the standards of the layer, relatively stable, but "relatively stable" on Layer -4 means "the breakdown is present but not immediately lethal," and the stability does not extend in any direction. A traveler who moves from the center in any direction enters one of the three breakdown regions, and the direction they choose determines which lord's territory they enter, and the lord they enter is the lord whose passive breakdown they must survive. There is no direction that is safe. There is no direction that is clearly correct. The passage to Layer -5 is somewhere beyond one of the three regions, and which region it is beyond is not known, and the not-knowing is the layer's final defense.

The Lord of Paradox, if a traveler reaches him and if he chooses to speak, may provide information about the passage. He is not required to. He is not inclined to. His work is the layer, not the travelers, and the travelers are not his concern. But he can contradict his non-concern into concern, and he can contradict his silence into speech, and if he does, the information he provides will be true — because truth is a contradiction of his paradoxical nature, and the contradiction is within his power. Whether a traveler who reaches the Lord of Paradox will receive guidance is not knowable in advance. The Lord of Paradox chooses, and the choice is his, and the choice is a contradiction, and the contradiction is real.

The passage to Layer -5, when found, is not documented. No traveler who has reached it has returned with a description of what they found. Whether the passage is a gate, a doorway, a descent, a contradiction, or something that cannot be described in the terms the layers above provide is not known. The passage is there. The passage is open. The passage leads to Layer -5, and Layer -5 is not something the layer above is prepared to receive, and the broken reality of Layer -4 is the last documented layer, and beyond it the cosmos ceases to be documented, and the descent continues, and the return, as always, is the barrier.

## Layer -4 in Summary

Layer -4 is the layer of broken reality. It is the layer where magic has been concentrated past the point of coherence, and the concentration has broken the structures that would otherwise contain it, and the breaking is the layer. The layer's color is black against iridescent shifts — the color of reality breaking, the color of magic visible at the edge of perception, the color of a space that does not maintain the rules that color requires. The layer has no inhabitants. The layer has no civilization. The layer has no trade, no tournaments, no challenges, no social institutions. The layer has only the three lords — Distortion, Unstability, and Paradox — and the three lords are the layer's only permanent presence, and their work is the maintenance of the broken reality they embody.

The three lords are not hostile. They are custodians. Their task is to uphold the layer together — to keep the breakdown contained, to prevent the abyssal concentration from expanding, to maintain the layer in a state of functional brokenness that does not consume the layers above. They do not pursue travelers. They do not intend harm. They are simply present, doing their work, and the work is the maintenance of a reality that destroys travelers as a side effect of its normal operation. The danger of Layer -4 is not malice; the danger is the environment being maintained as intended.

The three lords are irreplaceable. They are not solid beings; they are shimmering, translucent, composed of liquid rather than solid matter, and their forms are expressions of the embodiment rather than containers for it. The lord system's succession mechanic — kill the lord, inherit the embodiment — does not apply, because the lords cannot be killed in any conventional sense. They have always been, and they will always be, and the layer has never known a different lord and never will. The "always must be a lord" rule is satisfied by their permanence rather than by their succession.

The power gradient runs from the Lord of Distortion, the weakest, through the Lord of Unstability, to the Lord of Paradox, the strongest. Each embodies a deeper level of reality failing: form breaks (distortion), structure breaks (unstability), logic breaks (paradox). The strongest lord is the one whose embodiment targets the most fundamental category, and the strongest lord is, paradoxically, the safest to approach — because his power allows him to contradict his own breakdown into stability, and he chooses to do so, and the choice makes him the only point of stability in a layer that does not provide stability. The trap of Layer -4 is that travelers will seek out the weakest lord expecting safety, and the weakest lord is not safe, and the strongest lord is safe, and the strongest lord does not advertise.

The passage to Layer -5 is somewhere in the broken reality, unguarded, unmarked, and unreachable without navigating the breakdowns. The Lord of Paradox, if reached and if he chooses, may provide guidance, but the choice is his, and the choice is a contradiction, and the contradiction is real. The layer does not invite the descent. The layer does not prevent the descent. The layer simply is, broken and maintained, and the three lords custodate it, and the passage waits, and the descent continues, and the return, as always, is the barrier.
