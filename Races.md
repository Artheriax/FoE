Races

Entity Zero

Entity Zero is the ultimate mystery, a being whispered about in the most
ancient and forbidden texts. It is speculated to be the origin of all
things, existing outside reality, time, and space itself. It is not
bound by any law of existence, its form beyond comprehension. Some
believe it holds the secret to creation and destruction, the key to the
universal balance. To most, it is a myth, a concept dismissed in favor
of the more established hierarchy of The One. Yet, persistent rumors
suggest that Entity Zero\'s eventual revelation will shift the balance
of the universe irrevocably. It is the original force, existing both
within and without all of creation.

The Higher Ones

The One

The One is perceived as the confluence of all other Higher Ones, a
singular entity possessing absolute control over every form of cosmic
power. It is seen by many as the supreme being. However, those who
believe in Entity Zero see The One as a lesser, though still immense,
power. A common criticism among cosmic scholars is that The One is
inherently selfish, for it possesses ultimate power yet is never known
to act, remaining an passive, observing totality.

The Outer One

The Outer One is akin to The One but is defined by its domain: the
spaces outside the known universe. It is alien and incomprehensible,
feeling most at home in the boundless, terrifying voids between
realities. It is widely credited with the creation of the Aether, and by
extension, the Aetherians and Aetherions. Its existence is debated, as
its nature places it fundamentally beyond mortal perception and
understanding.

The Destroying One

The Destroying One is the embodiment of annihilation, a force of
unparalleled devastation tied to the end of matter, energy, and reality.
It is not inherently malevolent; its function is a necessary part of the
cosmic cycle. It destroys to pave the way for new creation, acting as a
cosmic reset to prevent stagnation. Some believe it destroys for its own
sake, while others argue it is an impartial force of nature, acting only
when the flow of the universe demands it. Its power can erase worlds,
histories, and memories with a mere thought.

The Creating One

The Creating One is the counterpart to the Destroying One, embodying
genesis, growth, and the manifestation of new realities. It is the
source of all creation, breathing life into the void and forming
galaxies, species, and laws of magic. It is often viewed through two
lenses: as a benevolent, nurturing force, or as an indifferent cosmic
function that creates without personal investment. It fosters the birth
of civilizations and the awakening of new possibilities, ensuring the
universe is in a constant state of becoming.

The Judging One

The Judging One is the cosmic arbiter of law, often depicted as a grim,
unwavering executioner. It does not concern itself with petty mortal
conflicts but enforces fundamental cosmic law. It judges those who
disrupt the universal order---through forbidden magic, reality-warping,
or defying the balance---and delivers absolute verdicts. Its punishments
are tailored to the crime and may be carried out by itself or entrusted
to enforcers like the Eternal Guards. It is feared and revered, for its
judgment is inescapable and its sentence, final.

The Balancing One

The Balancing One is the enigmatic right hand of the Judging One. Where
the Judging One determines guilt, the Balancing One ensures equilibrium
is restored. Its methods are fluid and mysterious, as balance is not
always synonymous with equality. It might ensure no single force
dominates by fostering an opposing force, or it might subtly influence
fate to correct imbalances. It does not seek fairness, but rather the
maintenance of a tense, dynamic equilibrium. It is the silent architect
who ensures the universe never tips too far into chaos or order.

The Intervening One

The Intervening One is a being of unpredictable will. It does not weave
fate or guide events, but instead watches until the most pivotal moment
in time---then chooses whether to step in and alter the course of
history or remain a silent observer. Its interventions are rare,
monumental, and governed by a logic beyond mortal comprehension. It is
one of the most feared Higher Ones, for its actions are entirely its own
and cannot be predicted, reasoned with, or defied.

The Observing One (Phariah)

The Observing One, also known as Phariah, is the ultimate historian. It
exists to watch, record, and collect all knowledge without ever
intervening or allowing itself to be seen. Legends say it was once a
powerful being that defied cosmic law and was punished by the Judging
One to forever observe without acting. It is the silent eyes of the
higher forces, a passive recorder of all that has ever happened. Some
believe it decides what is remembered and what is erased from reality
itself.

The Nonexistent One

The Nonexistent One is a paradox---a being that both exists and does
not. No one has ever seen it, spoken to it, or proven its existence, yet
its name appears in ancient texts across dimensions. It is theorized to
reside in Nihility, the dimension of nothingness, or to be the remnant
of a being that was utterly erased, leaving only an absence behind. To
study it is considered dangerous, as one risks being similarly erased
from history and memory.

The Forgotten One

Nothing is known of The Forgotten One, for it has been utterly erased
from all memory and record. Its name is the only evidence it ever
existed. Perhaps the other Higher Ones know its fate, but they do not
speak of it. Its existence serves as a silent testament to the ultimate
power of oblivion.

The Nameless One

The Nameless One is a being without identity. It has no fixed name,
form, or presence. It is said to wander the cosmos, shifting its shape
and stealing the appearances of others. Those who encounter it struggle
to recall what they saw, and details fade immediately. The greatest
mystery is its lack of a name; to name something is to define it, and
the Nameless One remains unbound. Some believe it was stripped of its
name as punishment, while others think it never had one, existing as a
flaw in reality itself. It is a shadow of forgotten truths, forever
searching for an identity it cannot possess.

Detailed Race Descriptions

Eternarc

The Eternarcs are the first confirmed beings outside the enigmatic
Higher Ones, acting as cosmic overseers and regulators. They are not
abstract concepts like their superiors but tangible, recorded entities
who enforce the cosmic law. Their primary role is to maintain order
among the immensely powerful races below them, such as the Primordials
and Celestials, preventing their power from destabilizing existence.
Eternarcs are known to act as judges, wardens, and keepers of forbidden
knowledge. They are the architects of cosmic stability, answering only
to the Higher Ones like the Judging One, and are the ones who decree the
title of Nebular Tyrant upon those who defy the universal order.

Nebular Tyrant

A Nebular Tyrant is not a natural race but a title of condemnation
bestowed by the Eternarcs. It marks a being---once among the most
powerful in existence---that has fallen to utter corruption, greed, or a
desire for absolute dominion. Once declared, they are exiled from the
cosmic order, stripped of divine rights, and hunted for eternity by the
Eternal Guards. Their very presence warps reality, bringing chaos and
misfortune. Nebular Tyrants like Xal\'Zorrath the Cosmic Ruin or Vaelgor
the Abyss Monarch are beings of immense power now dedicated solely to
their own destructive ambitions, serving as dark warnings of the price
of unchecked ambition.

Eternal Guard

The Eternal Guards are an elite force of cosmic enforcers, created by
the Eternarcs to hunt Nebular Tyrants and uphold universal law. They are
relentless, unyielding warriors who cannot be truly killed; if
destroyed, a new one is reborn from cosmic energy. Wielding Unmaking
Blades that can erase a being from reality itself, they can traverse
dimensions instantly and are governed by a strict hierarchy led by the
Arbiter of Judgment. They are both revered as holy protectors and feared
as ultimate executioners, representing the absolute and impartial
justice of the cosmos.

Ascendant

Ascendants are beings of limitless potential who exist outside the
control of any other entity. They begin their existence as members of a
standard race, with no outward signs of their true nature. Unlike
others, they do not have a maximum potential form; they continuously
grow stronger, forever pushing beyond known limits. Their
path is unpredictable and incredibly rare, making them wild cards in the
cosmic balance. Some believe an Ascendant may one day challenge or even
surpass the Eternarcs themselves.

Primordial

Primordials are the fundamental forces of the universe given form. They
are not gods of specific things but embodiments of vast,
all-encompassing concepts like Time, Creation, Destruction, or Life and
Death itself. They are the backbone of reality, shaping the very laws of
existence. While immensely powerful, they are subservient to the
Eternarcs, who ensure their actions do not disrupt the cosmic balance.
Their interaction with mortals is rare, as their influence is too vast
to comprehend, felt in the turning of ages and the birth and death of
galaxies rather than in direct intervention.

Celestial

Celestials are the traditional, accessible gods of the universe. Each
governs a specific aspect of existence, such as War, Love, Death, or
Nature, and resides in a personal realm that reflects their domain
(e.g., Luminara for Light). They are highly involved in mortal affairs,
accepting worship, bestowing blessings, and intervening directly in
times of great need. While powerful, they are far below the Primordials
in scope and answer to the broader cosmic structure maintained by the
Eternarcs. They are the most common point of contact for mortals seeking
divine favor.

Nephryx

The Nephryx are benevolent, peaceful spiritual beings. They exist as
ethereal presences rather than physical forms, radiating calm and
serenity. Their role is to guide and heal those deemed worthy, offering
wisdom and comfort during times of emotional or spiritual turmoil. They
are the antithesis of the Maltheryx, representing the gentle, healing
side of the spirit world and often serving as unseen mentors to those
with pure hearts.

Maltheryx

Maltheryx are rare and dangerous spirits born from immense, unresolved
hatred and a thirst for vengeance. They are the dark counterpart to the
Nephryx. These vengeful spirits manifest in the physical world to
torment and destroy those they believe wronged them, often through
haunting, possession, or spreading corruption. They are creatures of
pure malice, difficult to control or destroy, and represent the
catastrophic consequences of hatred that persists beyond death.

Seraphon

Seraphons are serene, spiritual beings who embody peace, wisdom, and
profound inner calm. They are master healers of the soul, capable of
mending even the deepest emotional wounds through their mere presence.
Deeply connected to Dreamveil (the Spirit World), they serve as guides
for spiritual enlightenment. Their teachings on meditation and balance
are highly sought after by wise elves and monks across Hyperion, who
revere them as beacons of tranquility.

Xenysian

The Xenysian are mediators between the physical and spiritual worlds.
They possess the unique ability to communicate directly with spirits
like the Nephryx and Maltheryx, and can project their spirit into
Dreamveil, leaving their vulnerable physical body behind. This makes
them invaluable guides and interpreters but also exposes them to great
danger from malevolent spirits. A core ambition of their culture is to
one day achieve a full physical presence within Dreamveil, a feat few
have accomplished.

Aetherion

Aetherions are the pinnacle of the Aetherian race, supreme masters of
the Aether---the energy between realms. They wield profound
reality-manipulating magic on a cosmic scale, far surpassing normal
Aetherians. They are living legends within their society, revered as
sages and advisors. They are distinguished by their glowing,
light-colored eyes and intricate markings on their arms, symbols of
their immense mastery and connection to the Aether.

Devil

Devils are cunning, malevolent beings from Gehenna (Hell) who
exclusively appear male. Unlike chaotic demons, devils rely on
intellect, manipulation, and infernal contracts. They appear as horned,
refined humanoids, often dressed elegantly to deceive and persuade
mortals into signing magical pacts that always hide disastrous fine
print. They are masters of long-term schemes and political intrigue,
thriving on the corruption of souls rather than mindless destruction.

Arch-angel

Arch-Angels are the elite warriors of Luminara (Heaven), a superior
class of angel. They are significantly more powerful than common angels,
with larger wings adorned with golden metallic elements and an intensely
radiant aura. They are the commanders of angelic hosts, guardians of
Luminara, and enforcers of divine justice. Their holy magic is
devastating to unholy creatures, and their presence on the mortal plane
often signifies a divine intervention of great importance.

Draconian

Draconians are powerful beings with the innate ability to transform into
dragons. This is a fixed path of growth; their strength and abilities
in both forms are determined by the type of dragon they are linked to
(e.g., frost, blaze, terra). They cannot naturally progress beyond this
form unless they undergo a special transformation to change their
race.

Demon

Demons are chaotic beings from Gehenna (Hell) who delight in the
forbidden and revel in chaos. They can mimic the appearances and
personalities of other species but cannot replicate their innate energy,
making their deceptions hollow. They come in various types: Succubi
(female seducers who drain energy through pleasure), Fiends (agents of
pure mayhem), and Shades (elusive tormentors). Some demons have used
their seductive and fearsome natures to integrate into and manipulate
societies on worlds like Hyperion.

Angel

Angels are celestial beings who reside among mortals on worlds rich with
holy magic, like regions of Hyperion. They are humanoid with large
feathered wings and often a faint ethereal glow. They are natural
wielders of holy magic, making them formidable opponents to dark
creatures like demons and vampires. While powerful, they are not
invincible and rely on their magic, making them vulnerable to anti-magic
measures. They often ally with races like the Arcanians to uphold
justice.

Umbraxan

Umbraxans are a variant of the Voidlings, born from the enigmatic
Void dimension. They possess a deeper connection to the Void\'s energies
and are more aggressive and powerful than their peaceful predecessors.
They are skilled warriors who use the Void\'s power for combat and
manipulation, often venturing into physical worlds to expand their
influence or seek resources that strengthen their bond to their dark
home.

Astrae

Astrae are a rare and revered offshoot of elves, considered holy
offspring. They are distinguished by intricate, glowing markings on
their faces that signify their divine connection. They possess access to
higher-tier magic and celestial crafting techniques beyond the reach of
normal elves, allowing them to create powerful artifacts and commune
with divine beings. They are seen as spiritual leaders and prophets
within elven society.

Aetherian

Aetherians are a race closely resembling humans but with a natural
connection to the Aether, granting them minor reality-manipulating
abilities. Their most distinctive feature is their brightly glowing
eyes, which shine in colors like blue, pink, or green. While not as
powerful as their Aetherion cousins, they are still advanced
magic-wielders, often seen as scholars and innovators within the cosmic
community.

Voidling

Voidlings are a peaceful race originating from the Void. They resemble
elves with pointed ears, black hair often tinged with purple, and dark,
mysterious eyes. They lack the glowing markings of Umbraxans. Their
connection to the Void is used for utilitarian purposes and daily life
rather than combat. They are contemplative beings who prefer the serene
nothingness of their home dimension, rarely venturing outward and
avoiding conflict.

Elf

The elves of Hyperion are a graceful, all-female race born from sacred
Trees of Life, the most revered being the Home Tree, Iloni. They are
deeply connected to nature and are master enchanters and artisans, known
for their longevity and wisdom. Their society is matriarchal and
spiritual, centered around the preservation of their forests and the
magic that flows through them.

Vampire

Vampires are an immortal race sustained by consuming human blood, which
grants them enhanced strength, speed, regeneration, and a euphoric high.
They can eat normal food but derive no power from it. Their society is
ruled by three great Houses: Noctaris (manipulative aristocrats seeking
to rule from the shadows), Morvayne (warlords who conquer through
force), and Dravenmore (mystic scholars obsessed with perfecting
vampirism). They have deeply infiltrated the political and corporate
structures of Hyperion.

Arcanian

Arcanians are humans who possess the innate ability to use magic.
Physically indistinguishable from baseline humans, their talent sets
them apart and allows them to compete in a world filled with magical
threats and wonders. They are the primary practitioners of magic within
human society and are often found in guilds, military units, or as
independent mages-for-hire.

Kitsune

Kitsune are a charismatic race native to the wilds of Hyperion,
resembling humans but with distinctive fluffy animal ears. They are
free-spirited, affectionate, and possess natural pheromones that make
them highly alluring, especially to humans. They live in harmony with
nature, valuing pleasure and freedom over strict tradition. Their
interaction with humans has grown since Hyperion became a travel
destination, leading to cultural exchange and rare romantic
relationships.

Human

Humans are the technologically adept race of Hyperion, lacking innate
magic but mastering science and engineering to an unmatched degree.
Their crowning achievement is Techno-Magic, a fusion of technology and
arcane energy that powers their cities, vehicles, and weapons. Their
ambition, adaptability, and innovative spirit have made them a major
political and economic force, though they often rely on magical races
like Arcanians or Angels to deal with supernatural threats.
