Teams & Guilds

The Monster Oversight Division (MOD)

Motto: \"Vigilance, Order, Balance.\"

The Monster Oversight Division is the sprawling bureaucratic and
military engine that maintains the delicate equilibrium between the
civilized races of Hyperion and the ever-present threat of monster
outbreaks from Dungeons. While individual nations and city-states have
their own governments for day-to-day affairs, the MOD\'s authority on
all matters related to monster hunting, power growth, and inter-dimensional
incursions is absolute. Its influence is a constant across Hyperion,
from the neon-lit spires of techno-arcanic metropolises to the most
remote village bordering a Dungeon zone.

History & Purpose

The MOD was formed centuries ago in the aftermath of the \"Great
Breach,\" a catastrophic event where multiple high-level Dungeons
erupted simultaneously, nearly overwhelming the world. The
near-apocalypse made it clear that unregulated hunters and competing
nations could not manage the threat alone. A treaty was signed by the
major powers, ceding authority to a new, neutral organization: the MOD.
Its primary purposes are:

-   Regulation: To register, test, and rank all hunters to ensure
    competency and track powerful individuals.

-   Containment: To monitor Dungeon activity, predict Breaches, and
    contain outbreaks before they threaten population centers.

-   Research: To study monsters, their essences, and the phenomenon of
    power growth to better understand and combat the threat.

-   Balance: To prevent any single entity or nation from accumulating
    enough power through growth to destabilize the world order.

Structure and Operation

The MOD is headquartered in a fortified, city-like complex known as The
Bastion. From there, its operations extend to thousands of regional
offices and field outposts near major Dungeons.

The Registry

Every hunter must be registered with the MOD. Registration involves a
thorough assessment of one\'s Race, skills, magic affinity, equipment,
and most importantly, their Amplitude (potential for growth within their
current race). This determines their starting Rank (D to X). Hunters are
legally required to report any power breakthrough for re-assessment.

The Ranking System

The alphanumeric Rank (E-X) is not just a measure of power but a
license. Higher Ranks grant access to more dangerous (and lucrative)
Dungeons, better quests from the MOD job board, and significant social
and economic privileges. An \"A-Rank\" hunter is a local celebrity,
while an \"S-Rank\" is a strategic asset known across continents.

The Enforcement Division

MOD isn\'t just paperwork. It has its own military force, the Sentinels.
These are highly trained hunters who have chosen a career within the MOD
structure. They handle major Breaches, apprehend rogue hunters who use
their powers for crime, and sometimes clash with strike teams that
operate outside MOD regulations.

Cultural Impact

To the public, MOD is a necessary and mostly respected institution, akin
to a powerful global police force. To hunters, they are both benefactor
and obstacle---a source of valuable contracts and intel but also a
relentless bureaucracy that demands taxes on essence cores and enforces
strict rules. There is a constant tension between independent strike
teams who cherish their freedom and MOD, which views them as
unpredictable variables that need to be controlled.

Guilds

Guilds are the lifeblood of Hyperion\'s economy and the primary vehicle
for ambitious individuals to pursue greater power. Licensed by the MOD, they
range from massive corporations to small, specialized collectives.

Major Guilds

Aethelgard Consortium

Motto: \"Order. Efficiency. Evolution.\"

The largest and most influential guild. Aethelgard operates like a
multi-national corporation. They have a standardized, militaristic
structure with clear ranks, salaried positions, and benefits packages
for members. They prioritize safety, data-driven strategies, and
reliable, repeatable results over high-risk, high-reward endeavors.

-   Recruitment: They recruit top graduates from MOD-accredited hunter
    academies and often headhunt successful independents. They favor
    individuals with high Amplitude ratings and disciplined,
    team-oriented mindsets.

-   Public Perception: Seen as professional, reliable, and a little
    soulless. They are the \"safe choice\" for a hunter career, but
    critics call them power-hungry institutions that stifle individual
    creativity and risk-taking.

Solaris Vanguard

Motto: \"From the Dawn, We Stand.\"

The oldest surviving guild, founded in the aftermath of the Cataclysm of
Yggdra. They have deep, traditional ties to the Elven nations and a
strong affinity for combating Radiant, Holy, and Frost-affinity
monsters. They are known for their mastery of defensive and purification
magics.

-   Recruitment: Heavily favors Elves, Astrae, and those with Radiant or
    Holy affinities. Membership is often seen as a sacred duty to
    protect Hyperion from the darkness that shattered the old world.

-   Public Perception: Respected and revered, but sometimes viewed as
    archaic and rigid in their ways. They are the noble paladins of the
    guild world.

The Umbra Shroud

Motto: \"Understand the Dark to Control It.\" (Official); \"No Shadow
Too Deep.\" (Unofficial)

The polar opposite of the Vanguard. Specialists in handling Umbral,
Unholy, and Keystone monsters with temporal or spatial affinities. They
operate with a level of secrecy that often borders on paranoia, and
their headquarters\' location is unknown. Rumor claims they have a
permanent base within a stabilized high-level Dungeon.

-   Recruitment: Secretive and invite-only. They seek out individuals
    with unique Gazes (like Gaze of Revelation or Binding), Voidlings,
    Umbraxans, or those who have survived traumatic encounters with dark
    entities.

-   Public Perception: Feared and distrusted, but indispensable. When a
    Dungeon starts leaking nightmare fuel or twisting time, the MOD
    calls the Shroud. Everyone is grateful for their work but nervous
    when they\'re around.

Minor Guilds

The Geode Crawlers

-   Specialization: Terra and Keystone Monsters (Geomantic). Experts in
    mining, tunneling, and securing subterranean Dungeons. They are the
    primary source of rare minerals and earth-attuned Core Essences.

-   Culture: Practical, sturdy, and no-nonsense. Their members are often
    sturdy Humans with earth-based affinities.

Stormseekers

-   Specialization: Shock and Blaze Monsters. They thrive in volatile,
    high-energy environments like volcanic rift Dungeons or eternal
    storm cells. Many are Draconians or beings with
    lightning/storm affinities.

-   Culture: Reckless, thrill-seeking, and fiercely competitive. They
    believe that true power comes from confronting raw, untamed
    power head-on.

The Crimson Gauntlet

-   Specialization: Dire and Lesser Monsters (Mass Extermination). They
    don\'t go for quality; they go for quantity. They handle MOD
    contracts for clearing out swarms of lower-tier monsters that
    threaten settlements or infrastructure.

-   Culture: Seen as the brutish laborers of the guild world. Not
    glamorous, but it provides a steady income and combat experience for
    those starting out or who have hit a power plateau.
