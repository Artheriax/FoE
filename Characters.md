Characters

Sersey

-   Race: Half-Elf (Book I).

-   Age: 70 (appears early-20s by human standards).

Background

Born and raised in the port city of Aethelburg, Sersey is the daughter
of a human shipwright and an elven navigator. Her mixed heritage has
always made her feel like an outsider in both communities. For decades,
she has been a hunter, stuck at the peak of her Half-Elf potential (Rank
B), unable to gather the immense energy required for a safe evolution.
This has bred a deep-seated frustration and a quiet desperation to prove
herself.

The Hidden Burden

Unbeknownst to the MOD and even to herself for many years, Sersey\'s
stagnation is not merely a lack of power. She carries two latent,
dormant gifts that her Half-Elf body is fundamentally incapable of
sustaining.

Dormant Gaze of Inner Thought

Sersey\'s mind is a vulnerable receiver, constantly picking up the
\"psychic static\" of those around her---surface anxieties, hidden
judgments, and fleeting desires. This unintentional eavesdropping has
made her profoundly paranoid and socially withdrawn, as maintaining
conversations while filtering this noise is exhausting. She often
suffers from crippling migraines after being in crowded places, which
she has always attributed to stress. This Gaze has made true connection
impossible, reinforcing her isolation.

Dormant Sigil of Adaptation

A faint, shifting pattern like heat haze is barely visible across her
shoulders and spine. It has been trying and failing for decades to adapt
her body into a vessel capable of holding the energy needed for both her
evolution and to power her Gaze. Instead of granting power, it manifests
as a constant, low-grade physical strain---a feeling of internal
pressure, unexplained fevers or chills, and a metabolism that burns
energy inefficiently, making the already arduous task of gathering
essence even harder. It is a parasite of potential, sapping her as it
tries and fails to make her more.

Personality

Determined, stubborn, and fiercely loyal to the few who manage to break
through her defensive shell. She masks her deep-seated insecurities and
chronic discomfort with a dry, sarcastic wit and an intense focus on the
mission. The departure of her friends felt like a confirmation of her
deepest fears: that she is fundamentally broken and destined to be left
behind. She is resilient not because she is strong, but because she has
endured a lifetime of silent, inexplicable suffering and has never given
up.

Goal

To finally evolve, not just to break her racial limitations, but in the
desperate hope that it will finally silence the noise in her head and
stop the ache in her bones. Her journey is one of self-reliance born
from necessity, and the discovery that her greatest curses are the seeds
of her ultimate strength.
