# Forbidden Magic

Forms of magic so dangerous or antithetical to reality that their use is banned by the MOD and hunted by the Eternal Guards. Six schools are designated forbidden, ranging from theoretical prohibition to extinction-level classification.

Any practitioner confirmed to use forbidden magic is subject to immediate apprehension and termination of citizenship rights. Several schools carry death-on-sight orders.

*→ `systems/Arcane.md` — Index: MOD Registry Overview | All Schools | Forbidden Magic*

## Nihility Magic

**Discovered:** Pre-Record era, exact date unknown. First documented evidence recovered from archaeological site at the Fracturing of the Eastern Rift.

**Summary:** Only three confirmed successful casts in recorded history. Each resulted in the complete historical erasure of the caster — no records of their names, deeds, or existence remain. Secondary effects created localized reality distortions lasting decades.

**Institutional Response:** Classified as highest-tier forbidden. Any evidence of attempted study results in immediate execution and memory purge of all institutional knowledge of the practitioner. Current containment protocol: Seal all theoretical texts in Eternal Guard vaults. No MOD personnel may research or compile data on this school.

**Cross-Reference:** `world/History.md` — The Cataclysm of Yggdra (theory: Nihility involvement; classification sealed)

The attempt to wield the "nothingness" of the Nihility dimension. It doesn't destroy; it erases. A single successful cast typically consumes the caster entirely, wiping them from history. It is the magic of absolute self-sacrifice and oblivion.

## Wall of Nihility "Magic"

**Discovered:** Ongoing since Pre-Record era. No successful interaction documented.

**Summary:** The Wall exhibits no response to any known magical manipulation. Research invariably leads to researcher psychological collapse or sensory deprivation psychosis. Three major research initiatives (Third Era, Fifth Era, Seventh Era) all terminated within 18 months due to personnel casualties.

**Institutional Response:** Absolute research ban. "Wall interaction study" classified as capital offense. Any material older than containment protocols is destroyed. Current knowledge held only by Primordial-tier Eternal Guard authorities.

It is believed no magic can affect the Wall. It is not a thing to be manipulated, but a law of reality. Research into it is forbidden because it invariably leads to madness or attracts the attention of things that should not be noticed.

## Nil Space Magic

**Discovered:** Theoretical only. Proposed by Archon Dravenmore (Spatial Magic master) in Seventh Era correspondence.

**Summary:** Hypothetically, Nil Space represents the conceptual "containment" of nothingness — if Nihility is emptiness, Nil Space is the imprisonment of that emptiness. No confirmed attempt to access it; all theoretical pathways produce mathematical paradoxes.

**Institutional Response:** Dravenmore case classified and terminated via permanent institutional reassignment. All correspondence and research notes sealed. Cross-discipline theoretical discussion forbidden. Any practitioner proposing Nil Space mechanics is immediately flagged for psychological evaluation and investigation.

**Cross-Reference:** `systems/arcane/Keystone.md` — Archon Dravenmore (sealed entry); Classified research tag applied.

A theoretical magic. If Nihility is nothingness, Nil Space is the prison for nothingness. Accessing it is considered impossible. Myths say a being who could wield its power would be able to imprison concepts like "hope" or "time" itself.

## Shadow Magic (Deep Umbraturgy)

**Discovered:** Third Era, documented case of the Veiled Operations (cross-reference `world/History.md` — The Veiled Operations and `systems/gazes/Standard-Gazes.md` — Gaze of Seduction).

**Summary:** Three documented practitioners across recorded history attempted communion with Void entities. Outcomes: Case 1 (Third Era) — possession, entity inhabitation resulted in reality distortion event localized to Ashenveil district. Case 2 (Fifth Era) — partial mutation, practitioner transformed into an anchor/beacon for Maltheryx incursions. Case 3 (Modern Era) — sealed file; outcome unknown; location: classified.

**Institutional Response:** Deep Umbraturgy practitioners are subject to immediate quarantine and execution if confirmed possessed. All known practitioners in institutional records are monitored with scrying protocols. Void entity contact automatically escalates to Eternal Guard authority.

**Cross-Reference:** `world/History.md` — The Veiled Operations (Third Era); `organizations/Organizations.md` — Eternal Guard Response Protocols (sealed).

Going beyond controlling darkness into communing with and drawing power from the entities that dwell in the deepest Void. This always comes with a price: possession, mutation, or becoming an anchor for a Maltheryx or worse.

## Soul Forging

**Discovered:** Fourth Era, practitioner: Mage Callax of Nethermore. Case terminated by Eternal Guard execution and entire settlement quarantine.

**Summary:** Callax developed technique for soul fragmentation and essence extraction. Documented 47 victims before apprehension. Souls fractured beyond recovery; victims left in cataleptic state. Practitioner developed extreme power surge before execution — Eternal Guard required 9 members to contain final casting.

**Institutional Response:** Soul Forging classified as capital crime worse than homicide. Any instrumental evidence (soul fragments, forged essence bottles) is immolated without study. Practitioners are executed on sight and their remains are cremated with purification rituals. Theoretical study forbidden; all texts destroyed.

**Cross-Reference:** `world/History.md` — Callax the Rendered (Fourth Era, sealed entry); Eternal Guard archives.

The art of tearing a soul apart and using its raw energy to power spells or enchantments. It is the most reviled form of magic, punishable by eternal imprisonment or execution by the Eternal Guards.

## Reality Fabrication

**Discovered:** Sixth Era, practitioners: Archmongers Thessalyn and Korven. Joint experimental timeline rewrite attempt.

**Summary:** Thessalyn and Korven attempted to alter a single historical event involving Hexline Tower construction failure. Instead created 47 intersecting parallel timelines, each progressively unstable. Collapse cascade resulted in existential space rupture affecting three continental zones. Reality damage persists: approximately 4,000 km² remain temporally fractured. Return expeditions result in severe temporal psychosis in survivors.

**Institutional Response:** Reality Fabrication classified as extinction-level forbidden magic. All practitioners immediately executed. Related research destroyed across all institutional records. Cross-timeline study sealed in Eternal Guard archives. Current containment: reality breach quarantined; no expeditions authorized.

**Cross-Reference:** `world/History.md` — The Sixth Era Collapse (sealed); `world/Dimensions.md` — Temporal Fracture Zones (restricted access).

Attempting to do what only the Creating One or an Absolute Gaze can do. History records several catastrophes where powerful mages tried to rewrite a single event and instead created unstable parallel timelines that collapsed, causing widespread existential damage.
