# Sources

In the cosmos of Hyperion, all action, all magic, and all manifestation of will draw their fuel from distinct, fundamental reservoirs known as Sources. A Source is not merely energy; it is the origin point from which a specific type of power flows. It is the engine that drives the body, the mind, and, for the most exalted beings, the fabric of reality itself.

Crucially, a Source is distinct from the soul. The soul is the core identity, the seat of consciousness, memory, and the spark of life. It is the "self." A Source, conversely, is the fuel tank and power plant that allows that self to interact with the world. One can exhaust their Source and be rendered powerless, yet their soul remains intact. Conversely, a damaged or stolen soul leaves a being an empty shell, even if their Source is full.

There are three primary categories of Sources, each operating on a different plane of existence and governed by its own rules of depletion and restoration.

---

## Physical Source (Vitalis)

### Nature

The most fundamental and universally accessible Source. It is the animating force of the flesh, the wellspring of kinetic energy, and the foundation for all physical exertion. Every living creature, from the lowliest goblin to the mightiest Draconian, possesses and draws upon their Physical Source.

### Manifestations

Powers all bodily functions beyond autonomic processes. This includes:

- Muscular strength, speed, and endurance.
- Physical strikes, weapon swings, and acrobatic maneuvers.
- The activation and passive function of innate physical abilities (e.g., the enhanced strength from a Fortitude-type trait, the rapid healing from a Vitality-type trait).
- The physical component of spells (e.g., the somatic gestures required for casting, the stamina needed to channel elemental magic).

### Depletion

Physical Source is depleted through exertion, injury, and the use of physically taxing abilities. A hunter sprinting through a dungeon, a warrior trading blows, or a mage maintaining a large geomantic barrier will all feel their Physical Source draining. When fully depleted, the body collapses into exhaustion, unable to move or act.

### Restoration

Recovery is tied to the body's basic biological needs:

- **Rest/Sleep:** The primary method. Deep, uninterrupted sleep allows the Physical Source to replenish fully.
- **Nutrition:** Consuming food and water provides the raw materials for the body to rebuild its energy reserves. High-energy or magically infused foods can accelerate this process.
- **Healing Magic:** Spells like Soothing Touch or Greater Restoration can directly mend the body and jump-start Physical Source recovery, bypassing the need for prolonged rest.

Races with innate physical enhancements (like Vampires or those with innate physical abilities) often have larger Physical Source pools or more efficient restoration rates, allowing them to outlast others in prolonged physical conflict.

### Documented Anomalous Cases

**Valdren Ashcrast** *(Seventh Era)* — The Irenmere Pass engagement produced the most precisely documented Physical Source endurance record in the Registry: sustained combat-peak Physical Source output maintained for 31 continuous hours. The established theoretical maximum for mortal Physical Source under moderate exertion at the time was 18–22 hours. Ashcrast's innate Fortitude-type ability is the accepted explanation, though the exact mechanism — whether the trait expanded his pool, accelerated real-time restoration, or reduced per-action depletion cost — was not resolved in the post-engagement assessment. The case remains the definitive evidence that innate ability bearers can exceed standard Source limits, not just augment output within them. Cross-Reference: *world/history/Seventh-Era.md*.

## Mental Source (Psyche)

### Nature

The power of the mind and spirit. This Source governs willpower, focus, psychic phenomena, and the channeling of non-physical, esoteric energies. It is the bridge between thought and reality, allowing for the manipulation of the unseen.

### Manifestations

Powers all abilities that originate from or affect the mind and spirit:

- The activation and use of innate mental abilities (e.g., the psychic intrusion of an Inner Thought-type trait, the reality-warping perception of an Illusion-type trait).
- Psychic attacks, telekinesis, telepathy, and mental domination.
- The casting of most spells, which require intense mental focus, visualization, and the manipulation of arcane formulae. Even elemental magic requires Mental Source to shape and direct the raw energy.
- Spiritual projection (used by Xenysians to enter Dreamveil).
- Resistance to mental attacks, illusions, and fear effects.

### Depletion

Mental Source is drained by concentration, emotional stress, and the use of mentally taxing abilities. A mage casting a complex spell, an ability bearer maintaining constant perception, or someone resisting a powerful psychic assault will feel their Mental Source waning. Depletion leads to mental fatigue, headaches, loss of focus, and eventually, unconsciousness or psychic burnout.

### Restoration

Recovery is an internal, meditative process:

- **Sleep:** Essential for the mind to reset and rebuild its energy. Dreams are often seen as the mind's way of processing and restoring its Mental Source.
- **Meditation/Trance:** A focused, conscious effort to calm the mind, center one's thoughts, and draw ambient spiritual energy to replenish the well. Seraphon and elven monks are masters of this technique.
- **Emotional Calm:** Being in a state of peace, safety, or profound serenity can passively restore Mental Source. Conversely, stress and fear accelerate its depletion.
- **Spiritual Aid:** Communing with benevolent spirits (Nephryx) or receiving blessings from Celestials can provide a direct infusion of Mental Source.

> **Note:** Sustained use of innate mental perception abilities is a notorious Mental Source drain, as it forces the bearer's mind to constantly process additional psychic "noise," leading to chronic migraines and mental exhaustion. Gaining control over such abilities is as much about managing Mental Source as it is about mastering the ability itself.

### Documented Anomalous Cases

**Healer Cassian of the Ashenmoor** *(Mid-Era)* — The only MOD-documented case of a practitioner dying specifically from sustained Mental and Physical Source dual-exhaustion under non-combat conditions. Across three years of continuous high-intensity Greater Restoration casting, Cassian's cumulative Source depletion pattern — documented in House Dravenmore post-career medical analysis — showed progressive reduction in baseline Mental Source recovery rate without corresponding reduction in output demand. The cause of death at age 67 was listed as magical exhaustion; the underlying mechanism was determined to be permanent degradation of Mental Source restoration capacity from chronically incomplete recovery cycles. Cassian's case is cited in MOD healer certification guidelines as the precedent establishing mandatory rest protocols for sustained healing practitioners. Cross-Reference: *systems/arcane/Holy-Unholy.md; world/history/Mid-Era.md*.

**The Dreaming of Valcrest Engagement Team** *(Fifth Era)* — The three hunters who did not recover from the Specimen NKS-1 engagement exhibited a condition classified as Permanent Psychic Displacement. Post-engagement Medical evaluation determined their Physical Sources were fully intact and functional. Their Mental Sources had not been depleted in the conventional sense but had been structurally displaced — the Sources remained present but were anchored to a subjective experiential timeline still running at the engagement's internal time ratio. The bodies functioned. The selves could not be reached. MOD classification of this failure mode: [Sealed]. The case is not publicly listed as a Source anomaly. Cross-Reference: *systems/Monsters.md; world/history/Fifth-Era.md*.

## Cosmic Source (Aeterium)

### Nature

The exclusive, infinite power of the Higher Ones. This is not a wellspring that can be depleted or restored; it is an intrinsic, boundless aspect of their being, as fundamental as their existence. A Cosmic Source is the concept they embody, made manifest as usable power.

### Manifestations

The direct application of a Higher One's cosmic function. These are not "spells" but the effortless exertion of their will upon reality:

- The Destroying One wields the Force of Entropy, unmaking galaxies with a thought.
- The Creating One channels the Force of Genesis, birthing new stars and laws of physics.
- The Judging One imposes the Force of Absolute Truth, compelling universal honesty.
- The Observing One utilizes the Force of Record, perfectly archiving all moments across time.
- The activation of certain cosmic-tier abilities, which are fragments or expressions of these cosmic powers granted to (or stolen by) mortals.

### Depletion & Restoration

Cosmic Sources are inexhaustible. They do not fatigue, nor do they require rest, sustenance, or meditation. They are a constant, infinite flow. A Higher One can exert their will across the multiverse for eternity without diminishing their power. This is what separates them from even the most powerful Ascendants or Primordials, who still draw from finite Mental or Physical Sources (albeit on a godlike scale).

The concept of Entity Zero transcends even the Cosmic Source in scholarly theory. If the Higher Ones are infinite wells, Entity Zero — were it confirmed to exist — would be the ocean from which those wells were carved: the origin of the concepts that the Higher Ones embody. No empirical evidence of Entity Zero's existence has ever been documented. It remains a theoretical construct, the cosmological missing link. The Wall of Nihility is a pre-existing boundary structure at the edge of dimensional reality — not constructed by the Higher Ones, not derived from a known power source. Its imperviousness to Cosmic Sources is an observed property, not a derived one. Scholars theorize it operates outside the framework within which all Sources function, including Cosmic Sources.

### Documented Anomalous Cases

**Artem and the Wall of Nihility** *(Ongoing)* — Artem, a baseline human with no documented innate abilities, survived an extended period inside the Wall of Nihility — a structure that erases everything entering it. The survival has no precedent and no confirmed explanation. Eternal Guard researchers have proposed multiple theoretical frameworks, none verified. The leading hypothesis suggests an interaction between the Wall's erasure architecture and Artem's specific biological state produced a condition in which he ceased to register in reality's targeting framework — existing while absent in the register that the Wall uses to process subjects. This condition is consistent with subsequent observations: instruments designed to detect dimensional presence, Source resonance, and ability-bearer signatures consistently read absence where Artem is visually present. The mechanism by which a baseline human survived a boundary structure that has consumed beings at every tier of the Source gradient remains unresolved. This is considered the most significant open question in Wall of Nihility research, and the case file is fully classified under Eternal Guard authority. Cross-Reference: *systems/arcane/Forbidden.md*.

## Essence as External Source Supplement

Energy Fragments and Essences harvested from monsters are not innate Sources but external fuel deposits that interact with a hunter's existing Source pools. Understanding this interaction is fundamental to understanding why monster hunting is the primary method of power acquisition on Hyperion.

### Energy Fragments

The raw, untyped remnant of a monster's life force at death. When absorbed, Energy Fragments provide a direct Physical Source boost — a short-term surge of kinetic and stamina energy that temporarily extends a depleted Physical Source. They do not expand the pool's permanent capacity; they are consumable fuel.

Absorption is not automatic. A hunter must be in physical contact with the Fragment within the extraction window post-neutralization. Fragments absorbed beyond a hunter's current Physical Source capacity dissipate as waste heat.

### Core Essences

The elemental-type residue of Elemental and Divine Monsters. Core Essences interact with both Physical and Mental Source depending on their type:

- **Fire, Frost, Terra, Shock:** Primarily Physical Source supplementation, with secondary effects on the casting aspect of the relevant elemental school. A Pyromancer absorbing a Fire Core Essence experiences both a Physical boost and a temporary reduction in Mental Source cost for fire-aspected casting.
- **Radiant, Umbral:** Primarily Mental Source supplementation. Radiant Essences provide clarity and focus; Umbral Essences provide a dulling of psychic noise, which can be beneficial for bearers of perception-type abilities.
- **Holy, Unholy:** Affect both Sources but include a secondary spiritual component that interacts with the soul. Holy Essences provide a stabilizing effect that can partially resist ongoing Mental Source depletion effects. Unholy Essences are regulated — their necrotic affinity means improper handling can create Source contamination rather than supplementation.

### Keystone Essences

The most dangerous form of external Source interaction. Keystone Essences do not merely supplement an existing Source; they attempt to temporarily expand it. A hunter absorbing a Keystone Essence experiences a surge of capacity that exceeds their normal pool — in theory, accessing abilities, power thresholds, or magical output beyond what their innate Source supports.

The risks are proportional to the gap between the hunter's native Source capacity and the Keystone Essence's power level:

- **Compatible absorption** (hunter capacity near Keystone level): successful temporary expansion, gradual permanent capacity increase possible with repeated correct use.
- **Partial absorption** (gap is significant): the expanded capacity spike is unstable; the hunter experiences the amplified output window but exits it with a Source depletion debt — both Physical and Mental Sources drop below their normal baseline floor and recovery is slower than standard.
- **Incompatible absorption** (gap is extreme): Source burnout. The influx exceeds what the Source structure can process; the excess does not disperse into heat but back-propagates through the Source, damaging its architecture. Recovery from Source burnout is measured in months; severe cases produce permanent capacity reduction.

MOD regulations require X-rank oversight for Keystone Essence absorption and identity logging prior to absorption. The primary reason for the regulation is not misuse — it is ensuring that burnout cases are identifiable and that the hunter's pre-absorption Source baseline is on record for medical comparison.

## MOD Source Assessment Framework

MOD evaluates hunter Source capacity as a component of all rank registration and advancement assessments. Source capacity is rated on two independent scales:

- **Vitalis Grade** — Physical Source pool depth and recovery rate. Rated Grade I (minimal, untrained civilian level) through Grade VII (exceptional; documented in fewer than 0.1% of registered hunters).
- **Psyche Grade** — Mental Source pool depth, focus stability, and recovery rate under stress. Rated Grade I through Grade VII by the same scale.

Rank advancement requires demonstrated improvement in both Grades alongside combat performance metrics. The typical Grade distribution by hunter rank:

| Rank | Typical Vitalis Grade | Typical Psyche Grade |
|------|-----------------------|----------------------|
| E | I–II | I–II |
| D | II | II |
| C | II–III | II–III |
| B | III | III |
| A | III–IV | III–IV |
| S | IV–V | IV–V |
| X | V–VII | V–VII |

Grade assessments are conducted by MOD-certified evaluators using standardized depletion-and-recovery protocols. Anomalous results — Grades that diverge significantly from the hunter's apparent combat performance, or Grades that shift substantially between assessments — trigger additional review. Innate ability bearers routinely produce anomalous assessments, as their augmented output frequently suggests a Grade higher than their innate pool, while their restoration rates suggest a Grade consistent with their actual innate capacity. This discrepancy is a standard identification signal in the MOD Assessment Unit.

Source capacity grows slowly over a hunter's career through repeated near-depletion cycles. This growth is not guaranteed — a hunter who never approaches Source exhaustion does not expand their pool. MOD training academies structure advancement programs to deliberately approach (but not reach) Source floor, on a schedule calibrated to the hunter's current Grade and recovery profile.

## Cross-Source Interactions and Failure States

Most abilities draw from one Source predominantly. However, several ability types require simultaneous draw from both Physical and Mental Sources, and the risks of cross-Source depletion are distinct from single-Source exhaustion.

### Dual-Draw Abilities

Standard spellcasting is the most common dual-draw activity: the somatic component (gestures, posture, channeling stance) depletes Physical Source; the concentration and formulaic manipulation deplete Mental Source. A mage casting continuously will feel both draining in parallel. This is why magical exhaustion produces both physical collapse and cognitive impairment simultaneously, rather than one following the other.

Other dual-draw systems:

- **Innate soul-anchoring abilities:** The soul-anchoring trait draws from both Sources to maintain its function during active contract enforcement attempts. The Compact Defiant case suggests this draw is significant but finite — the bearer did not appear to experience Source failure during the adjudication. What sustained the output is not documented.
- **Ability suppression under extreme psychological load:** A bearer actively fighting their own innate ability (suppressing rather than using) draws continuously from Mental Source but produces Physical Source strain through the sustained tension of suppression, particularly over long durations.

### Cross-Source Failure

When both Physical and Mental Sources approach exhaustion simultaneously, the failure cascade is not additive — it is multiplicative. The body and mind reinforce each other's depletion in a feedback loop: physical exhaustion increases mental focus cost, which accelerates Mental Source draw, which reduces the mental control that moderates physical exertion, which accelerates Physical Source draw. The collapse is typically faster than either source depleting alone.

Recovery from dual-depletion is significantly slower than recovery from single-source exhaustion: baseline recovery time is estimated at 3–4x that of an equivalent single-source depletion. During the recovery window, both Sources are below functional threshold simultaneously — the individual is neither physically nor mentally capable of action.

Repeated dual-depletion events without sufficient recovery — as in Healer Cassian's case — can permanently degrade one or both Sources' restoration architecture. The effect is not reduction of pool size but reduction of recovery rate: the hunter retains their capacity but takes longer to refill it after each use. Over time, the practical effect is indistinguishable from pool reduction.

## The Source Scale: From Mortal to Cosmic

Physical and Cosmic Sources represent the two poles of a continuous gradient, not a binary distinction. The race hierarchy maps onto this gradient:

**Mortal Races** operate with finite Physical and Mental Sources. Their pools vary by individual and grow through training and near-depletion cycling. The upper bound for mortal Source capacity — absent innate abilities or Essence interaction — is Grade VII on both scales. This ceiling is rarely reached; most mortal hunters plateau at Grade IV–V regardless of training.

**Celestials** possess expanded finite Sources. Their pools operate on the same physical and mental principles but with a higher structural ceiling — the equivalent of a Grade VIII or IX if the current scale extended that far. Celestials still deplete and recover; they do not have infinite endurance. The difference is scale and recovery rate efficiency.

**Primordials** operate with what scholars call "deep-pool finite" Sources: pools so vast that on a mortal or even Celestial timescale, they appear functionally inexhaustible. A Primordial engaged in continuous exertion would deplete eventually — the theoretical models suggest this would require sustained engagement lasting decades or centuries — but within any practical encounter window, the pool is effectively without bottom. Primordials additionally demonstrate Source recovery rates so efficient as to be nearly continuous: they replenish as fast as they expend under normal conditions.

**Ascendants** represent a transitional category. Their finite Sources have expanded to Primordial scale, but they additionally possess a limited resonance with Cosmic Source — the ability to briefly touch the conceptual layer without fully embodying it. This manifests as intermittent access to power that exceeds their pool's nominal capacity, drawing against the Cosmic layer for short windows. The mechanism is not fully understood; MOD theoretical frameworks describe it as "borrowing against a conceptual debt that Cosmic Sources do not track."

**Eternarcs** are the borderline case. Their finite pools have, through accumulated age and power over spans that exceed the current historical record, expanded to a scale where the distinction between "extremely deep finite" and "functionally cosmic" is no longer meaningful in practice. Whether their Sources have genuinely crossed into the Cosmic category or whether they simply possess the highest finite pools in recorded existence is an open theoretical question. Eternarcs have never been studied under controlled conditions.

**Higher Ones** possess pure Cosmic Sources: not expanded finite pools but a qualitatively different category of power that operates outside depletion and restoration mechanics entirely. The boundary between Ascendant-resonance-with-Cosmic and genuine Cosmic Source is the most significant threshold in the entire gradient — it is the line between borrowed access and native embodiment.

## Entity Zero

Entity Zero is, in the theoretical framework accepted by MOD-adjacent scholars with access to Eternal Guard cosmological archives, the hypothetical origin point of all Sources — the substrate from which Cosmic Sources are theorized to have separated. It is not a confirmed being, an observed phenomenon, or a documented interactant in any accessible record. It is the answer scholars reach for when the chain of causation runs past the point where documentation stops.

The Higher Ones each embody a concept: Destruction, Creation, Judgment, Observation. Theoretical cosmology proposes that these concepts did not originate with the Higher Ones but pre-existed as undifferentiated potential within an Entity Zero-like substrate, and that the Higher Ones are concepts that separated from that substrate and became individuated. Their Cosmic Sources would therefore be portions of Entity Zero's original whole, now operating as independent infinite flows rather than unified potential. This framing is entirely theoretical. No empirical evidence supports or refutes it.

This framing has several implications that MOD scholars have documented under sealed classification, all predicated on the unverified assumption that Entity Zero exists:

- **Entity Zero's power would not be infinite in the sense that Cosmic Sources are infinite.** Cosmic Sources are infinite because they are continuous flows with no depletion mechanism. Entity Zero's power — were the construct to represent a real entity — would be primordial: it would precede the concepts of depletion and restoration, which means those concepts would not apply to it at all. This is a different category of "unlimited," and one that cannot be verified because no mechanism exists to observe it.

- **The Wall of Nihility is not derived from any known Source architecture.** It is a pre-existing boundary structure at the edge of dimensional reality, observed to exist but not explained by any framework within the Source gradient. Physical Sources cannot interact with it because it does not operate under physical rules. Mental Sources cannot interact with it because it does not occupy mental space. Cosmic Sources cannot interact with it because even Cosmic concepts appear to be bounded by the framework the Wall underlies. Whether the Wall originates from an Entity Zero-like substrate, from an architecture that predates the Higher Ones entirely, or from a category that has no name in current cosmological theory is unresolved.

- **Whether Entity Zero exists at all is unknown.** No documented interaction with Entity Zero exists in any accessible record. No instrument has detected it. No witness has reported contact. The Eternal Guard's cosmological archives describe the construct as "pre-intentional" — a theoretical state that precedes the distinction between purposeful action and inaction — but the description is noted as speculative. MOD classification of all Entity Zero research: [Sealed — Eternal Guard Authority Only].
