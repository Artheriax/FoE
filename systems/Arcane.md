# Arcane

*The Arcane Systems of Hyperion*

Magic on Hyperion is the art of shaping the ambient energy that permeates all existence. This energy is neutral, but the method of shaping it and the intent behind it define its school and effect. The most common form of magic is learned and cast through intricate gestures, spoken words (arcane formulae), and mental focus. However, some beings, particularly higher races or those with unique bloodlines, can wield magic through sheer force of will.

Magical aptitude is not innate—any sentient being can learn to practice magic. However, individual skill, dedication, and cognitive capacity determine mastery level. Some practitioners naturally excel at certain schools of magic, but this is a matter of personal affinity and talent, not predetermined ability.

---

## MOD Arcane Registry Overview

The Monster Oversight Division maintains comprehensive records on all registered spell-casters and arcane practitioners across Hyperion. Registration occurs upon demonstrated competency (typically casting spells consistently at Lesser or Greater tiers) or when a practitioner registers voluntarily for institutional work.

- **Registered Practitioners:** ~45 million active spell-casters tracked by primary school and secondary specialties — approximately 38 million Novice/Apprentice tier, 6 million Journeyman/Adept, 850,000 Expert/Master, and roughly 2,200 practitioners who have received formal Archmage-level recognition
- **Amplitude Ratings:** Skill classification from Novice (experimental Lesser spells) to Archmage (masters of Keystone schools). Unlike Gaze amplitude, arcane amplitude reflects measured casting capacity and theoretical knowledge, not mystical power.
- **Regulatory Framework:** High-tier practitioners (Keystone school users) require MOD licensing and periodic evaluation. Forbidden magic practitioners are subject to immediate apprehension and termination of citizenship rights.
- **Institutional Roles:** Luminancy practitioners in medical and sanctioning operations; Fulminancy specialists in Hexline Tower maintenance; Terra Magic users in infrastructure development; Umbraturgy practitioners subject to enhanced oversight.

## Spells (Arcane Formulae)

Spells are the structured, repeatable application of magical energy. They are categorized by their nature and effect.

### Holy Magic (Luminancy)

The magic of Luminara, fueled by faith, purity, and the essence of creation. It is anathema to corruption and decay.

#### Notable Practitioners

- **Saint Meridus the Radiant** (Third Era) — Pioneer of Celestial Luminancy; first documented practitioner of Resurrection outside direct divine intervention. Established the theoretical framework for reanimating souls across dimensional barriers. Posthumously canonized by the Eternal Guard for contributions to mortality studies and resurrection ethics.
- **Healer Cassian of the Ashenmoor** (Mid-Era) — Legendary field medic whose mastery of Greater Restoration saved over 3,000 lives during the Ashenmoor Campaigns. Developed rapid-deployment healing protocols still used by MOD emergency response teams.
- **Inquisitor Vaughn** (Modern Era) — Anonymous MOD investigator specializing in using Judgment's Gaze spell for guilt-determination interrogations. Amplitude: Supreme. Institutional sanctioning authority.

#### Lesser Luminancy

- **Luminous Orb:** Creates a ball of harmless, bright light.
- **Soothing Touch:** Minor healing of cuts and bruises.
- **Ward of Purity:** Creates a small barrier that weakens minor unholy effects.

#### Greater Luminancy

- **Sanctify Ground:** Consecrates an area, making it difficult for unholy creatures to enter or remain.
- **Radiant Lance:** A spear of solidified light that sears flesh and disorients dark beings.
- **Greater Restoration:** Heals poisons, diseases, and crippling wounds.

#### Celestial Luminancy

- **Resurrection:** An immensely complex and draining ritual to return a recently deceased soul to its body. Requires the soul's willingness and an intact body.
- **Judgment's Gaze:** The caster's eyes glow with divine light, forcing a target to confess their greatest sins or be momentarily paralyzed by guilt.
- **Heaven's Fall:** Calls down a devastating pillar of pure holy energy to obliterate a target.

### Unholy Magic (Umbraturgy)

The magic of Gehenna and the Void, fueled by ambition, pain, and the essence of negation. It is often faster and more destructive but can corrupt the user.

#### Notable Practitioners

- **Magister Valdross the Profane** (Pre-Third Era) — Mastered Profane Umbraturgy; attempted to create a permanent plague-zone in the Nardec district using Mass Enthrall thralls as vectors. Operation halted by Eternal Guard intervention at cost of 4,000+ lives. Validation Date: Executed by celestial fire, year of Judging. Case remains classified but referenced in forbidden magic restriction ordinances.
- **Corvus Nightwhisper** (Fifth Era) — Anonymous Soul Shackle specialist under MOD contract for paranormal containment. Amplitude: Greater. Active 47 years, sealed institutional record.
- **The Void-Touched Collective** (Modern Era) — Underground network of Greater Umbraturgy practitioners. MOD active surveillance, 3 known cells eliminated since Seventh Era. Location: Sealed.

#### Lesser Umbraturgy

- **Shadow Tendril:** Creates a whip of darkness to grasp or strike.
- **Minor Hex:** Causes bad luck, minor paranoia, or a sense of dread in a target.
- **Siphon Life:** Drains a small amount of vitality from a target to heal the caster's minor wounds.

#### Greater Umbraturgy

- **Soul Shackle:** Temporarily binds a soul, preventing resurrection or communication with the afterlife.
- **Hellfire Eruption:** Unleashes a burst of green or black fire that burns both physically and spiritually.
- **Abyssal Possession:** Allows the caster's consciousness to inhabit and control a weak-willed or injured creature.

#### Profane Umbraturgy

- **Plague of Gehenna:** Summons a virulent, magical disease that spreads rapidly and is resistant to conventional healing.
- **Mass Enthrall:** Subverts the will of multiple weaker beings, turning them into loyal thralls.
- **Annihilating Ray:** A thin, black beam that does not burn or freeze, but simply unmakes matter it touches, leaving behind absolute nothingness.

### Elemental Magic

The manipulation of the world's fundamental forces. Power is drawn from internal reserves or by drawing energy from the environment.

#### Notable Practitioners by School

- **Pyromancer Helios** (Sixth Era) — Master of Blaze Magic; demonstrated plasma generation for industrial applications in Hexline Tower construction. Documented 94 major constructions across three continents. Amplitude: Archmage (provisional).
- **Glaciarch Frostweaver** (Fourth Era) — Pioneering Cryomancy theorist; developed the absolute zero field preservation technique used in MOD evidence storage. Work forms the basis of all modern cryo-containment protocols for dangerous essences and artifacts.
- **Conductor Voltrix** (Modern Era) — Fulminancy specialist; serves as lead architect for Hexline Tower maintenance protocols. Amplitude: Supreme. Institutional critical role—cannot be reassigned or retired without Ministry approval.
- **Geomancer Kael Stoneheart** (Fifth Era) — Terra Magic master; responsible for subterranean city infrastructure in three major settlements. Posthumously identified as practitioner after death; underground structures remain operational and stable.
- **Illuminator Prism** (Modern Era) — Photomancy researcher (anonymous); experimental hard-light construct development for MOD weaponization. Project status: Ongoing. Amplitude: Greater, classified.
- **Shadowweaver Lykaios** (Seventh Era) — Umbrakinesis specialist; elite hunter known for shadow-phase evasion tactics. Amplitude: Supreme. Sealed institutional access logs.

#### Elemental Schools

- **Blaze Magic (Pyromancy):** Control over fire and heat. Masters can create plasma, induce spontaneous combustion, or absorb flames.
- **Frost Magic (Cryomancy):** Control over ice and cold. Masters can freeze the air in lungs, create absolute zero fields, or forge unmelting glacial constructs.
- **Shock Magic (Fulminancy):** Control over lightning and electromagnetism. Masters can manipulate technology, disrupt neural signals, or teleport short distances via lightning arc.
- **Terra Magic (Geomancy):** Control over earth, stone, and metal. Masters can cause earthquakes, magnetize armor, or create complex underground structures.
- **Radiant Magic (Photomancy):** Control over light. Distinct from Holy magic, it manipulates light's physical properties. Masters can create hard-light constructs, bend light for perfect invisibility, or fire lasers.
- **Umbral Magic (Umbrakinesis):** Control over darkness and shadows. Distinct from Unholy magic, it manipulates the absence of light. Masters can travel through shadows, create zones of sensory deprivation, or solidify darkness into weapons.

### Keystone Magic

This is not a school but a classification for spells that manipulate reality's most complex and fundamental aspects. Accessing this magic requires a Keystone Essence and is incredibly dangerous. Most who attempt it without a natural affinity (like a Celestial or Higher One) risk their mind and soul.

#### Notable Practitioners

- **Chronomancer Timerion** (Second Era) — Temporal Magic pioneer; developed the theoretical framework for Moment's Recall and demonstrated feasibility of Hasten/Slow at scale. Disappeared during unauthorized Temporal Stasis experiment. Status: Presumed lost to temporal fracture.
- **Archon Dravenmore** (Mid−Modern Era) — Spatial Magic master; principal designer of the Hexline system spanning all major cities. Amplitude: Archmage—lifetime institutional contract. Currently active on classified research involving Nil Space mechanics (cross-reference Forbidden Magic Case: Dravenmore Nil Space Expedition).
- **Sage Stellarion** (Seventh Era) — Cosmic Magic theorist; pioneered Starfall targeting protocols for elite hunter support. Attempted unauthorized Fateweave research; case terminated with immediate incarceration. Status: Sealed.

#### Temporal Magic

Manipulation of time. Even minor spells are incredibly draining.

- **Hasten/Slow:** Affects the flow of time for a single target or small area.
- **Moment's Recall:** Allows the caster to rewind time by a few seconds for themselves only, effectively giving them a second chance at a single action. Extremely disorienting.
- **Temporal Stasis:** Places a target in a bubble of stopped time. The energy cost scales exponentially with the size of the target and duration.

#### Spatial Magic

Manipulation of space and distance.

- **Teleportation:** Personal teleportation is complex but possible for masters. Teleporting others or objects is far more dangerous.
- **Spatial Fold:** Creates a pocket dimension. Most are small and temporary, used for storage. Creating a stable, habitable pocket dimension is a feat for a Primordial.
- **Dimensional Gate:** The principle behind Hexlines. Creating a stable gate requires immense, fixed infrastructure (Hexline Towers).

#### Cosmic Magic

Manipulation of celestial forces, gravity, and fate.

- **Gravity Well:** Increases or decreases gravity in a localized area.
- **Starfall:** Pulls a fragment of astral energy from the heavens to strike a target.
- **Fateweave:** A forbidden branch that attempts to see or alter the threads of destiny. The backlash is always severe and unpredictable.

## Forces

Forces are not "spells" but the fundamental expressions of a Higher One's will. They are not learned; they are wielded. A mortal attempting to channel even a fraction of this power would be instantly unmade.

- **The One:** The confluence of all Higher Ones, embodying absolute cosmic authority and the totality of divine power.
- **The Outer One:** Force of the Beyond, the spaces outside the known universe, the creation of the Aether and all things alien and incomprehensible.
- **The Destroying One:** Force of Entropy, Annihilation, Unmaking.
- **The Creating One:** Force of Genesis, Potential, Manifestation.
- **The Judging One:** Force of Absolute Truth, Law, Consequence.
- **The Balancing One:** Force of Equilibrium, Cause and Effect, Cosmic Counterweight.
- **The Intervening One:** Force of Aberration, the Unforeseen Variable, Divine Intervention.
- **The Observing One (Phariah):** Force of Record, Unchanged Memory, Silent Witness.
- **The Nonexistent One:** Force of Erasure, the Un-Concept, the Void Before and After.
- **The Nameless One:** Force of Anonymity, the Forgotten Detail, Identity Theft on a cosmic scale.
- **The Forgotten One:** Force of Oblivion, erased from all memory and record, existing only as an absence.

## Forbidden Magic

These are forms of magic so dangerous or antithetical to reality that their use is banned by the MOD and hunted by the Eternal Guards.

### Case Origins and Institutional Response

#### Nihility Magic

**Discovered:** Pre-Record era, exact date unknown. First documented evidence recovered from archaeological site at the Fracturing of the Eastern Rift.

**Summary:** Only three confirmed successful casts in recorded history. Each resulted in the complete historical erasure of the caster—no records of their names, deeds, or existence remain. Secondary effects created localized reality distortions lasting decades.

**Institutional Response:** Classified as highest-tier forbidden. Any evidence of attempted study results in immediate execution and memory purge of all institutional knowledge of the practitioner. Current containment protocol: Seal all theoretical texts in Eternal Guard vaults. No MOD personnel may research or compile data on this school.

**Cross-Reference:** History.md — The Cataclysm of Yggdra (theory: Nihility involvement; classification sealed)

- **Nihility Magic:** The attempt to wield the "nothingness" of the Nihility dimension. It doesn't destroy; it erases. A single successful cast typically consumes the caster entirely, wiping them from history. It is the magic of absolute self-sacrifice and oblivion.

#### Wall of Nihility "Magic"

**Discovered:** Ongoing since Pre-Record era. No successful interaction documented.

**Summary:** The Wall exhibits no response to any known magical manipulation. Research invariably leads to researcher psychological collapse or sensory deprivation psychosis. Three major research initiatives (Third Era, Fifth Era, Seventh Era) all terminated within 18 months due to personnel casualties.

**Institutional Response:** Absolute research ban. "Wall interaction study" classified as capital offense. Any material cross older than containment protocols is destroyed. Current knowledge held only by Primordial-tier Eternal Guard authorities.

- **Wall of Nihility "Magic":** It is believed no magic can affect the Wall. It is not a thing to be manipulated, but a law of reality. Research into it is forbidden because it invariably leads to madness or attracts the attention of things that should not be noticed.

#### Nil Space Magic

**Discovered:** Theoretical only. Proposed by Archon Dravenmore (Spatial Magic master) in Seventh Era correspondence.

**Summary:** Hypothetically, Nil Space represents the conceptual "containment" of nothingness—if Nihility is emptiness, Nil Space is the imprisonment of that emptiness. No confirmed attempt to access it; all theoretical pathways produce mathematical paradoxes.

**Institutional Response:** Dravenmore case classified and terminated via permanent institutional reassignment. All correspondence and research notes sealed. Cross-discipline theoretical discussion forbidden. Any practitioner proposing Nil Space mechanics is immediately flagged for psychological evaluation and investigation.

**Cross-Reference:** Arcane.md — Keystone Magic practitioners, Archon Dravenmore (sealed entry); Classified research tag applied.

- **Nil Space Magic:** A theoretical magic. If Nihility is nothingness, Nil Space is the prison for nothingness. Accessing it is considered impossible. Myths say a being who could wield its power would be able to imprison concepts like "hope" or "time" itself.

#### Shadow Magic (Deep Umbraturgy)

**Discovered:** Third Era, documented case of the Veiled Operations (cross-reference History.md and Gazes.md — Gaze of Seduction bearer involved in deep pact with Void entities).

**Summary:** Three documented practitioners across recorded history attempted communion with Void entities. Outcomes: Case 1 (Third Era)—possession, entity inhabitation resulted in reality distortion event localized to Ashenveil district. Case 2 (Fifth Era)—partial mutation, practitioner transformed into an anchor/beacon for Maltheryx incursions. Case 3 (Modern Era)—sealed file; outcome unknown; location: classified.

**Institutional Response:** Deep Umbraturgy practitioners are subject to immediate quarantine and execution if confirmed possessed. All known practitioners in institutional records are monitored with scrying protocols. Void entity contact automatically escalates to Eternal Guard authority.

**Cross-Reference:** History.md — The Veiled Operations (Third Era); Organizations.md — Eternal Guard Response Protocols (sealed).

- **Shadow Magic (Deep Umbraturgy):** Going beyond controlling darkness into communing with and drawing power from the entities that dwell in the deepest Void. This always comes with a price: possession, mutation, or becoming an anchor for a Maltheryx or worse.

#### Soul Forging

**Discovered:** Fourth Era, practitioner: Mage Callax of Nethermore. Case terminated by Eternal Guard execution and entire settlement quarantine.

**Summary:** Callax developed technique for soul fragmentation and essence extraction. Documented 47 victims before apprehension. Souls fractured beyond recovery; victims left in cataleptic state. Practitioner developed extreme power surge before execution—Eternal Guard required 9 members to contain final casting.

**Institutional Response:** Soul Forging classified as capital crime worse than homicide. Any instrumental evidence (soul fragments, forged essence bottles) is immolated without study. Practitioners are executed on sight and their remains are cremated with purification rituals. Theoretical study forbidden; all texts destroyed.

**Cross-Reference:** History.md — Callax the Rendered (Fourth Era, sealed entry); Eternal Guard archives.

- **Soul Forging:** The art of tearing a soul apart and using its raw energy to power spells or enchantments. It is the most reviled form of magic, punishable by eternal imprisonment or execution by the Eternal Guards.

#### Reality Fabrication

**Discovered:** Sixth Era, practitioners: Archmongers Thessalyn and Korven. Joint experimental timeline rewrite attempt.

**Summary:** Thessalyn and Korven attempted to alter a single historical event involving Hexline Tower construction failure. Instead created 47 intersecting parallel timelines, each progressively unstable. Collapse cascade resulted in existential space rupture affecting three continental zones. Reality damage persists: approximately 4,000 km² remain temporally fractured. Return expeditions result in severe temporal psychosis in survivors.

**Institutional Response:** Reality Fabrication classified as extinction-level forbidden magic. All practitioners immediately executed. Related research destroyed across all institutional records. Cross-timeline study sealed in Eternal Guard archives. Current containment: reality breach quarantined; no expeditions authorized.

**Cross-Reference:** History.md — The Sixth Era Collapse (sealed); Dimensions.md — Temporal Fracture Zones (restricted access).

- **Reality Fabrication:** Attempting to do what only the Creating One or an Absolute Gaze can do. History records several catastrophes where powerful mages tried to rewrite a single event and instead created unstable parallel timelines that collapsed, causing widespread existential damage.

## Institutional Applications

Arcane practitioners serve critical roles across MOD operations and civilian infrastructure. Distribution of registered practitioners reflects institutional demand and regulatory controls.

### School-Specific Roles

**Luminancy (Holy Magic)**

- Medical operations: Field healing, disease treatment, resurrection consultation
- Sanctioning authority: Guilt determination, purification rituals, soul consultation
- Infrastructure: Holy grounds consecration, corruption suppression
- Civilian applications: Healing temples, faith-based counseling

**Umbraturgy (Unholy Magic)**

- MOD oversight: Advanced surveillance operations, intelligence extraction
- Criminal investigation: Deep interrogation, psychological manipulation (regulated)
- Military applications: Tactical darkness deployment, morale disruption
- Regulatory status: Enhanced licensure requirements; practitioners monitored for corruption risk

**Elemental Magic (All Six Schools)**

- Pyromancy: Industrial heating, metallurgical forging, tactical fire support
- Cryomancy: Essence containment (frozen storage facilities), evidence preservation, extreme climate research

- Photomancy: Surveillance technology, hard-light barrier construction, classified weaponization research

- Temporal Magic: Highly restricted; used only in Eternal Guard consultation for historical validation
- Spatial Magic: Hexline system (Archon Dravenmore active oversight), interdimensional containment, pocket dimension management

### Disposition by Era

- **Third Era:** Institutional registry formalized; Keystone Magic oversight established following Dravenmore protocols
- **Modern Era:** ~12,400 registered practitioners; institutional demand exceeds supply, particularly for Cryomancy and Fulminancy specialists

## Enchantments

The art of permanently imbuing objects with magical properties. It requires rare materials, a powerful energy source (like Core Essences), and immense skill.

### Weapon Enchantments

- **Elemental Edge:** Grants the weapon a permanent elemental affinity (Flaming, Frostbite, Shocking, etc.).
- **Soul-Drinker:** The weapon stores a fraction of the life force of those it kills, granting the wielder enhanced vitality or healing.
- **Phase-Reaver:** Allows the weapon to phase through armor or magical barriers to strike the target directly. Requires a Keystone Essence of Space.
- **Bane of [Creature Type]:** The weapon is supernaturally effective against a specific type of monster or race (e.g., Bane of Umbral Monsters, Bane of Demons).
- **Judgment:** An enchantment that can only be placed by a high-ranking servant of the Judging One. The weapon deals no physical damage but inflicts psychic pain proportional to the target's guilt.

### Armor Enchantments

- **Aegis of Reflection:** Has a chance to reflect a spell back at its caster.
- **Environmental Adaptation:** Automatically adjusts to extreme temperatures and pressures.
- **Null-Field:** Generates a weak anti-magic field, making the wearer resistant to direct magical effects.
- **Kinetic Shunt:** Absorbs and disperses the force of physical impacts.

### General Enchantments

- **Spatial Pocket:** Creates a small, extradimensional space within an object (like a bag or ring) for storage.
- **Hexline Key:** Allows the wielder to activate personal Hexlines for rapid transit across short distances (within a city).
- **Truth of the Observing One:** The enchanted object (often a book or crystal) cannot be altered by any known means; it perfectly records what is spoken to it.
- **Gaze-Bound:** An extremely rare and powerful enchantment that binds a lesser version of a Gaze's power to an object, like a crown that grants a weak form of the Gaze of Seduction.

### Theoretical Magic Debate

Several forms of magical practice remain unconfirmed, theoretically proposed, or actively disputed among the arcane community. Their scientific validity remains subject to scholarly debate and institutional scrutiny.

#### Fateweave (Cosmic Magic Branch)

**Status:** Documented but uncontrolled

**Theoretical Framework:** Cosmic Magic practitioners theorize that Fateweave represents an attempt to perceive or alter the threads of destiny woven by the Balancing One or higher cosmic forces.

**Evidence:** All documented attempts resulted in severe backlash—psychological fracturing, temporal disorientation, or death. No successful benign casting recorded.

**Scholarly Debate:** Some theorists argue Fateweave is impossible for mortals to wield without immediate divine punishment. Others propose that sufficiently skilled practitioners (Archmage tier) might achieve limited scrying of likely futures without manipulation. MOD stance: Research forbidden; all theorists registered and monitored.

**Cross-Reference:** History.md — Sage Stellarion (sealed); Arcane.md Forbidden Magic section.

#### Synthesis Magic (Proposed)

**Status:** Theoretical; no confirmed examples

**Theoretical Framework:** Proposed by unknown theorists in Seventh Era: the ability to combine two or more magic schools simultaneously into hybrid casting, potentially creating effects impossible in standard schools.

**Evidence:** Anecdotal reports only; no laboratory replication attempted.

**Scholarly Debate:** Mainstream arcane theorists dismiss Synthesis Magic as impossible without splitting magical focus or causing immediate burnout. Fringe researchers argue that Higher Ones and Primordials may naturally perform synthesis casting, suggesting mortals with sufficient power might achieve hybrid spells.

**MOD Status:** Theoretical discussion permitted; practical experimentation forbidden without explicit Archmage authority consultation.

#### Soul-Resonance Magic (Speculative)

**Status:** Highly speculative; one anecdotal reference

**Theoretical Framework:** Rumored ability to attune spells specifically to a target soul rather than through conventional casting. Proposed by Archon Dravenmore in sealed Seventh Era correspondence alongside Nil Space proposals.

**Evidence:** Single unverified reference in classified archives.

**Scholarly Debate:** If possible, Soul-Resonance Magic would represent the inverse of Soul Forging—healing and alignment rather than destruction. Debate centers on whether this is a legitimate magical discipline or a metaphysical impossibility for beings with finite understanding.

**MOD Status:** No official recognition; discussion marked as speculative pseudoscience. Further theoretical proposals require Eternal Guard approval.

#### Dream-Weaving (Rumored)

**Status:** Rumored; no institutional confirmation

**Theoretical Framework:** Anecdotal reports of practitioners influencing dreams, implanting suggestions, or accessing shared dreamscape. Proposed mechanism: targeting the Mindscape Gaze bearer phenomenon rather than external reality.

**Evidence:** Unverified reports from hunter logs and subjective accounts; no scientific replication.

**Scholarly Debate:** Some theorists align Dream-Weaving with Mindscape Gaze abilities rather than independent magic. Others argue they represent distinct disciplines—one innate, one learned. Further research restricted pending clarification.

**Cross-Reference:** Gazes.md — Gaze of Mindscape (Ethereal tier, Sighting Status: Unconfirmed); History.md — The Sleeper Incident (sealed).
