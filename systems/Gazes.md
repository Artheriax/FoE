# Introduction to Gazes

A hunter steps into a crowded guild hall and knows, without being told, which contractor in the back corner is lying about the payout. A child looks at her mother across the breakfast table and her eyes shimmer gold — the district Assessment nurse will visit before noon, because her teacher noticed and filed the standard form. A MOD analyst reads case files for thirty years without anyone discovering she can perceive the truth of a witness statement before the witness opens their mouth.

In the world of Hyperion, power is not always measured in the energy one collects or the power one achieves. Sometimes, it is bestowed at birth, a latent potential waiting behind the eyes. Among Hyperion's 15 billion inhabitants, roughly 30 million carry one of these innate abilities — known as Gazes — making them about as common as distinct birthmarks, a familiar part of daily life rather than an anomaly.

A Gaze is not a learned skill or a magical spell; it is a fundamental part of an individual's soul, manifesting as a unique ocular phenomenon. The eyes of a bearer are often, but not always, physically altered — shimmering with unnatural colors, displaying unusual pupil shapes, or glowing with an inner light that corresponds to their power. The activation of a Gaze is typically a conscious act, requiring focus and intent, though powerful emotions can sometimes trigger them involuntarily.

The origin of Gazes is one of Hyperion's greatest mysteries. Some theological scholars believe they are blessings — or curses — from the Higher Ones, fragments of cosmic power embedded in a mortal soul. Arcane theorists propose they are spontaneous mutations in the spiritual DNA, a random confluence of energy that occurs at conception. Others whisper they are echoes from past lives or bleed-over from other dimensions. What is known for certain is that they cannot be replicated by technology or magic, and their appearance is entirely unpredictable.

The MOD maintains a comprehensive registry of all documented Gazes — one of the institution's largest operational divisions. Bearing a Gaze increases a hunter's Amplitude rating, though the degree depends on the Gaze's tier and the bearer's demonstrated control.

There is a small chance of Gazes to also change or evolve, becoming stronger when the user evolves into the next race. The user must already possess a Gaze. In some cases with too little documentation there are dual or even triple Gazes.

## Categories of Gazes

- **Standard Gazes:** The most common tier — roughly 30 million bearers across Hyperion, about 1 in every 500 people. Each grants a specific ability tied to perception, manipulation, or physical effect. Most Standard Gaze bearers are identified during childhood medical screenings and live unremarkable lives; the minority who pursue hunter careers receive a modest Amplitude adjustment and standard Monitoring Unit oversight.

- **Supreme Gazes:** Roughly 1.5 million bearers. A Supreme Gaze is a significant asset — its abilities affect larger areas, groups, or operate at magnitudes that make their bearers strategically relevant to guilds, MOD operations, and institutional power structures. Supreme bearers are actively recruited by guilds and monitored more closely by the Assessment Unit.

- **Absolute Gazes:** Roughly 7,500 confirmed cases, all classified above MOD general Registry access at Eternal Guard authority. Absolute Gazes are powers on the order of cosmic authority — the Oblivion Gaze erases targets from existence, the Mindscape Gaze projects consciousness into populations. To possess one is to be categorically beyond MOD's enforcement capacity; all Absolute bearers fall under Eternal Guard jurisdiction.

- **Ethereal Gazes:** The most enigmatic tier. Fewer than ten cases are documented, none confirmed through standard MOD verification. Confirmed Ethereal bearers (Artem) are treated as unique cases rather than representatives of a reproducible category. Proposed functions interact with Gaze mechanics themselves — Null cancels other Gazes, Mimicry copies them, Convergence merges them — operating one conceptual level above the abilities they affect.

Possessing a Gaze is a double-edged sword. At Standard and Supreme tiers, it provides social and professional advantage. At Absolute tiers, it attracts Eternal Guard attention. At any tier, it makes the bearer a potential target for trafficking networks, institutional exploitation, and unregistered research interests.

## MOD Registry Overview

The Monster Oversight Division maintains the most comprehensive registry of verified Gaze bearers in Hyperion. All individuals confirmed to possess a Gaze, regardless of tier, are registered and monitored through the Bureau of Phenomenal Capacity (BPC). Registration provides legal protection but also mandates periodic evaluations and restrictions on ability use.

**Amplitude Ratings** for Gaze bearers are adjusted accordingly:

- Standard Gazes: +1 to +3 ratings
- Supreme Gazes: +4 to +6 ratings
- Absolute Gazes: Classified
- Ethereal Gazes: Classified (unconfirmed)

Individuals suspected of possessing unregistered Gazes are subject to mandatory MOD evaluation. Resistance to evaluation is grounds for facility commitment under the Threshold Directive of 245 Eastern Era.

**Registered Bearers (current):** Approximately 30 million confirmed Standard Gaze bearers across all twelve types are on active Registry record. Approximately 1.5 million confirmed Supreme Gaze bearers carry individual standing files. Absolute-tier confirmed mortal cases number approximately 7,500 but are classified above standard Registry access level; MOD's publicly accessible records reflect zero confirmed Absolute bearers. Ethereal tier remains unconfirmed within Registry records.

With 1 in 500 Hyperion inhabitants bearing a Standard Gaze, MOD's Assessment Unit operates as one of the institution's largest divisions, maintaining regional registration centers across all major population zones. Gaze identification typically occurs during childhood medical screenings; late-life discovery is uncommon but not unheard of, particularly among Voidling and Dark Elf populations with below-average institutional engagement. Public infrastructure in most urban centers includes Gaze-dampening zones in sensitive facilities (hospitals, schools, government buildings), and most employers are legally required to provide reasonable accommodation for Gaze bearers whose abilities create workplace complications.

**Incidents and Oversight:** Gazes have been responsible for 23 documented major incidents affecting population centers. All major incidents resulted in revised containment protocols and/or enforcement of sanctuary placement for problematic bearers. The volume of registered bearers makes comprehensive individual monitoring impractical; MOD employs statistical sampling, automated amplitude flagging, and mandatory self-reporting requirements as primary oversight mechanisms rather than direct surveillance.
