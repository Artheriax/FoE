# Standard Sigils

**MOD Classification:** Approximately 15 million confirmed Standard-tier Sigil bearers are on active Registry record across all six types. These enhance the body's core attributes: strength, speed, senses, endurance, and adaptability. They are the foundation of physical superiority.

## Sigil of Fortitude

**Effect:** Enhances the body's foundational strength, granting incredible stamina and muscle endurance. Allows for feats of prolonged exertion that would cripple others and significantly accelerates muscle recovery. The bearer can sustain peak physical output for hours before fatigue becomes a limiting factor.

**Appearance:** A pattern of interlocking, shield-like shapes or robust geometric forms, typically found on the shoulders, chest, or back. Glows a steady, earthy brown or deep green when the bearer is exerting themselves; remains nearly invisible at rest.

**Lore:** Fortitude is the most documented Standard Sigil and the one most easily mistaken for exceptional training or natural constitution. Bearers rarely discover it through triumph — they discover it through surviving something that should have broken them. A sparring accident that should have shattered a wrist. A dungeon collapse that pinned a less-built hunter for three days they simply walked away from. The Sigil does not announce itself with power; it announces itself with the absence of expected damage.

In social terms, Fortitude bearers are simultaneously the most normalized and the most strategically hunted of the Standard tier. The Morvayne vampire house maintains informal "talent records" of suspected Fortitude carriers in cities they influence. The MOD's Enforcement Division specifically prioritizes Fortitude-bearing recruits for Sentinel positions. The broader guild world treats them as prized anchors for front-line teams — reliable, steady, and almost insultingly hard to kill. Many spend years believing they are simply very good at what they do before the Sigil is formally identified.

The clearest named historical case is Valdren Ashcrast, recorded in the 7th Era campaign logs of the Siege of Irenmere Pass. Tasked with holding a narrow mountain passage for six hours against a force at fourteen-to-one odds, Ashcrast held it for thirty-one hours before reinforcement arrived. The after-action report notes, in flat military language, that he was returned to the field with no listed injuries despite arriving at the evacuation point covered in blood that subsequent medical review confirmed was not primarily his. He was registered as a Fortitude bearer the following month. He rejected the Sentinel assignment the MOD offered and spent the rest of his career as a freelance contractor. The Morvayne house later attempted to acquire his contract. His documented response to their offer is reproduced in hunter culture as a short poem. Its meaning is considered self-evident.

## Sigil of Agility

**Effect:** Rewires the nervous system for peak performance, granting lightning-fast reflexes, perfect balance, and explosive movement speed. Bearers move with an unnatural, fluid grace that becomes apparent at their limits — the moment they're pushed toward the edge of their capability, every motion becomes unnervingly precise.

**Appearance:** Sleek, flowing lines and arrowhead patterns that seem to suggest motion even when still. Commonly appears on the legs, ankles, or around the eyes. Glows with a swift, electric blue or yellow during fast movement; pulses briefly with each sudden burst of speed.

**Lore:** The neurological enhancement that makes Agility bearers so effective in combat creates a specific and persistent problem long before they ever enter one. The nervous system processes everything faster than the body needs it to, which in childhood manifests as an inability to fully switch off. Young Agility bearers often cannot sleep well — their bodies make constant small corrections, microadjustments to position and balance, even in stillness. Most are diagnosed with some form of heightened sensitivity before the Sigil is identified. The hypervigilance rarely fully disappears; most simply build habits around it.

In the wider world, Agility bearers occupy a particular cultural niche. They are romanticized in popular stories as "dancers of death" — assassins and duelists who move like they invented gravity by choice. The Umbra Shroud quietly recruits any Agility bearer they can identify before the MOD does. Several famous historical thieves have been posthumously confirmed as Agility carriers. The irony the MOD publicly avoids acknowledging is that their most capable enforcement officers and their most capable criminal opponents are frequently drawn from the same Sigil type.

The figure recorded in the Ashenveil quarter records as "the Stormstep" is the most frequently cited historical Agility bearer who was never, in their lifetime, identified as one. Seventeen successful contract extractions from locations the MOD classified as impenetrable. An eight-year operational career during which no witness description meaningfully matched any other. A death from causes entirely unrelated to any known hunter conflict. The posthumous reconstruction of their movement patterns — assembled by the very MOD division that had spent seven years hunting them, using archived surveillance constructs — produced a classified paper whose authors stated in the abstract that they were "uncertain whether the movements documented were physically possible in principle, or were simply possible for this individual specifically." The Umbra Shroud claimed the attribution immediately. The MOD noted, in a footnote, that the Stormstep had twice been offered legitimate MOD contracts during their career and had declined both. The footnote does not speculate on what this means.

## Sigil of Senses

**Effect:** Dramatically heightens all five senses to superhuman levels. The bearer can hear a heartbeat across a crowded room, see with perfect clarity for miles in adverse conditions, track a weeks-old scent across changed terrain, and register temperature differentials too subtle for conventional instruments to detect.

**Appearance:** A complex spiral or mandala pattern, often centered on the forehead or the palms of the hands. It glows with a faint silver light when the senses are actively extended, resembling a scatter of stars.

**Lore:** No Standard Sigil isolates its bearer from ordinary social life more consistently than Senses. Living in any populated area is a continuous exercise in managing input that was never meant to be managed. The smells of a market, the layered cacophony of a busy street, the distant argument two floors up, the sound of someone's panicked heartbeat in a crowd — bearers receive all of it, constantly. Most develop eccentric mitigation habits before adolescence ends: muffling fabric around sensitive areas, deliberate routes through cities that avoid peak traffic, a tendency toward sparse, quiet living spaces.

The ability to selectively focus rather than simply receive takes years to develop, and young bearers are frequently misdiagnosed with magical sensitivity disorders or dismissed as having anxiety. The MOD recognizes the pattern and has learned to look for the misdiagnosis as an identification flag. Once registered, Senses bearers become among the most valued intelligence and tracking assets in the entire hunter system. A persistent rumor within MOD internal culture is that certain senior administrative positions are deliberately assigned to Senses bearers to passively catch corruption — they can hear the specific quality of a voice that is lying.

Maelen Voss, senior analyst of MOD Internal Review's 22nd Cycle, holds a case-resolution record across a thirty-year career that remains unmatched in institutional history. She attributed it, in published profiles, to methodology and unusual patience. MOD's classified human-resource files — opened two decades after her retirement — noted her as a confirmed Senses bearer whose Sigil had been identified and documented seven years into her career and subsequently suppressed from public record at her personal request. She did not want the cases re-examined with her Sigil as an explanation. Her reasoning, recorded in the suppression request: *"The work stands on its own. The cases were solved. The evidence was real. I want credit for reasoning, not for hearing things."* The suppression held until her file opened. Several of her most famous convictions are now under secondary procedural review — not because the outcomes were wrong, and not because the evidence failed, but because the documentation of *how* certain evidence was identified does not satisfy current regulatory standards when an unacknowledged passive ability is retroactively introduced. The convictions have held. The methodology review has not yet concluded.

## Sigil of Vitality

**Effect:** Supercharges the body's natural healing processes and fortifies the immune system. Cuts close in seconds, broken bones mend in hours rather than weeks, and the body becomes highly resistant to toxins, diseases, and environmental contaminants. The bearer's blood itself has anomalous clotting properties.

**Appearance:** A branching, tree-like or vein-like pattern that spreads out over the body, typically originating from the chest near the heart. It pulses with a warm, life-giving red or pink energy when healing is actively occurring, visibly brightening as damage is processed.

**Lore:** Vitality is the easiest Sigil to live with and the hardest to hide under pressure. In ordinary life, the bearer simply seems very healthy. Minor injuries vanish overnight. They are never sick for more than a day. Field physicians assume good constitution. But in combat, the Sigil becomes conspicuous: wounds that should be incapacitating close visibly while the fight continues. Blood stops before a bandage is applied. This visibility makes Vitality bearers both deeply valued and deeply targeted.

Vampires take a particular academic interest in them. House Dravenmore maintains genealogical records of known Vitality lineages going back several centuries, ostensibly for medical research. What exactly they use those records for is a matter of ongoing speculation in hunter circles. On the institutional side, medical corps assigned to high-casualty MOD operations make direct requests for embedded Vitality bearers, who function as self-sustaining field assets. Historically, wartime records across multiple conflicts note the same pattern: a soldier survives conditions that kill everyone around them, and only after the war ends is the Sigil identified and catalogued. Many were never registered at all by choice.

The most openly documented Vitality bearer in historical record is Orvielle Caste, a combat medic from the mid-era Ashenmoor Campaigns whose unit's survival rate of eighty-three percent across a three-year deployment stands as a statistical anomaly that was never attributed to her Sigil during her lifetime. Her commanders credited her medical skill, which was also exceptional. The Sigil was identified after her death, when a reviewing Dravenmore scholar noted that her field journal contained a recurring personal observation, entered across dozens of separate entries over fifteen years: *"wounds close when I don't expect them to."* She had apparently spent her entire career believing she healed unusually fast because she was careful. House Dravenmore added her lineage to their private genealogical registry within the week.

## Sigil of Adaptation

**Effect:** Allows the body to temporarily alter its physiology to survive extreme environments — adjusting to intense pressure, generating internal heat in freezing cold, filtering toxins from ingested material or breathable air. Unlike the Ethereal evolution, Standard Adaptation requires active environmental stress to trigger meaningful response, and the changes are temporary rather than permanent.

**Appearance:** A shifting, camouflage-like pattern that changes subtly based on its surrounding environment. It often appears on the torso or neck, and at rest glows with a muted, adaptive grey. In active environments — extreme cold, toxic air, crushing depth — the pattern brightens and shifts as the body processes its adjustments.

**Lore:** The Adaptation Sigil is the rarest documented at Standard tier and the most dangerous to bear without understanding. Because it responds to *threats*, its calibration depends entirely on the bearer's history of exposure. A bearer raised in safety and comfort may have a Sigil that never receives the input it was built to process, resulting in a mark that runs in a constant state of low-power searching — scanning for threats that don't arrive, attempting adaptations that the environment doesn't require. This produces a specific and miserable experience: chronic low-grade fevers, joint aches that move and shift with environmental changes, a metabolism that burns inefficiently as the body prepares for something that isn't coming. Many bearers in this state spend years convinced they are simply unwell.

The MOD currently lists nine confirmed active Adaptation bearers in its modern registry. All carry exceptional Amplitude scores. The registry's classified annex — accessible only under Project Crimson clearance — notes that ancient bloodline records trace consistent Adaptation carriers back to a pre-MOD lineage labeled only as *"The First Strand."* What that lineage connects to is redacted. The Eternal Guards are documented to take notice of Adaptation bearers at a frequency that has no institutional justification. The MOD has not commented on this.

## Sigil of Resilience

**Effect:** Reinforces the body's structural integrity, strengthening bones to near-unbreakable density and making tissues highly resistant to cuts and blunt trauma. It also dramatically reduces the subjective experience of fatigue, allowing the bearer to continue functioning at reduced capacity long past the point where the body would otherwise shut down.

**Appearance:** A pattern resembling cracked stone or diamond latticework. Typically found on the knees, elbows, and fists — the impact points of the body. Glows with a dull, sturdy grey or orange when the bearer takes significant physical force, as though the impact is being distributed and absorbed.

**Lore:** Resilience bearers rarely discover their Sigil through triumph. They discover it through the absence of expected consequence. The fall from a training obstacle course that should have broken an ankle. The bar fight where the punch connected fully and nothing happened. A blade that skimmed across the arm and stopped at the skin. The discovery is typically quiet, private, and accompanied by a brief period of disorientation — the gap between anticipated pain and received experience feels like missing a step on a staircase, but for getting hit by something that should have mattered.

This subtlety makes Resilience the most common undisclosed Standard Sigil. Bearers who have never been told what they carry are estimated to significantly outnumber registered ones, because the Sigil doesn't demand attention. It simply makes the bearer survive things. In the wider hunter world, Resilience bearers rarely become famous heroes. They become the person who is still standing when the contract resolves. The Crimson Gauntlet guild keeps informal tallies of hunters who come back from assignments that killed their partners without visible explanation, specifically to make recruitment offers. Many never pursue the Sigil at all, viewing the ability to absorb punishment as simply "being tough" and seeing no reason to draw MOD scrutiny.

The Resilience lineage shares a notable anomaly with the Adaptation progression: it skips a tier. Every confirmed case that has evolved past Standard has transitioned directly to Absolute Resilience, bypassing Supreme entirely. While Adaptation skips Absolute, Resilience skips Supreme — making it the only Sigil known to exhibit tier-transcendent progression across a different gap. MOD's Theoretical Research Division has documented the phenomenon in sealed files; the most widely held hypothesis is that Resilience, by its nature, resists being graded. It does not intensify by degree the way other Sigils do, and the attempt to force it into a Supreme framework may be category error rather than limitation.

The most culturally embedded Resilience bearer in Hyperion's popular history is Toryn Kell, who operated as a doorguard and debt-collector in the Nardec financial district for approximately twenty-two years and who was, in that time, subject to twelve documented serious assault attempts by clients of varying organization and capability. The records of those incidents share a common entry format: incident escalated, physical force applied to Kell, incident was then resolved. No medical costs for Kell appear in any related filing. His response to a street reporter who asked, late in his career, whether he worried about being hurt: *"I've been hit a lot. It doesn't really take."* He was registered posthumously. The MOD's note on his file is one sentence: *Standard Resilience, likely unawakened for the majority of his adult life; career outcomes consistent with full activation.* The guild structure has since used him as an informal case study for why lifetime registration is preferable to posthumous cataloguing — the argument being that Kell, had he known what he carried, could have been something considerably more than a very hard-to-hurt doorman. Whether Kell would have agreed is not recorded.

## Sigil of Seduction

**Effect:** Optimizes the bearer's body to its genetic ceiling — fittest possible musculature, clearest skin, healthiest hair and nails, ideal posture and symmetry. A sedentary bearer gains the physique a lifetime of perfect discipline would have produced. Beyond physical optimization, the sigil radiates a passive supernatural magnetism. Others instinctively gravitate toward the bearer; hostility softens, attention drifts toward them unbidden, their voice and presence carry weight disproportionate to their words. The effect is not mind control — it is gravity. Both the physical optimization and the supernatural pull scale with baseline attractiveness: the more inherently appealing the bearer's features, the more dramatic the result.

**Appearance:** A delicate, symmetrical pattern of fine lines tracing both sides of the face, following the cheekbones and jawline like ornamental filigree. At rest, the mark is barely visible — a faint shimmer under the skin. When the supernatural magnetism is in active effect, it glows with a soft, minimal light, usually in shades of pink, red, or purple. The color deepens faintly in response to attention directed at the bearer, a subtle accent that reinforces rather than distracts from their presence.

**Lore:** The Sigil of Seduction occupies a strange cultural position — simultaneously dismissed and envied, underestimated and exploited. Bearers are statistically overwhelming female; the MOD's demographic records note in internal files that the distribution skews so heavily it has shaped the sigil's entire institutional reputation. In hunter circles, the Seduction bearer is a figure of persistent, low-grade contempt — the mark is considered "soft," a social crutch, something for diplomats and courtiers rather than real operators. Those who hold this view have never been on the wrong side of a Seduction bearer who chose to weaponize what they carry.

Bearer paths tend to crystallize into three distinct archetypes. The first, and the one that defines the sigil's popular image, is the bearer who leans fully into its nature. These are the courtesans, the companions, the performers — women whose allure functions as both art and industry. The adult entertainment sector on Hyperion maintains informal but aggressive recruitment networks targeting confirmed Seduction bearers, and several of the most famous names in that industry are registered carriers who operate their careers with a level of professional control that the guild structure quietly envies. The second archetype is the one the guild structure quietly fears: the thief, the infiltrator, the con artist who understands that attraction is the most efficient lockpick ever designed. These bearers don't announce themselves. They register, file the paperwork, smile at their intake interview, and then disappear into the spaces between institutional notice. The third archetype is the icon — the muses, the influencers, the figures whose image becomes a genuine standard to which others aspire. These bearers often work in fashion, performance, or public-facing creative arts, and their cultural footprint is frequently broader than any single combat contract could produce.

The psychological cost is substantial and rarely discussed. A Seduction bearer has no way of knowing where their natural charm ends and the sigil's effect begins. Compliments, attraction, social success — all of it arrives with an asterisk. Is the attention real, or is it the sigil? Did they earn the deal, or did the mark close it? Bearers who dwell on this too long tend to spiral. Bearers who accept it tend to weaponize it. Neither path is comfortable, and the MOD's psychological support division lists Seduction as one of the highest-referral sigil types among registered bearers seeking identity-integration counseling.

The most cited historical bearer in Seduction scholarship is Emira Tess, a diplomat operating during the tail end of the 5th Era's Meridian Trade Wars. Tess was dispatched to broker a ceasefire between two merchant guilds whose conflict had escalated into open urban warfare across three port cities. Conventional negotiation had failed for eighteen months. Tess secured a binding treaty in eleven days. Contemporaries attributed her success to rhetorical brilliance and diplomatic instinct. A posthumous registry review — conducted after her sigil status was identified from archived intake records — noted that every surviving account of her meetings contained the same observation across multiple, independent witnesses: people in Tess's presence consistently described wanting her to be right. Not agreeing with her arguments — wanting to agree. The review's conclusion was that Tess was almost certainly a fully active Seduction bearer who had either been unaware of her sigil or had chosen not to disclose it. The treaty held for forty-two years. The methodology review remains unresolved.

# Dual Sigils

In all of recorded history, one confirmed case exists of a bearer who carried two Standard Sigils simultaneously: a Morvayne vassal documented in the 3rd Era who bore both Fortitude and Resilience. His autopsy — conducted by House Dravenmore — produced a classified report that neither house has publicly acknowledged. Scholars who have accessed adjacent records describe the interaction between the two marks as producing a pattern that moved, shifting between configurations as though the Sigils were negotiating precedence. MOD notation on the case reads: *review suspended, subject deceased, lineage tracked.*

The theoretical question of whether dual Sigils can stably coexist, or whether one inevitably subsumes the other, has no reliable answer. The Adaptation lineage represents a special case: its mark has been observed briefly overlapping with a Standard Vitality Sigil in one documented bearer before the Adaptation mark expanded to encompass and replace the other entirely over a period of weeks. The bearer survived. They were reportedly unaware it had happened until the Vitality mark failed to appear in a follow-up registry scan. Whether this constitutes absorption, suppression, or simply dominance remains an open question.

Dual Sigils are not theorized to occur at the Supreme tier or above. At that level, the marks are redefinitions of the body's operating rules rather than additions to them, and two such redefinitions in a single biological system are considered, by the MOD's theoretical division, to be "incompatible with continued existence." This statement was marked as provisional when it was written. It has not been revised.
