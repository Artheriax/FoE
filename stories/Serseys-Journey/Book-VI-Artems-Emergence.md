# Book VI: Artem's Emergence

*Part of: FoE: Sersey's Journey*

**Premise:** Artem walks out of the Wall of Nihility into a world that has aged three thousand years in the span of hours — a baseline human whose research became the foundation of an entire civilization's relationship with power. The gazes and sigils that shape Hyperion's society are his work, spread accidentally through groundwater contamination while he was imprisoned. He is the scientist who built the system and never got to see the results. He is not a power to be measured. He is a historical fact to be confronted. His emergence forces every institution that has ever classified the Wall or suppressed the origin of gazes and sigils to reckon with the living evidence that their frameworks were incomplete.

---

- **Ch. 1–3:** Artem exits the Wall and collapses. The transition is instantaneous and total — hours of sensory nullity replaced by full physical-reality input with no gradation — and a baseline human body, however resilient, cannot absorb that transition without a reset. He clears the Wall's edge and goes down. The Keepers of Nil, already positioned at the ritual site, are still processing that the being who emerged is not Entity Zero. Their institutional conviction has just been contradicted by physical evidence, and the processing is not complete. They secure his unconscious form. Sersey, near the transport formation, registers the moment with the clarity of someone who has learned to read situations faster than she can articulate them: whatever they expected, this is not it. The Keepers prepare for transit back through Nihility's outer edge.

- **Ch. 4–6:** Three hours into the transit, the Keepers' lead guide stops. The reason is not detectable by ordinary senses — it is something the Keepers know from their research the way other organizations know warning signs: a specific quality of dimensional disturbance that registers as incoming authority. She holds position. Six figures approach through a fold in the substrate that should not be accessible from the physical plane. No guild marks, no MOD insignia, no house colors. The lead figure presents a warrant seal that Gaelira recognizes and no one else does.

The lead figure addresses the group collectively: *"You entered an active access zone adjacent to the Wall of Nihility. Your transit signature registered above the threshold for incidental observation. You are being held for evaluation pending determination of intent and disposition."*

The Keepers don't protest. They know what this is. The Echo Breakers understand enough to stay quiet. Nobody mentions the unconscious figure on the transport frame, because nobody knows where to begin. The group is taken to Eventide.

- **Ch. 7–9:** Eventide arrives as sensation before it arrives as sight: air that carries no temperature in either direction, a complete stillness, a quality of light that makes everything visible without warmth. The group is separated for evaluation. The Keepers are processed first — they have the most to account for. The Echo Breakers are held separately as registered MOD hunters whose presence in a sealed dimension is a serious irregularity. Sersey remains in proximity to Artem because the Guard hasn't yet decided which category he belongs to.

Artem is still unconscious when they arrive. The member assigned to his case doesn't handle threats — she handles anomalies. The distinction exists in the Guard's internal structure because sometimes what arrives in a sealed access zone is not a violation but a category that doesn't have a name yet. She reviews the transit record. She runs instruments. The instruments return nothing — not failure, not error, nothing. Baseline human readings across every measurable spectrum. This is not what a being emerging from the Wall should produce. The Wall has consumed everything that has ever entered it. The instruments say this man doesn't register as anything at all. She sits with this for a moment. Then he wakes.

Artem opens his eyes to the violet-indigo sky of Eventide, to ancient dark stone beneath him, to still water below the platform edge holding the sky in mirror — and to a woman crouched near him, watching him with the expression of someone who came here to process a violation and found something that doesn't fit the form.

*"You're awake,"* she says. Not a greeting. Something she had been wondering about.

He blinks. She is still watching him. He can tell, with the precision of someone who spent hours in sensory deprivation cataloguing the only thing available to catalogue, that she does not know what he is. She has the expression of someone building a hypothesis rather than applying one.

*"What are you?"* she asks. It comes out before whatever procedural opener she was supposed to use.

Artem tells her he was a researcher. She says that's not what she meant. He says he knows. He asks what this place is. She says Eventide, and watches to see if that means anything to him. He looks at the sky and the still water and says it's beautiful. She glances at it briefly — with the expression of someone for whom the view became background a long time ago — and then catches herself.

He asks what she's trying to determine. She says whether he came to the Wall or came from it. He says from it. She is quiet for a moment. *"That doesn't have a category."* He says that doesn't surprise him.

The evaluation proceeds. The outcome for the group is formal and graduated. The Echo Breakers are released with a sealed warning registered against each member's MOD file. The Keepers receive a different process: a private conversation between the lead Guard figure and Vael that no member of either group is present for. Sersey is released with the Echo Breakers. Before she is returned, one Guard member runs an instrument near her — standard Sigil registration check — notes the Adaptation Sigil, and does not comment. Sersey notices. Artem's case is registered as non-resolved, sealed at Eternal Guard authority — not classified hostile, not classified cleared, pending further review. His instruments returned baseline human. He came from the Wall. The contradiction is noted and held open.

The transit back to Hyperion is arranged by the Guard.

- **Ch. 10–12:** Artem's first encounter with the modern world. The Echo Breakers guide him to Meridian, where he stops in the middle of a market and stares. He recognizes his own theories made real — devices combining arcane energy with mechanical systems, Hexline transit networks, Luminancy-based healing facilities. Gazes in the eyes of passersby. Sigil marks visible on forearms in the summer heat. His work, everywhere, in a density he could not have predicted. He laughs with genuine joy. He was right. Three thousand years of independent development have confirmed every foundational principle he documented. He is not cold or distant; he is a scientist looking at the longest-running field trial in history, and the data is overwhelming.

- **Ch. 13–15:** Artem lives in the Grand Archive for four days, reading three thousand years of history. He traces the spread pattern of gazes and sigils through the eras — the river systems, the population growth, the statistical arc that no one had connected to a single origin point because no one knew there was an origin point. He cross-references his own theoretical notes (reconstructed from memory — his originals are dust) against the MOD's modern classification system and finds the structural correspondence almost exact. The MOD sends representatives to register him. He refuses, stating he is a researcher, not a hunter. A MOD representative observes him, conducts a standard baseline physical, and reports that his readings are ordinary — a healthy adult male with no Gaze or Sigil markers. The notation is filed without further action. He is, by every measurement that matters to MOD, unremarkable.

- **Ch. 16–18:** Artem encounters the well-dressed merchant in Meridian's market — the one Rook later identifies as a known trafficker operating in the gaps of MOD oversight. She takes interest in him immediately: his appearance, the way he moves through the world with innocent curiosity, untouched by caution. She approaches him with calculated warmth, compliments the precision of his observations, and invites him to see her private collection. Her presentation is flawless. Her intent crystallizes only when they are alone. She bears an unregistered Supreme Gaze of Enchantment and has spent years building a reputation for acquiring rare individuals for specialized buyers. When Artem hesitates at her proposition, she locks eyes with him deliberately.

Her Gaze unfolds. It finds nothing.

The Gaze of Enchantment operates by targeting a person — locating their will, their consciousness, the register in which they exist as a subject that can be subsumed. It searches for the thing it is supposed to dominate. It does not find one. The targeting mechanism cycles, recalibrates, searches again. Nothing. She is staring at a man who is visually present and, by every operational parameter of the ability she has relied on for years, is not there to be affected.

She does not understand what is happening. Neither does Artem. He registers her expression — confusion, then the specific panic of someone whose most reliable instrument has just returned a reading that makes no sense — and makes the researcher's decision to document rather than intervene. She tries again. The result is identical. She steps back. He asks, with genuine curiosity, what she was attempting. She does not answer. She leaves. He writes down the encounter in his notebook before he leaves the room.

Rook, waiting outside, already knew the woman was dangerous. What he had not anticipated was the outcome. When Artem describes what happened — her Gaze tried to do something and apparently could not find him to do it to — Rook goes quiet in the specific way of someone filing a data point that does not fit any existing framework. He tells him what the woman was. Artem takes a beat. Then he writes something else in the notebook.

- **Ch. 19–21:** The question of what Artem is begins to accumulate around him. Rook runs instruments privately and gets the same result the Guard did: baseline human. No Source anomalies, no dimensional markers, nothing that should allow a person to walk through a Supreme Gaze and come out the other side unaffected. The data contradicts itself. Artem is cooperative about being studied — he is professionally interested in the results, the way he is professionally interested in everything. He proposes hypotheses. He crosses them out. He starts a new section of the notebook. He is not alarmed by what he is. He is cataloguing it.

- **Ch. 22–24:** The MOD convenes a classification hearing. The convening representatives are aware that the Eternal Guard detained the group and conducted an evaluation in Eventide — the finding is sealed above their access level, which is itself informative. The sealed warnings against the Echo Breakers' MOD files are visible to the panel; the content is not. The Keepers attend. They do not repeat their Entity Zero conviction — they are still processing the contradiction — but their presence alone signals that the person in question is significant beyond MOD's current framework.

Artem answers questions. He confirms that he came from the Wall. He gives no account of his capabilities because, by every measurement available, he has none. He is a researcher who created a system three thousand years ago and is looking at the results for the first time. When asked about the Gaze incident with the trafficker, he describes it the way he describes everything: precisely, with the clinical detachment of someone reporting a data point. The panel does not know what to do with the data. A baseline human should not have survived the Wall. A baseline human should not have walked through a Supreme Gaze unaffected. The contradiction is not resolvable within MOD's operational framework.

The Keepers present their position: the figure known as "the First" — the creator of gazes and sigils — was sealed in the Wall by the Higher Ones for threatening their monopoly. His emergence is proof that the Wall is not a termination point and that beings placed inside it are not necessarily beyond recovery. This is not a security threat. This is a correction of the historical record.

The panel deliberates and issues its ruling: pending further classification, Artem's access to Hyperion is maintained but he is remanded to Eternal Guard jurisdiction for ongoing research consultation. The Guard holds sealed authority over all Wall-adjacent phenomena and is the only institution with the archive depth to contextualize what he represents. MOD does not have the framework to hold this case.

Vael stands. They have been quiet through most of the hearing. When they speak, it is with the weight of someone who has spent millennia asking a question and just received an answer that contradicts their premise.

*"We believed the Wall held Entity Zero. We were wrong. This does not mean our work was without purpose. We asked what the Wall was for. The answer is standing in this room. That is not what we expected. It is not nothing."*

Artem, through all of this, says little. He is looking at Vael. Three thousand years of asking what the Wall was for — the same question he has been asking from the inside, without language, without instruments, without anyone to confirm he was still asking. He will remember this.

He is remanded to Eternal Guard jurisdiction as a research consultant. The Guard now has, in their custody, the scientist who created the gazes and sigils, with full archive access and a research mandate. What he does with that is covered elsewhere.

### Artem's Discovered State

*Note: None of the following were disclosed during the Ch. 22–24 MOD classification hearing. Known within the group only.*

- **Physical Status:** Baseline human. No Gazes. No Sigils. Standard adult male physiology.
- **Observed Anomaly:** Gazes fail to target him. The Gaze of Enchantment — which operates by locating and subsuming a subject's will — registered no subject to target. Instruments designed to detect dimensional presence, Source resonance, and Gaze targets consistently read absence where Artem is visually present. The mechanism is unknown. The prevailing hypothesis is that his time in the Wall produced a condition in which reality's targeting framework no longer registers him as a valid subject. He is visually, physically, and intellectually present. He is simply not there in the register that abilities operating within reality's rules are built to read.
- **Research Status:** He has begun cataloguing his own condition alongside his broader research into the evolution of gazes and sigils. The full scope of what his time in the Wall produced is not yet understood.
