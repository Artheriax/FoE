# Book VI: Artem's Emergence

*Part of: FoE: Sersey's Journey*

*Artem's Abilities: Unknown → Discovered (Sigil of Transcendent Adaptation, Gaze of Null, Gaze of Mimicry, Gaze of Convergence, Nihility Affinity)*

**Premise:** Artem walks out of the Wall of Nihility into a world that has aged three million years in thirty days. He is a researcher who preceded the universe, carrying abilities he does not understand and a hatred for the beings who imprisoned him. The Echo Breakers guide him through a world built from his descriptions — a world that proved his theories correct without ever knowing he existed. As he discovers the extent of his powers, he must navigate institutions that want to classify him, a Keepers order that believes he is Entity Zero, and his own burning need to find the being the Higher Ones erased.

---

- **Ch. 1–3:** Hours after Artem emerges from the Wall, the Keepers of Nil have secured a transit route away from the Wall's vicinity. Artem refuses their immediate transport to sanctuary, instead asking for several hours alone with Sersey and the Echo Breakers to acclimate to the physical world's sensory input. The transition from Wall-time to physical time has disoriented him more than he expected; his adaptive physiology is recalibrating to a reality with variable inputs. Sersey notices her Sigil behaving strangely in proximity to him — not strain or rejection, but something closer to recognition. When she mentions it, Artem explains that her Sigil carries lineage markers from his own Standard Sigil of Adaptation from before the Wall. His body spent millions of years becoming a native of Nihility's erasure architecture. Her body, carrying the genetic echo of that original Sigil, recognizes the lineage source. It is not a bond but an acknowledgment: her Sigil knows it descends from his.

- **Ch. 4–6:** Artem's first encounter with the modern world. The Echo Breakers guide him to Meridian, where he stops in the middle of a market and stares. He recognizes his own theories made real — devices combining arcane energy with mechanical systems, Hexline transit networks, Luminancy-based healing facilities. He laughs with genuine joy, realizing his life's work was prophetic. He is not cold or distant; he is a kid in a candy store, overwhelmed by a world that proved him right. The Keepers arrange for him to access the Grand Archive.

- **Ch. 7–9:** Artem lives in the Grand Archive for four days, reading 3000 years of history since his time before meeting Entity Zero. The MOD sends representatives to register him; he refuses, stating he is a researcher, not a hunter. A MOD representative observes him and reports that his baseline resilience exceeds any recorded measurement. Dr. Kayleth, a Sigil researcher, arrives and determines that Artem's body adapts to damage faster than it can accumulate — his Sigil evolved during millions of years of exposure to the Wall's erasure. Testing reveals he can withstand X-rank elemental attacks without injury.

- **Ch. 10–12:** Artem discovers his first Gaze when he realizes he copied Sersey's Gaze of Inner Thought during the dungeon breach near the Keepers' sanctuary. He demonstrates by reading Thorne's surface thoughts. Rook theorizes this is an Ethereal-ranked ability. The second Gaze discovers itself when Sersey tests her Gaze near Artem and he instinctively activates the grey eye, deactivating her Gaze entirely. He names it the Gaze of Null. Rook determines it can perceive and deactivate any Gaze-based ability temporarily.

- **Ch. 13–15:** Artem encounters a well-dressed merchant in Meridian's market who takes interest in him immediately — not his abilities, but his appearance and the way he moves through the world with innocent curiosity, untouched by caution. She approaches him with calculated warmth, compliments the precision of his observations, and invites him to see her private collection. Her presentation is flawless; her intent crystallizes only when they are alone. She bears an unregistered Supreme Gaze of Enchantment and has spent years building a reputation for acquiring rare individuals and finding "specialized buyers" willing to pay significantly for unique acquisitions. Elves, voidlings, astrae, kitsune, demons, and dark elves command premium prices, but a being who moves like nothing she has encountered before might fetch considerably more. When Artem hesitates at her proposition, she locks eyes with him deliberately, and her Gaze unfolds — his will begins to sublimate to hers, and he experiences the mechanism clearly for the first time. It fascinates him enough that he allows it to continue for longer than is safe. Then his grey eye opens. The Gaze of Null suppresses her Enchantment mid-activation, and she feels the thread snap. Panic flickers across her face. Artem, now understanding the mechanism perfectly, rotates his other eye and copies her Supreme Gaze precisely. When he meets her stare again bearing her own Gaze, his version operates at identical power. She experiences exactly what she intended for him — her will sublimating to his — and the terror is absolute. He holds it long enough for her to understand the permanence of what she attempted, then releases her and walks away. Rook, waiting outside, already knew what was happening; she recognized the merchant as a known trafficker working the gaps in MOD oversight. The encounter teaches Artem that his innocence in a new world makes him prey to those who traffic in the rare and uncatalogued. Artem inquires to Rook about how bad the trafficking problem is and which races are most targeted. Hearing this Artem has a visceral reaction — not just intellectual understanding, but a deep, personal revulsion. Artem always loved those races as part of his research, and the idea of them being exploited in this way is abhorrent to him. Rook reminds him that this problem is worldwide and too deep. To stop this it would require a level of institutional reform and resource allocation that is currently unimaginable. The question is: Power. He can copy gazes, nullify gazes of those who prey on others, and adapt to threats faster than they can harm him. He has the power to make a difference. He just needs to copy more powerful gazes until he can find them all without even traveling to all the places in the world.

- **Ch. 16–18:** Artem discovers his third Gaze — the Gaze of Convergence — when he instinctively merges the Gaze of Null with a mimicked Gaze, creating a compound effect that both replicates and cancels abilities simultaneously. Rook theorizes this is unprecedented; no documentation describes a Gaze that can merge other Gazes. Artem realizes he holds three Gazes simultaneously, which exceeds the documented ceiling for mortal Gaze bearing. He also discovers his affinity for Nihility magic — the Wall's fundamental nature resonates within him, granting him access to Wall of Nihility magic and Nil space magic.

- **Ch. 19–20:** The MOD convenes a hearing to classify Artem. He presents his abilities and origins, establishing that he predates the MOD by three million years and cannot be classified by their reference frame. The Keepers attend, still believing he is Entity Zero. Artem demonstrates his abilities and explains the distinction between himself and Entity Zero. The hearing ends with Artem classified as a "Phenomena Requiring Special Consideration" — outside the MOD's framework but not hostile. He leaves the hearing with a new understanding: the Higher Ones imprisoned him and erased Entity Zero, and he will find her to tell her what really happened.

### Artem's Discovered Abilities

- **Sigil of Transcendent Adaptation (Ethereal):** Evolved from Standard Sigil of Adaptation during millions of years in the Wall. Adapts to any threat faster than damage can accumulate. Grants baseline resilience that exceeds any recorded measurement.
- **Gaze of Null (Ethereal):** Grey eye. Perceives and deactivates any Gaze-based ability temporarily. Does not remove the ability; simply turns it off.
- **Gaze of Mimicry (Ethereal):** Shifting eye. Copies any Gaze from observation alone. Functions at the same level as the original. Requires comprehension, not sustained exposure.
- **Gaze of Convergence (Ethereal):** Merges mimicked Gazes with each other or with the Gaze of Null, producing more powerful compound effects. No documented equivalent.
- **Nihility Affinity:** Resonance with the Wall of Nihility's fundamental nature. Grants access to Wall of Nihility magic and Nil space magic. Mechanism not yet understood.
