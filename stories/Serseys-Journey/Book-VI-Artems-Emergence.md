# Book VI: Artem's Emergence

*Part of: FoE: Sersey's Journey*

*Artem's Abilities: Unknown → Discovered (Sigil of Transcendent Adaptation, Gaze of Null, Gaze of Mimicry, Gaze of Convergence, Nihility Affinity)*

**Premise:** Artem walks out of the Wall of Nihility into a world that has aged three million years in thirty days. He is a researcher who preceded the universe, carrying abilities he does not understand and a hatred for the beings who imprisoned him. The Echo Breakers guide him through a world built from his descriptions — a world that proved his theories correct without ever knowing he existed. As he discovers the extent of his powers, he must navigate institutions that want to classify him, a Keepers order that believes he is Entity Zero, and his own burning need to find the being the Higher Ones erased.

---

- **Ch. 1–3:** Artem exits the Wall and immediately loses consciousness. The crossing is instantaneous and total — millions of years of sensory nullity replaced by full physical-reality input with no gradation — and even his adaptive physiology cannot absorb that transition without a complete reset. He clears the Wall's edge and collapses into the outer boundary of Nihility, insensate. The Keepers of Nil, already stationed at the transit perimeter, secure his unconscious form and prepare for transport. The Echo Breakers form up around him. Sersey, moving into position near the transit formation, notices her Sigil responding strangely before she can identify what it is — not pain, not rejection, but something older than the behavior she has learned to assign to it. She doesn't have time to sit with the question. Artem doesn't wake. The Keepers begin the transit back through Nihility's outer edge.

- **Ch. 4–6:** Three hours into the transit, the Keepers' lead guide stops moving. The reason isn't detectible by ordinary senses — it's something the Keepers know from their research the way other organizations know warning signs: a specific quality of dimensional disturbance that registers as incoming authority rather than incoming threat. She holds position. The remaining Keepers follow without being told. Vael, near the back, moves to the front with the particular stillness of someone who has spent millennia avoiding exactly this kind of notice and has just understood the avoidance is over.

Six figures approach through a fold in the substrate that should not be accessible from the physical plane. No guild marks, no MOD insignia, no house colors. The lead figure presents a warrant seal. Sersey doesn't recognize it. Gaelira does. Thorne moves in front of Sersey before he consciously decides to. Lyra stands completely still in the way that means she is calculating whether fear is something she can afford right now. Rook is already cataloguing exit options he knows don't exist. The Keepers step out of the way without being told.

The lead figure addresses the group collectively. Not any individual. Collectively — which is worse.

*"You entered an active access zone adjacent to the Wall of Nihility. Your transit signature registered above the threshold for incidental observation. You are being held for evaluation pending determination of intent and disposition."*

That is all. The Keepers don't protest. They know what this is. The Echo Breakers understand enough to stay quiet. Nobody mentions the unconscious figure on the transport frame, because nobody knows where to begin.

The group is taken to Eventide.

- **Ch. 7–9:** Eventide arrives as sensation before it arrives as sight: air that carries no temperature in either direction, a complete stillness, and a quality of light that makes everything visible without warmth. The substrate fold deposits the group onto a wide platform of ancient dark stone above a channel of water that does not flow — its surface holding the violet-indigo sky in perfect reflection. No sun. Stars visible through perpetual twilight. At distances across the island, other figures move with the particular efficiency of people occupied with their own lives. It is beautiful. Nobody in the group says so, because they have just been detained.

The group is separated for evaluation. The Keepers are processed first — they have the most to account for, and the Guard knows who they are even if no formal record acknowledges the organization's existence. The Echo Breakers are held separately as registered MOD hunters whose presence in a sealed dimension is a serious irregularity but carries different institutional weight than the Keepers' position. Sersey remains in proximity to Artem because the Guard hasn't yet decided which category she belongs to.

Artem is still unconscious when they arrive. The member assigned to his case doesn't handle threats — she handles anomalies. The distinction exists in the Guard's internal structure because sometimes what arrives in a sealed access zone is not a violation but a category that doesn't have a name yet, and naming it requires a different kind of attention. She reviews the transit record, the failed instrument results, and the Wall-crossing signature — which is unlike any crossing signature she has encountered in her full tenure — and then begins the assessment.

First instrument: nothing stable. His Sigil reconfigures around the scan in real time. Second instrument: nothing at all. She closes it and sits with this for a moment. Then he wakes.

Artem opens his eyes to the violet-indigo sky of Eventide, to ancient dark stone beneath him, to still water below the platform edge holding the sky in mirror — and to a woman crouched near him, a stylus hovering over a document she has not written in, watching him with the expression of someone who came here to process a violation and found something that doesn't fit the form.

He has not processed sensory input in millions of years. Everything arrives simultaneously and without gradation. He holds still and lets it resolve.

*"You're awake,"* she says. Not a greeting. More like something she had been wondering about.

He blinks. She is still watching him with that focused engagement — not waiting on a report, just looking. He can tell, with the precision of someone who spent millions of years reading a language no one else knew, that she does not know what he is. She has the expression of someone building a hypothesis rather than applying one.

*"What are you?"* she asks. It comes out before whatever procedural opener she was supposed to use, and her tone makes clear she knows the order. She doesn't correct it.

Artem, managing the sensory experience of having a body again, tells her he was a researcher. She says that's not what she meant. He says he knows. He asks what this place is. She says Eventide, and watches to see if that means anything to him. He looks at the sky and the still water below the platform edge and says it's beautiful. She glances at it briefly — with the expression of someone for whom the view became background a long time ago — and then catches herself.

He asks what she's trying to determine. She says whether he came to the Wall or came from it. He says from it. She is quiet for a moment. *"That doesn't have a category."* He says that doesn't surprise him.

The evaluation proceeds. The outcome for the group is formal and graduated. The Echo Breakers are released with a sealed warning registered against each member's MOD file — the Guard's notation will appear as a seal MOD can see and cannot read, a permanent record of an incident MOD cannot investigate. The Keepers receive a different process: a private conversation between the lead Guard figure and Vael that no member of either group is present for, after which Vael is very quiet for the rest of their time in Eventide. What was said is not disclosed. What it produces in Vael is a stillness that even the other Keepers know not to interrupt. Sersey is released with the Echo Breakers. Before she is returned, one Guard member pauses at her Sigil reading, checks the notation twice, and does not comment. She notices. Artem's case is registered as non-resolved, sealed at Eternal Guard authority — not classified hostile, not classified cleared, pending further review above MOD access. The woman delivers this last part in the formal register required for such statements, and Artem notices the shift in her voice and files it away.

The transit back to Hyperion is arranged by the Guard and does not use the Keepers' routes. Nobody in the group has taken it before. On the way back, Artem asks Sersey about the sensation she has been noticing in her Sigil. He explains it before she finishes describing it: her Sigil carries lineage markers from his own Sigil of Adaptation, the Standard-tier ability he held before the Wall. His body spent millions of years inside the Wall's erasure architecture and evolved beyond recognition; hers, descended from the same source, knows where it came from. It is not a bond. It is an acknowledgment: her Sigil knows it descends from his.

- **Ch. 10–12:** Artem's first encounter with the modern world. The Echo Breakers guide him to Meridian, where he stops in the middle of a market and stares. He recognizes his own theories made real — devices combining arcane energy with mechanical systems, Hexline transit networks, Luminancy-based healing facilities. He laughs with genuine joy, realizing his life's work was prophetic. He is not cold or distant; he is a kid in a candy store, overwhelmed by a world that proved him right. The Keepers arrange for him to access the Grand Archive.

- **Ch. 13–15:** Artem lives in the Grand Archive for four days, reading 3000 years of history since his time before meeting Entity Zero. The MOD sends representatives to register him; he refuses, stating he is a researcher, not a hunter. A MOD representative observes him and reports that his baseline resilience exceeds any recorded measurement. Dr. Kayleth, a Sigil researcher, arrives and determines that Artem's body adapts to damage faster than it can accumulate — his Sigil evolved during millions of years of exposure to the Wall's erasure. Testing reveals he can withstand X-rank elemental attacks without injury.

- **Ch. 16–18:** Artem discovers his first Gaze when he realizes he copied Sersey's Gaze of Inner Thought during the dungeon breach near the Keepers' sanctuary. He demonstrates by reading Thorne's surface thoughts. Rook theorizes this is an Ethereal-ranked ability. The second Gaze discovers itself when Sersey tests her Gaze near Artem and he instinctively activates the grey eye, deactivating her Gaze entirely. He names it the Gaze of Null. Rook determines it can perceive and deactivate any Gaze-based ability that tries to affect him.

- **Ch. 19–21:** Artem encounters a well-dressed merchant in Meridian's market who takes interest in him immediately — not his abilities, but his appearance and the way he moves through the world with innocent curiosity, untouched by caution. She approaches him with calculated warmth, compliments the precision of his observations, and invites him to see her private collection. Her presentation is flawless; her intent crystallizes only when they are alone. She bears an unregistered Supreme Gaze of Enchantment and has spent years building a reputation for acquiring rare individuals and finding "specialized buyers" willing to pay significantly for unique acquisitions. Elves, voidlings, astrae, kitsune, demons, and dark elves command premium prices, but a being who moves like nothing she has encountered before might fetch considerably more. When Artem hesitates at her proposition, she locks eyes with him deliberately, and her Gaze unfolds — his will begins to sublimate to hers, and he experiences the mechanism clearly for the first time. It fascinates him enough that he allows it to continue for longer than is safe. Then his grey eye opens. The Gaze of Null suppresses her Enchantment mid-activation, and she feels the thread snap. Panic flickers across her face. Artem, now understanding the mechanism perfectly, rotates his other eye and copies her Supreme Gaze precisely. When he meets her stare again bearing her own Gaze, his version operates at identical power. She experiences exactly what she intended for him — her will sublimating to his — and the terror is absolute. He holds it long enough for her to understand the permanence of what she attempted, then releases her and walks away. Rook, waiting outside, already knew what was happening; she recognized the merchant as a known trafficker working the gaps in MOD oversight. The encounter teaches Artem that his innocence in a new world makes him prey to those who traffic in the rare and uncatalogued. Artem inquires to Rook about how bad the trafficking problem is and which races are most targeted. Hearing this Artem has a visceral reaction — not just intellectual understanding, but a deep, personal revulsion. Artem always loved those races as part of his research, and the idea of them being exploited in this way is abhorrent to him. Rook reminds him that this problem is worldwide and too deep. To stop this it would require a level of institutional reform and resource allocation that is currently unimaginable. The question is: Power. He can copy gazes, nullify gazes of those who prey on others, and adapt to threats faster than they can harm him. He has the power to make a difference. He just needs to copy more powerful gazes until he can find them all without even traveling to all the places in the world.

- **Ch. 22–24:** Artem discovers his third Gaze — the Gaze of Convergence — when he instinctively merges the Gaze of Null with a mimicked Gaze, creating a compound effect that both replicates and cancels abilities simultaneously. Rook theorizes this is unprecedented; no documentation describes a Gaze that can merge other Gazes. Artem realizes he holds three Gazes simultaneously, which exceeds the documented ceiling for mortal Gaze bearing. He also discovers his affinity for Nihility magic — the Wall's fundamental nature resonates within him, granting him access to Wall of Nihility magic and Nil space magic.

- **Ch. 25–26:** The MOD convenes a classification hearing. The convening representatives are aware that the Eternal Guard detained the group and conducted an evaluation in Eventide — the finding is sealed above their access level, which is itself informative. The sealed warnings against the Echo Breakers' MOD files are visible to the panel; the content is not. The Keepers attend. They believe he is Entity Zero. They have not said this before a formal institutional body until today.

Artem answers questions. He confirms that he came from the Wall. He gives no account of his capabilities — he is a researcher, and three million years of studying the nature of power is enough to understand what full disclosure in this room would produce. Every form of power in MOD's framework exists somewhere on a scale it can measure, regulate, or suppress. He does not give them a scale to use. MOD's concern sharpens not around what he says but around the premise he represents: he entered the world through the Wall of Nihility. Not from Nihility, not from a sealed dimension with a known access profile — from the Wall itself. Nothing has ever done that. The Wall is defined as the boundary condition at which existence ends. He is evidence that the definition is incomplete, and incomplete definitions are the category that produces the worst outcomes.

The Keepers present their position: he is Entity Zero, or the entity the Wall was constructed to contain, and his emergence indicates that what was done to him was done unjustly and without public record. This argument is intended as advocacy. It is received as the opposite — Entity Zero is not a category that produces sympathetic rulings, and the Keepers have just named him the most dangerous theoretical figure in MOD's cosmological framework.

The panel deliberates and issues its ruling: pending further classification, Artem's access to Hyperion is revoked as a precautionary measure. Jurisdiction is forwarded to the Eternal Guard, which holds sealed research authority over all Wall-adjacent phenomena and has more relevant operational experience. MOD does not have the institutional framework to hold this case.

Vael stands.

He has already been in a prison. Millions of years inside the Wall — the same Wall this institution will not study, cannot explain, and has no record of anyone surviving. Nothing came from beyond it until he did, and the justification for his original imprisonment has never been made available to anyone present. The answer to not understanding something — another cage — is not classification. It is repetition.

The panel does not respond to this directly. The ruling stands.

Artem, through all of this, says nothing. He is looking at Vael. Three million years in the Wall, and the being arguing against another imprisonment is the same being who spent those years asking what the Wall was for — who refused the institutional answer of *stop asking* before most of the institutions in this room existed. He will remember this.

He is remanded to Eternal Guard jurisdiction.

### Artem's Discovered Abilities

*Note: None of the following were disclosed during the Ch. 25–26 MOD classification hearing. Known within the group only.*

- **Sigil of Transcendent Adaptation (Ethereal):** Evolved from Standard Sigil of Adaptation during millions of years in the Wall. Adapts to any threat faster than damage can accumulate. Grants baseline resilience that exceeds any recorded measurement.
- **Gaze of Null (Ethereal):** Grey eye. Perceives and deactivates any Gaze-based ability temporarily. Does not remove the ability; simply turns it off.
- **Gaze of Mimicry (Ethereal):** Shifting eye. Copies any Gaze from observation alone. Functions at the same level as the original. Requires comprehension, not sustained exposure.
- **Gaze of Convergence (Ethereal):** Merges mimicked Gazes with each other or with the Gaze of Null, producing more powerful compound effects. No documented equivalent.
- **Nihility Affinity:** Resonance with the Wall of Nihility's fundamental nature. Grants access to Wall of Nihility magic and Nil space magic. Mechanism not yet understood.
