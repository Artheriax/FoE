# Between the Wall and the World

*A standalone arc following Artem. Runs parallel to FoE: Sersey's Journey, Books VII–VIII.*

*Preceded by: Origins.md | Book VI: Artem's Emergence*

**Premise:** The Eternal Guard remanded Artem to their jurisdiction on the grounds that nothing has ever come through the Wall and MOD had no framework for what he was. What they did not anticipate is that a researcher who spent thirty days in subjective time mapping the Wall of Nihility from the inside would not, in Eventide, stop doing what he has always done: observe, document, and ask questions until the shape of the problem resolves. His situation is not a prison. Eventide is a home — an occupied, lived-in one — and the Guard begin as his classifiers and slowly become something else. Seravane is his colleague first and his closest friend by the end of it. Velin has decided, with the specific determination of someone who has decided, that this man's condition of not being with anyone is a correctable problem and she is going to correct it. Artem finds this more amusing than alarming, which is not the reaction she expected. The world built from his descriptions is outside the island's edge. He is learning it from the inside out, one archived record at a time, and the records are beginning to contain his name — and someone else's, and that is the part that matters.

---

- **Ch. 1–3:** The first morning. Artem wakes in the rooms the Guard assigned him — at the edge of one of Eventide's lower islands, comfortable, personal in the way of a space long enough inhabited to leave the previous resident's traces. He finds the kitchen. There is coffee, a variety that did not exist in his era, and he finds this completely acceptable. He sits down with his notebook and maps the island's geometry from the window because mapping is the reflex that survived everything else. The notebook is current-era materials and he has filled three-quarters of it since Meridian. He adds *new notebook* to a mental list that now also includes: find the archive, identify who here can be asked questions without institutional caution, and determine whether Seravane is going to stand in the doorway every time she visits or eventually come inside. She knocks before entering. She stands in the doorway both times she comes that first day. He asks if she intends to keep doing that. She says she is not sure yet. He says she can come in. She does. Her instruments are already out.

- **Ch. 4–6:** Training begins. The Guard's orientation for new jurisdictional residents is not gentle and not hostile — it is efficient. A sequence of assessments across multiple disciplines: dimensional sensing, threat-response evaluation, Source capability measurement, institutional protocol orientation. The Source gauge fails on Artem in a way that produces an immediate, unscheduled consultation among the evaluators. The dimensional sensing output is written down, stared at, and slid to a colleague without comment. He completes each session cooperatively and with the attentiveness of someone who finds the assessments genuinely interesting as data. He takes notes. In the evenings he goes back to his rooms and adds to a separate section of the notebook — private, not for the Guard's debriefs. He encountered three Guard members with Gazes he had not seen before. He now has two of those Gazes that he did not have this morning. He runs the first test in his bathroom mirror with the door closed. The Mimicry eye shifts. He writes twelve words. He underlines three of them.

- **Ch. 7–9:** Velin. She introduces herself on the fourth training day in the way of someone who has decided to do a thing and is doing it without preamble. She is a Succubus, which she states before he could ask and with the complete ease of someone who stopped waiting for people to finish processing it before continuing the conversation. She is also, she explains while settling into a training position beside him that was not assigned to her, on a personal mission. She does not clarify the mission. She does not need to; the mission is self-evident within about forty seconds and becomes more so every time she says *Sigil of Endurance* with the specific intonation she uses for those three words. Her Endurance Sigil is visible on her forearms — deep bronze, steady, doesn't pulse — and she mentions its advantages in a context that generates a two-second silence from the other Guard members present and an expression of complete innocence from her that fills it. Artem watches her demonstrate a training technique, writes *Velin — Supreme Gaze of Enchantment (Mimicry pending: mechanism first) — Supreme Sigil of Endurance — mission: ongoing and obvious — note: do not look directly at eyes until mechanism confirmed,* and adds it to the private section. She glances at his notebook. He closes it. She smiles. He does not yet understand exactly what he is dealing with but he is already more interested than he expected to be.

- **Ch. 10–12:** The accident. Three weeks into training, a Guard member running a precision exercise in an adjacent corridor produces a spatial miscalculation. An Absolute Gaze activates at close range, redirected. Artem is in the corridor. The grey eye opens. It has never been a decision; it was installed by the Wall itself as the only viable response to a process designed to erase him, and it remains automatic. The Absolute Gaze deactivates mid-activation. The Guard member stops. Seravane is around the corner and hears the silence. Practitioners who have worked at Absolute tier know what a Gaze stopping sounds like — the specific absence where a force was. She rounds the corner. One grey eye, one ordinary eye, and Artem noting the Guard member's expression with what appears to be academic interest. She is already writing before she has fully processed what she saw. The formal notation goes into his file that evening: *Gaze of Null, confirmed. Left eye documented. Right eye: presents as ordinary at rest, no detectable signature, mechanism unknown. Anomaly flagged for ongoing assessment.* The Guard member who triggered the incident submits a one-paragraph incident report and, in the notes field, writes: *I don't think he was surprised.*

- **Ch. 13–15:** The arcane assessment. Standard evaluation protocol for jurisdictional residents includes a full arcane affinity survey — school alignment, amplitude, any forbidden-school resonance. The evaluator runs Artem's survey and then recalibrates the instrument because the output is unusual. The recalibrated instrument produces the same output. Every arcane school tested registers at maximum measurable affinity, including the six forbidden schools, which should require an entirely separate procedural escalation under standard protocol. The evaluator submits the results and an incident flag simultaneously. The escalation reaches Seravane within the hour. She reviews it and then goes to find Artem, who is in the kitchen. She puts the survey results in front of him. He looks at them. He asks: *is this a normal survey output?* She says: no. He says: *what happens when a forbidden school registers high?* She says: normally, MOD executes the practitioner. He considers this. He says: *convenient that I am not under MOD jurisdiction.* She says this is not funny. He says he is genuinely not sure it was a joke. The Guard formally offers him the position of Nihility expert and Wall of Nihility research consultant within two days — partly because of the survey results, partly because his theoretical knowledge of the Wall's structure is unlike anything in their archive, and partly because having him somewhere with a formal research mandate is easier to manage than having him somewhere without one. He accepts. He now has access to the archive.

- **Ch. 16–18:** The archive. He sits down with the pre-record holdings on the first afternoon and does not move for six hours. Seravane comes back to find him still there, surrounded by three pages of handwritten notes and two archival documents held carefully at the edges with the technique of someone trained on materials older than these. She asks what he found. He says: *gaps. Pre-record accounts that stop in the middle. References to a cosmological formation event and then the next document is several eras later and written by someone who does not understand what they are referencing.* She says: some of the sealed continuations require her clearance level to access. She pulls up a chair. She opens them. They read in silence for a long time. In the margin of his notes he writes one word and then crosses it out. Later that week, Velin — who has noticed his archive hours and adjusted by simply being present in adjacent research rooms with the frequency of someone whose campaign has relocated to better terrain — asks him at dinner what he is looking for. He says something true but incomplete, the way he always does when someone asks a question he is not ready to answer fully. She lets it stand. She says: *you are very good at not answering questions.* He says: *you are very good at noticing when questions aren't answered.* She smiles the particular smile of someone who has been correctly observed and is not finished being interesting.

- **Ch. 19–21:** Velin's campaign. The mission has not slowed. She is creative, patient, and operating on a timeline that her Sigil ensures she will never tire of. Her approach this week involves ensuring she and Artem are assigned adjacent positions in every training exercise — which requires minor creative reinterpretation of the duty roster, which she handles with the specific innocent expression she reserves for situations where she has clearly done something. Artem has noted the pattern in writing. He has also noted that her Enchantment Gaze — which shows each viewer their ideal eyes — has never been directly tested on him because he has been careful not to look into it squarely. He decides to do something about that. He asks her, one afternoon after training, whether she would give him permission to deactivate the Gaze briefly. She asks why. He says he wants to see what her eyes actually look like. She goes quiet in a way that is different from her normal silence, which tends to have the quality of a person preparing the next thing they intend to say. He says he is not trying to be rude; he is curious about the mechanism, and the mechanism requires seeing the baseline. She says yes. He opens the grey eye very slightly — not a full Null activation, narrow and directed, the way the training has been teaching him to focus it. The Gaze of Enchantment above the threshold of his suppression field deactivates. Her real eyes are a dark amber with a particular quality of refracted light — not beautiful in the designed way of the Gaze, but specific, and hers. He looks at them for about four seconds and then says: *amber, lighter toward the center. I expected the Gaze's mechanism would change the iris color entirely. It doesn't.* She says nothing. He closes the grey eye. She blinks. Her face has a quality he has not seen on it before and he files it under *interesting* with the particular emphasis his notation system reserves for things he intends to return to.

- **Ch. 22–24:** The recognition. Seravane has been running her own archive thread — not the same documents Artem read, but a parallel investigation through the Guard's cosmological record collection, cross-referencing formation-event accounts from multiple eras. She finds the continuity she was looking for in a sealed Second Era document interpreting oral Pre-Record accounts. She reads it three times. Then she reads it four more times. Then she goes to find Artem. She puts the document on the kitchen table between them and does not say anything. He reads it all the way through, which takes forty seconds. The accounts are fragmentary and wrong in places — Third Era scholars recording what they thought they understood about something they could not have understood. The Wall imprisonment is recorded as *peaceful containment at the request of the cosmic foundation for the bearer's own stability,* which is The One's version and apparently the version that survived into institutional record. He reads that line twice. His expression does not change but the quality of the silence that comes after it does. Seravane asks: *is that you.* He says: *it doesn't say my name anywhere in there.* She says: *Artem.* He says, more quietly: *yes.* She writes nothing for a moment. He asks: *what does it say happened to her.* Not to him. She checks the document. The records describe Entity Zero withdrawing from active presence in the cosmos following the containment event. The language is careful — *grief-driven withdrawal* in one source, *voluntary recession* in another. He is quiet for a long time. She sets the document down. He says, eventually: *she believed them. They told her I was sleeping. She withdrew because she thought I was safe.* He does not say anything else for a while. Seravane makes two mugs of coffee and puts one in front of him. He picks it up. Then she asks him to tell her what actually happened — not the document's version. His. He takes a long drink of the coffee and then he does. He was there when she coalesced. He taught her what an ocean was, what fire was, what music was — that last one undid her completely, he says, because music was organized sensation that meant nothing and everything simultaneously and she could not explain why that mattered to her so much. He taught her and she built. The Higher Ones emerged from aspects of him — the Observing One from his curiosity, the Judging One from his accountability, the Creating One from his delight in function. He knew each of them before they had names, because each of them was built from a piece of him given independent will. The physics of this universe are his explanations. The logical framework of how matter works is the framework he used to describe matter before any matter existed to describe. He was not present at the beginning. He was the prior document from which the beginning was compiled. Seravane stops writing. Her pen is still in her hand. For a long moment she is very still — the specific stillness of a researcher who has just received data that recontextualizes every prior data point in her field. The man who has been drinking coffee in her kitchen since week three. The man whose instruments fail on. The man she came to Eventide's lower islands to classify and instead befriended, and found archive records for, and sat in silence beside while he read four thousand years of history in four days. He is the prior document. Her hands do not shake. She has trained too long for them to shake. The quality of her attention shifts to something sharper and more careful — the attentiveness of someone who has just understood that everything she thought she was studying was the footnote, not the text. She stares at the table for a long time. He says: *I know what you are going to do.* She says: I have to. He says: *I know. I would too.* She goes to the high Guard that evening. What happens in that meeting is sealed at a classification level the Guard's public institutional documentation does not name. The notation on Artem's file changes overnight — the line reading *non-resolved, pending, above MOD access* is removed and replaced with something that, below the seal, is seventeen words long. Three days later, a senior Guard member Artem has never spoken to nods to him in a corridor with the specific quality of attention that very old practitioners develop when they are in the presence of something they cannot fully process but have decided to be careful around. Artem notes it. He makes the notation and continues to the kitchen. She writes one thing in her own working notes. When he reads it later, that one thing is: *she withdrew because she thought he was safe.* He does not ask her to stop.

- **Ch. 25–27:** The authorization. Four months into the arc, with his position as Nihility research consultant settled and his notebook's private section running to a second volume, Seravane comes to his door in the evening at a later hour than usual. She has the quality of someone who has been deciding how to say something for several hours. She tells him: the Sleeper Incident has a Modern Era address. She tells him what the transmission contained and how it arrived. She tells him that among the people in the affected location there is someone who has been dreamworked into a compliance structure and has been carrying it for months. She does not tell him which group. He already knows — the sealed warning notation on a MOD file is specific in its architecture, and he has been reading Guard case structures for months. He asks: *what is being asked of me.* She says: the Guard can neutralize a Mindscape bearer in standoff conditions. The dreamwork in affected minds does not reverse when the active Gaze deactivates. We have no instrument that reaches inside that. He says: *you are asking me to copy a Gaze I have not seen yet.* She says: when you have seen it, can you use it in reverse. He thinks about this. He says: *yes.* She says: the Guard knows about the Null Gaze. He says: *I know what you know.* She says: I know you know more than that. He looks at her. She looks back. He says: *that's the correct answer.* He opens his notebook to a blank page and writes one line at the top. She asks what it says. He says he will tell her when they get back. She says she will have his operational briefing ready in the morning. He says: *bring coffee.*

- **Closing:** The arc ends not on the mission but on the preparation. Artem spends the evening before deployment in his rooms, the notebook open to the page he wrote the line on. The line reads: *Mindscape (Absolute) — mechanism: projection of consciousness into multiple minds simultaneously via direct neural/spiritual interface. Reversal: same mechanism, opposite direction — project extraction rather than projection implantation. Requires comprehension of target mechanism before copying. Requires the target to be present. Requires the target to be active.* Below it, in the margin, in smaller handwriting: *the dream is a structure. structures can be mapped. if it was built by a Gaze, it can be unmade by the same Gaze.* He closes the notebook. Looks at the second one — the private section — and does not open it. That one is for the field. Seravane has already seen the operational briefing. She knows what he can do. He knows what she knows and what she has guessed. The gap between those two things is the space in which they have been friends for four months. He finds this satisfactory. The coffee is ready in the morning.

## Gazes Copied — Status at Arc's End

*Artem's private collection. None disclosed to the Guard except the Gaze of Null. He is not collecting power — he is collecting specimens, the way a naturalist documents species.* His private section of the notebook does not read like a tactical inventory. It reads like field notes: mechanism, observed behavior, variance from described type, test result. The Omnikinesis-Revelation merge is the one he has spent the most time on, because the combined effect — moving objects while simultaneously sensing their resistance and texture as though by touch — is the kind of interaction between systems that a researcher builds an entire career around. He does not show it to anyone. He does not hide it either. He treats it the same way he treated his dungeon rift notes in his original era: private until published, and he is a long way from publication.

|------|------|--------|-------|
| Gaze of Null | Ethereal | Born from the Wall | Known to Guard |
| Gaze of Mimicry | Ethereal | Born from the Wall | Guard filed other eye as anomaly — mechanism unknown |
| Gaze of Convergence | Ethereal | Born from the Wall | Undisclosed |
| Gaze of Omnikinesis | Supreme | Copied from Guard trainer | Private; merged with Revelation via Convergence |
| Gaze of Revelation | Standard | Copied from assessment handler | Private; merged with Omnikinesis via Convergence |
| Gaze of Enchantment | Supreme | Copied from Velin | Private; mechanism documented, full test pending |

*Forty-two pages on the Omnikinesis–Revelation merge alone. Seravane has seen none of them. Neither has Velin, who spent three times as long under methodological observation as any other subject and whose mechanism produced the longest private entry in the notebook — not because it was the most powerful, but because the question of how a Gaze can physically alter what each individual observer sees was, to a researcher who spent millions of years studying a structure that sustained incompatible states simultaneously, the most interesting structural problem in the collection.*

## Arcane Affinity

Highest affinity: **Nihility magic, Nil space magic, Wall of Nihility magic** — the result of millions of years adapting to and within the Wall's core architecture. He does not practice these; the Guard has asked him not to test the Wall-adjacent affinities inside Eventide until the operational parameters are understood.

All other schools: **Maximum measurable affinity across every school tested**, including the six forbidden schools. The evaluator recalibrated the instrument. The result was the same. The Guard's position is that this is consistent with a being whose Sigil has adapted to the full scope of reality over an indefinite period, and that the forbidden school resonance is theoretical until demonstrated. Since he has no intention of practicing Soul Forging or Nihility magic inside a populated floating island, this has not become an incident.

**Formal position offered:** Nihility expert and Wall of Nihility research consultant for the Eternal Guard. He accepted.

## Notes on Key Relationships

**Seravane** — anomaly specialist, the Guard's researcher in residence. Their working dynamic is two people approaching the same subject with incompatible instruments who have stopped noticing the incompatibility. She runs instruments that fail. He provides data she could not extract any other way. She found the archive document that names what he is. They have been drinking coffee in the kitchen after his sessions since week three. Best friends is not a category either of them has articulated, but it describes what they are.

**Velin** — Succubus, Supreme Gaze of Enchantment, Supreme Sigil of Endurance. Her mission is specific and she is patient. Her Enchantment Gaze shows each viewer their ideal eyes; he deactivated it with her permission to see the actual ones. That produced an expression he has filed as *interesting* and intends to revisit. She does not know he has copied her Gaze. She does not know he spent three times as long studying her mechanism as any other. He is amused by her persistence in a way that she finds more difficult to navigate than resistance would be — she has a system for resistance; she does not have one for someone who watches her campaign with obvious entertainment and genuine curiosity.
