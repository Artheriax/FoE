Artem was a researcher before the Wall. He is a researcher still.

In an era roughly 3,000 years before the present, magic had begun bleeding into the world — dungeons splitting the ground open, creatures carrying energy no one had language for yet, survivors returning with inexplicable changes to their bodies. While the institutions of his time declared it divine judgment, Artem took notes. He was a scientist: a cataloguer, a theorist, the kind of mind that responded to the incomprehensible by measuring it until it became familiar.

His work centered on the intersection where biological mutation met arcane energy. Where others saw contamination, he saw a mechanism — two systems that had no business interacting, producing stable, inheritable changes in those exposed. He spent years documenting survivors of monster encounters who developed new properties: enhanced senses, accelerated healing, environmental responsiveness. The traits appeared to embed themselves and pass to offspring. That should not have been possible. Mutation was biological. Magic was external. The barrier between them was considered absolute.

It was not. Artem identified the junction point and, in a laboratory setting, forced the two systems to fuse at a fundamental level. The result was a stable, inheritable birth-trait architecture: the first gazes and sigils. He had created a new category of existence — a permanent bridge between the biological and the arcane that the body could carry from conception.

He did not test his research on himself. His interest was theoretical and documentary. He catalogued the properties, documented the inheritance patterns, and began writing the framework that would, thousands of years later, look remarkably like the MOD's Gaze and Sigil classification system.

What he did not account for was the water.

His laboratory drew from a groundwater source that fed into broader river systems. The residual energy from his experiments — the fused mutation-magic substrate he had stabilized — leached into it. He never noticed. There was nothing to notice. The contamination was inert by every instrument he had. What he could not have known was that the substrate was not inert in living tissue. Over the following decades and centuries, gazes and sigils began appearing in the population along the watershed — spreading outward generation by generation, following the flow of contaminated water into the world's bloodlines. By the time the pattern was statistically visible, Artem was no longer present to confirm it.

The Higher Ones noticed before anyone else.

A mortal had created something that produced inheritable power at scale — a system that bypassed their cosmic authority entirely. This was not an offense they had a category for. Nobody created things of this magnitude except them. The response was not a trial. It was a quarantine. They imprisoned him in the Wall of Nihility — a pre-existing boundary structure at the edge of dimensional reality, a place where existence itself ran out. They did not kill him. Killing him would have left questions. Imprisonment in a structure from which nothing returns was cleaner.

They did not understand what they had done.

### The Wall's Mark

Artem should not have survived the Wall. He was a baseline human — no gazes, no sigils, no adaptation mechanism. The Wall was designed to process anything that entered it into nothing. It should have taken seconds.

It did not.

The precise reason is not understood. The prevailing theory — assembled after the fact by Eternal Guard researchers who have had more time than anyone else to study the data — is that the Wall operates on things that exist within the framework of reality. It erases by targeting what is real. Artem, over the course of his time inside, stopped registering. Whether the Wall's mechanics were incompatible with his particular biology, or whether the extended exposure produced an interaction his body was not supposed to be capable of, is unresolved. What is documented is the result: Artem exists, but reality's rules do not acknowledge him.

Gazes look through him, not at him. The Gaze of Enchantment, which locks onto a person and subsumes their will absolutely, finds no target. The Gaze of Inner Thought, which reads surface-level psychic output, registers silence where a person should be. Instruments that detect dimensional presence, soul resonance, or Source signatures read absence while visual confirmation says presence. He is not invisible. He is not protected. He is simply not there — in the only register that matters to abilities that operate within reality's framework.

He did not know this when he walked out. He discovered it the way he discovers everything: by observing, documenting, and building a theory after enough data accumulated. The first time a Gaze failed against him, he assumed the bearer had misfired. The third time, he started a new section of his notebook.

### Personality

The thing he still reaches for is a notebook.

He carries one — small, plain, current-era materials since his original notebooks are not recoverable — and uses it the way he always did: as the external architecture of a mind that processes by externalizing. His memory is near-perfect. The journal is not for retention. It is for the physical act of description, which is a different cognitive function than remembering, and one his mind appears constitutionally unwilling to operate without.

From his perspective, the Wall took hours. The world outside moved on by 3,000 years. He is the same person who walked into the rift: the naturalist who traveled to monster emergence sites in a world that feared them, interviewed changed survivors when everyone else was burning them, and kept meticulous notes through conditions that drove colleagues home early. He was thorough, direct, patient with confusion, and good enough with people to sustain a decade of productive field collaboration. That is still who he is. What he does not have is familiarity with thousands of years of specific context — the guild structures, the MOD registration requirements, what titles carry weight now and which organizations are sensitive about their authority, which questions are considered rude in this era when they would have been unremarkable before. He is learning. That is what he does with everything unfamiliar.

He gets cold in winter. His body is baseline human. He has no sigil monitoring his temperature. He simply experiences being cold. He does not think about it much.

The one thing that did not survive the Wall unchanged is his relationship to the Higher Ones. He hates them. Not the complicated, unresolved anger of someone still working through their feelings — genuine, settled, clear-eyed hatred. They took his life, his work, his era. They entombed him in a boundary structure and told no one. He knows their natures from his research and the archives he has since accessed, and the more he understands about the architecture of what they did, the more focused the hatred becomes. It is not loud. It is precise.

He is still capable of wonder, and that is the thing people encounter first if they meet him in the right circumstances. He stops in the middle of streets and stares. A market. A crowd. Two strangers disagreeing with more energy than the topic requires. He looks at all of it with the expression of a researcher who has found the most interesting field site of his life — because the world has been shaped by his research for 3,000 years without knowing he existed. Gazes and sigils are everywhere. The system he built in a laboratory before the guilds existed, before MOD, before the eras had names, is the foundation of an entire civilization's relationship with power. He finds that completely compelling.

---

### Goal

Curiosity. He wants to understand what became of his research — how the system he created evolved over 3,000 years, what variants emerged, what the world built on top of his original architecture. He is a scientist looking at the longest-running experiment in history, and the experiment is everything.

There is no cosmic reunion he is seeking. There is no being he needs to find. What drives him forward is the same thing that drove him into the laboratory three millennia ago: the conviction that something complicated can be understood if you watch it long enough and write things down.

### Developed Abilities

*These do not exist when Artem walks out of the Wall. They are developed later — through research conducted during his time in Eventide and afterward, combining his original gaze-and-sigil formula with modern magic types and biological mutations that did not exist 3,000 years ago. He researches for purpose, not power. The application to himself is a decision made after the Mindscape incident, not before it.*

The **Gaze of Null** cancels the powers of other Gazes. Any active Gaze operating on him or in proximity is suppressed while Null is active. It does not affect physical forces or Source-derived abilities; it nullifies Gaze phenomena specifically. It was the first ability he developed and applied to himself — a countermeasure produced after witnessing what an uncontrolled Absolute Gaze could do to a population.

The **Gaze of Mimicry** copies whatever Gaze it targets — both the ability and the appearance. His eye shifts to look identical to the Gaze being mimicked. The copied ability functions at the same level as its source. He needs comprehension, not sustained exposure — once he understands a Gaze's mechanism, Mimicry can hold it.

The **Gaze of Convergence** merges his mimicked Gazes with each other, or a mimicked Gaze with the Gaze of Null, producing more powerful variants of the combined abilities. A Gaze merged through Convergence operates above the tier of either component.

The **Sigil of Transcendent Adaptation** — an Ethereal-tier sigil developed by applying his original Adaptation formula through Nihility-adjacent magic and modern biological mutations. It evolves his physiology permanently in response to sustained threat. Unlike the Standard Adaptation sigil, which responds to conditions within predictable rules, the Ethereal variant has no documented ceiling. He developed it last and treats it with the caution of someone who knows exactly what happens when an adaptive system is given unlimited time and pressure.

That he holds multiple Gazes and an Ethereal Sigil simultaneously is an anomaly that sits outside the documented ceiling for mortal bearing. He is the only confirmed case.
