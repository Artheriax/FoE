# Gaelira

- **Race:** Astrae
- **Age:** Unknown; appears late twenties

Gaelira is tall in the way that reads as height before it reads as anything else — taller than both Sersey and Nyari, which in elven spatial vocabulary carries a specific kind of weight. Silver hair, light blue eyes, the abstract patterns of glowing white marks across her face that are Astrae-native: they follow angles an artisan might approximate but couldn't reproduce, and they track, very faintly, with her internal state. When she is focused, they brighten. When she is waiting, they dim. She has never found this convenient. She has adjusted.

The bearing and proportions of an Astrae raised in a culture that treats physical presentation as a form of language: posture not as performance but as habit, the kind of presence that fills a room without announcing itself. She is the sort of beautiful that stops being described and starts being taken as part of the background.

She was Sersey's other original closest friend. She left for the arranged marriage. What she came back with after her spin-off is not a returned thing but a replaced one — she is no longer the Gaelira managing an exit strategy from a situation she did not choose. She is the one who chose to come here.

### The Senses and the Radiant

Gaelira's Sigil is Senses — a Standard-tier gift that, in Astrae physiology, compounds in ways that make it more than the sum of its enhancement. Her perception was already expanded beyond mortal norms; the Sigil sits on top of that and refuses to let anything small be invisible. Sound, scent, motion at distance, the faint luminance differentials that Astrae vision reaches even in near-darkness — all of it runs hotter and clearer than any unaugmented being can manage.

It was this combination that allowed her to read the scroll's second layer in the vampire house archive. The divine seals were encoded in wavelengths that only Astrae perception reaches, and the Sigil ensured she was perceiving them at full resolution. Other readers found one layer. She found two. She took the scroll before she fully understood what she had done.

Her arcane affinity is Luminancy — holy light used in the applied register rather than the theological. She uses it for what it does specifically: clarity, exposure, healing. She can illuminate a space without destroying what is inside it. She can find things hidden in darkness by introducing light that does not behave the way darkness expects. In a group with Lyra's Void work and Kaelen's spirit sight, radiant covers the register those two do not — things that do not want to be present but are, things that are structurally obscured rather than spiritually distinct.

Her enchantments are practical and precise. She taught Nyari the foundation of the practice; her own applications lean toward weapons and armor — hexline reinforcement, resonance patterns that make a blade hold its edge against forces it should not hold against, armor that distributes incoming damage across a wider frame. She does not enchant objects for atmosphere. She enchants objects so that the people holding them do not die.

### Personality

Gaelira thinks before she speaks. This is not hesitation — it is the habit of someone who learned early that words carry weight and deploying them carelessly is expensive. When she says something, she means it exactly, including the things she says that are not comfortable to hear. She is assertive in the mode of someone who earned the authority she carries through demonstrated competence rather than position, and does not feel it necessary to soften conclusions that are correct.

She knows when to let loose. This surprises most people who work with her because the default register is so composed — and then she makes a choice that is fully, clearly herself, without the structure, and the contrast lands with some weight. She is not always controlled. She chooses when.

She does not extend trust quickly and is not surprised when it is not extended to her quickly. She navigated a spin-off in which two institutions treated her as an asset to be acquired and found, at the end, that what she wanted was a place where that calculus did not apply. She came back to Sersey. That is what coming back means.

### Goal

Be here because she chose to be here, and remain here because it remains her choice. The scroll is still in her keeping. Whatever it is pointing toward is still an open question. She has no institutional backing for whatever answer she finds — and does not need any.
