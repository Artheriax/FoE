# Lyra

- **Race:** Voidling
- **Age:** Unknown

Most Voidlings stay.

The Void is home in the specific and complete sense — it provides, contains, and asks nothing of those within it except that they remain. The threshold between the Void and the outside world exists; most Voidlings treat it as definitional rather than crossable. Lyra was always more interested in what was on the other side.

She is slight in the way of scholars who move through stacks of accumulated knowledge rather than through terrain. Black hair with purple accents that catch differently depending on the quality of light around her. Pointy ears — a Voidling trait acquired somewhere in the long dimensional intersection between the Void and its bordering spaces. Dark purple eyes that in poor light look almost black, and in good light are the specific deep color of something that has absorbed most of what it was given. She tends toward dark clothing without having a reason for it; aesthetic legacy of a dimension with no natural light source.

She was a scholar before she was anything else. The Orchid contract found her at a research desk with seventeen open texts and a genuine answer to a question no one else had been able to answer. It paid well. The terrain was interesting. She went.

### The Void in Practice

Lyra's affinity is Umbral and void-adjacent — not in the watered-down sense of basic shadow work, but in the sense of someone who grew up surrounded by the real thing and learned to read it before she learned to navigate outside it.

In the field this means she does things that explain poorly to people without her background. She can locate dimensional edges by feel. She knows where a door should be before its frame is constructed. Her Umbral work is not showy — she does not conjure spectacular darkness; she uses existing shadow as material, which means she is most effective in the places other people find most difficult. Caves. Night. The particular quality of light near Void-adjacent dimensional boundaries. In Dreamveil she navigated a labyrinth of living memories with less disorientation than anyone else in the group, because living memories are not structurally dissimilar from the ambient echo of the Void's deep spaces. You learn to recognize the difference between a memory and a wall.

She has no Gaze, no Sigil. What she has is context nobody else in the group possesses.

### Personality

Lyra is sarcastic. This is not a defense mechanism; it is her natural register — the tone of someone who processes the world through a dry, sideways lens and finds most things slightly more absurd than they appear to be taking themselves. She delivers useful information in the flat voice of someone delivering mildly disappointing news and has not adjusted this delivery for general reception.

She acts as if she does not care. She does. The acting is halfhearted enough that anyone paying attention can see through it, which is probably intentional — she would rather be seen through than perform investment she considers obvious. She keeps going on jobs she could opt out of. She keeps appearing in rooms she was not assigned to. She has opinions about how the group is doing, never volunteered, always accurate when extracted.

Her curiosity about the outside world, the thing that originally drew her past the threshold, is still operating. It turns out to be larger and stranger than academic study of it suggested. She notes this. She finds it satisfying in the particular way of someone who suspected the world would be stranger than described and has been proven right.

### Goal

Continue. Lyra has not articulated a goal that would survive examination, and she would resist doing so on principle. What she has is forward motion — a research thread that keeps presenting new data, a group that keeps ending up in dimensional spaces where she is the most useful person present, and a version of "outside the Void" that has turned out to be worth staying in.
